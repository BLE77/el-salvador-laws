# State of Exception in El Salvador

El Salvador has maintained a **state of exception (regimen de excepcion)** since **March 27, 2022**, initially declared in response to a surge in gang-related homicides. It is governed by **Articles 29 through 31 of the Constitution** and has been renewed continuously by the Asamblea Legislativa.

## Constitutional Basis

The Constitution allows the suspension of specific constitutional guarantees during war, public calamity, or serious disturbance of public order. The president requests the state of exception, and the **Asamblea Legislativa must approve it** by a qualified majority.

## Rights Suspended

During the state of exception, the following constitutional rights are suspended:

- **Freedom of association and assembly**: Public gatherings may be restricted
- **Privacy of correspondence and communications**: Authorities may intercept communications without prior judicial authorization
- **Right to be informed of reasons for arrest**: Detained persons may not immediately receive formal charges
- **Extended detention without charges**: Police may hold suspects for **up to 15 days** without filing formal charges, compared to the standard 72-hour limit

## Renewal Process

The state of exception must be **renewed every 30 days** by vote of the Asamblea Legislativa. Each renewal requires a separate legislative session and vote. Since its inception, it has been renewed without interruption.

## Practical Impact

Tens of thousands of individuals have been detained under the state of exception. Military and police conduct joint operations with expanded authority. Citizens should carry identification at all times, as security checkpoints are common. Legal counsel should be sought immediately if detained, though access to attorneys may be delayed during the initial detention period.
