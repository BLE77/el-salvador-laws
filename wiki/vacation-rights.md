# Vacation Rights in El Salvador

Paid vacation in El Salvador is a mandatory labor right governed by the **Labor Code (Decreto 15), Articles 177-189**. Employers cannot deny, reduce, or replace vacation with monetary compensation.

## Entitlement

After completing **one year of continuous service** with the same employer, every worker is entitled to **15 consecutive calendar days** of paid vacation. This right accrues annually upon each work anniversary.

## Vacation Pay

Workers receive their regular salary for the vacation period plus a **30% vacation premium** (prima vacacional). For example, a worker earning $400 per month would receive their regular pay plus an additional 30% for the vacation days. This premium must be paid before the vacation begins.

## Scheduling

- The **employer determines the timing** of the vacation period, but must grant it within **four months** following the worker's anniversary date
- Vacation must be taken as **consecutive days**; it cannot be split into shorter periods without the worker's agreement
- The employer must notify the worker of the scheduled dates in advance

## Key Restrictions

- Vacation **cannot be waived** by the worker, even voluntarily
- Vacation **cannot be compensated with money** in lieu of actual time off, except upon termination of employment when unused vacation is paid out
- Workers who are terminated before taking accrued vacation are entitled to proportional vacation pay plus the 30% premium

## Practical Notes

Workers who believe their vacation rights are being denied should file a complaint with the **Ministerio de Trabajo y Prevision Social**. The labor inspectorate can intervene directly with the employer. Persistent violations may result in fines and mandatory compliance orders.
