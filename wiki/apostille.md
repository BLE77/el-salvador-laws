# Getting an Apostille in El Salvador

El Salvador has been a member of the **Hague Apostille Convention** (Convention of 5 October 1961) since **1996**. An apostille is an international certification that authenticates the origin of a public document, allowing it to be recognized in other member countries without further legalization.

## Issuing Authority

Apostilles in El Salvador are issued by the **Ministerio de Relaciones Exteriores** (Ministry of Foreign Affairs). No other institution is authorized to issue apostilles.

## Step-by-Step Process

1. **Obtain the original document**: Get the official document you need apostilled (birth certificate, diploma, court record, etc.)
2. **Authenticate at the source**: The document must first be authenticated by the issuing authority. For example:
   - Civil registry documents: authenticated by the **Registro Civil** or municipality that issued them
   - University degrees: authenticated by the **Ministerio de Educacion**
   - Notarial documents: the notary's signature must be verified by the **Corte Suprema de Justicia**
3. **Submit to the Foreign Ministry**: Bring the authenticated document to the Ministerio de Relaciones Exteriores
4. **Pay the fee**: A small administrative fee applies
5. **Receive the apostille**: Processing typically takes **1 to 3 business days**

## Important Notes

- The apostille only certifies the **origin and authenticity** of the document, not the accuracy of its content
- Documents in Spanish may still require **certified translation** in the destination country
- If the destination country is **not a Hague Convention member**, you will need full diplomatic legalization (consular legalization) instead of an apostille
- The Foreign Ministry office in **San Salvador** handles most apostille requests
