# Free Trade Zones in El Salvador

Free trade zones (zonas francas) in El Salvador are governed by the **Ley de Zonas Francas Industriales y de Comercializacion (Decreto 405 of 1998)**. These zones offer significant tax incentives to attract foreign investment and promote exports.

## Tax Incentives

Businesses operating within authorized free trade zones enjoy:

- **Income tax exemption**: Complete exemption from corporate income tax for **15 to 20 years**, depending on the zone location and type of activity
- **Import duty exemption**: No customs duties on raw materials, machinery, equipment, and supplies imported for production
- **IVA exemption**: Exempt from the 13% value added tax on imports and local purchases of inputs
- **Municipal tax exemption**: Exempt from municipal taxes for the incentive period
- **Free repatriation of profits**: No restrictions on transferring earnings abroad

## Requirements

To benefit from free trade zone status, businesses must:

- Operate within a **designated and authorized zona franca** or obtain individual status as a Deposito para Perfeccionamiento Activo (DPA)
- **Export the majority of production** (typically 100% for zona franca, with limited local sales permitted under certain conditions)
- Comply with Salvadoran labor and environmental laws
- Maintain proper accounting and submit reports to the Ministry of Economy

## Sectors

The most common industries in Salvadoran free trade zones include textile and garment manufacturing (maquila), electronics assembly, food processing, and call center operations.

## Administration

The **Ministry of Economy (Ministerio de Economia)** oversees the authorization and supervision of free trade zones. Applications for zona franca benefits are filed with the Direccion de Comercio e Inversion. Periodic audits ensure compliance with export and operational requirements.
