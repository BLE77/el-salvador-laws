# Small Claims and Minor Disputes

For minor legal disputes in El Salvador, simplified procedures are available through lower courts and alternative dispute resolution mechanisms. The **Civil and Commercial Procedure Code (Codigo Procesal Civil y Mercantil), Decreto 712**, and the **Organic Law of the Judiciary** govern these processes.

## Juzgados de Paz (Justice of the Peace Courts)

- **Juzgados de Paz** are the lowest-level courts and handle **minor civil and criminal matters**.
- For civil disputes, they hear cases involving small monetary amounts as set by law.
- Criminal jurisdiction covers minor offenses (faltas) and preliminary hearings for more serious crimes.
- These courts are located throughout the country, including in smaller municipalities, making them the most accessible part of the justice system.

## Simplified Procedures

- Small claims follow a **streamlined process** with fewer formalities than regular civil proceedings.
- Hearings are typically oral rather than written, reducing time and cost.
- **No lawyer is required** for small claims, allowing individuals to represent themselves.
- Judgments in small claims are generally issued more quickly than in ordinary courts.

## Mediation and Conciliation

- The **Procuraduria General de la Republica (PGR)** offers free **mediation services** for civil and family disputes.
- Mediation is voluntary and aims to reach a mutually acceptable agreement without going to trial.
- Mediated agreements, once approved by a court, have the same legal force as a judicial ruling.
- Many courts also offer **conciliation hearings** early in proceedings to encourage settlement.

## Practical Tips

- Gather all relevant documents (contracts, receipts, correspondence) before filing.
- File claims at the Juzgado de Paz in the jurisdiction where the defendant resides or where the dispute arose.
