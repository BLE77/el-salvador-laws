# Landlord-Tenant Law in El Salvador

Rental agreements (contratos de arrendamiento) in El Salvador are primarily governed by the **Civil Code** (Codigo Civil), Book IV, Title XXVI, which covers the contract of arrendamiento (lease). Municipal regulations under **Decreto 274** (Municipal Code) may also apply to housing standards.

## Lease Requirements

- Leases can be verbal or written, but a **written contract is strongly recommended** for enforceability. For leases longer than one year, a written instrument is generally required.
- The lease should specify: the property, rent amount, payment schedule, duration, and obligations of each party.
- There is no statutory rent control at the national level, though municipalities may enact local regulations.

## Tenant Rights

- Tenants have the right to **peaceful enjoyment** (goce pacifico) of the rented property for the agreed term.
- The landlord must maintain the property in a **habitable condition** and make necessary repairs, unless the contract assigns minor maintenance to the tenant.
- Security deposits (depositos de garantia) are customary but not extensively regulated by statute. Typically one to two months' rent is standard practice, refundable at lease end minus legitimate deductions.

## Eviction and Termination

- A landlord must provide **written notice** to terminate a lease. For indefinite-term leases, notice periods are typically tied to the rental payment period (e.g., 30 days for monthly rent).
- Grounds for eviction include: **non-payment of rent**, use of the property for unauthorized purposes, significant damage to the property, or subletting without permission.
- Evictions must go through **judicial process** (juicio de desahucio). Self-help evictions (changing locks, cutting utilities) are not legally permitted.
- For fixed-term leases, the contract ends at the agreed date without need for notice, unless the tenant remains and the landlord does not object, which may create a tacit renewal.

## Dispute Resolution

Landlord-tenant disputes are resolved in **civil courts** (juzgados de lo civil). The Civil Procedure Code (Decreto 712, Codigo Procesal Civil y Mercantil) governs the procedural aspects. Mediation is encouraged before formal litigation.

## Practical Notes

Rental markets in El Salvador operate with significant informality, particularly outside San Salvador. Many agreements are verbal, which can complicate enforcement. Tenants and landlords alike benefit from formalizing agreements in writing and registering them where possible.
