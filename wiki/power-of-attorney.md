# Power of Attorney (Poder)

A power of attorney (poder) in El Salvador is a legal instrument that authorizes one person (the agent or apoderado) to act on behalf of another (the principal or poderdante). It must be executed before a **notary public (notario)** to be legally valid.

## Types of Power of Attorney

**General Power of Attorney (Poder General):**
- Grants broad authority to the agent to act on the principal's behalf in a wide range of legal, business, and administrative matters.
- Commonly used for ongoing business management or when someone will be absent from the country for an extended period.
- Does not cover acts that the law requires to be performed personally.

**Special Power of Attorney (Poder Especial):**
- Authorizes the agent to perform **one or more specific acts**, such as selling a particular property, signing a specific contract, or representing the principal in a particular lawsuit.
- Preferred when the principal wants to limit the agent's authority to a defined scope.

## Key Rules

- A power of attorney can be **revoked at any time** by the principal through a notarized document.
- The death of the principal automatically terminates the power of attorney.
- Acts performed by the agent within the scope of the power are legally binding on the principal.
- The agent must act in the **principal's best interest** and cannot exceed the authority granted.

## Foreign Powers of Attorney

- Powers of attorney executed **outside El Salvador** must be **apostilled** (or legalized through consular channels if the issuing country is not a Hague Apostille Convention member).
- The document must be **translated into Spanish** by an authorized translator if executed in another language.
- Once apostilled and translated, the document must be registered or presented before the appropriate Salvadoran authority.
