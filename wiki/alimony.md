# Alimony and Spousal Support in El Salvador

Spousal support, referred to as **pension alimenticia** for the spouse, is governed by the **Family Code (Codigo de Familia, Decreto 677 of 1993)**. It is distinct from child support (pension alimenticia for children), though both fall under the broader category of alimentos.

## When Alimony is Awarded

Alimony may be ordered during or after divorce proceedings. It is not automatic — a spouse must **petition the family court** and demonstrate need. The court evaluates:

- **Need of the requesting spouse** — whether they lack sufficient income or means to support themselves
- **Ability to pay** — the financial capacity of the other spouse
- **Duration of the marriage** — longer marriages are more likely to result in alimony awards
- **Standard of living** — the lifestyle the couple maintained during the marriage
- **Age and health** — the physical condition and employability of the requesting spouse
- **Contributions to the marriage** — including non-economic contributions such as homemaking and child-rearing

## Duration

Alimony can be:

- **Temporary (temporal)** — granted for a defined period to allow the receiving spouse to become financially self-sufficient, such as through education or job training
- **Permanent (permanente)** — awarded in cases where the spouse cannot reasonably become self-supporting, typically due to age, health, or long-term dependency

## Failure to Pay

Non-payment of court-ordered alimony is a **criminal offense** in El Salvador. The obligated spouse can face arrest and prosecution. Courts may also order wage garnishment or seizure of assets to enforce payment.

## Modification and Termination

Alimony orders can be modified if either party's circumstances change significantly. Alimony typically ends upon the recipient's remarriage, cohabitation with a new partner, or death of either party.
