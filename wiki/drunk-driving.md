# Drunk Driving / DUI Laws in El Salvador

Driving under the influence of alcohol or drugs in El Salvador is governed by the **Ley de Transporte Terrestre, Transito y Seguridad Vial (Decreto 420)**. Drunk driving is treated as both an administrative and criminal offense depending on severity.

## Blood Alcohol Limit

The legal blood alcohol concentration (BAC) limit is **0.05 g/dL** (0.05%). For commercial and public transport drivers, the limit is **zero tolerance**. Exceeding these thresholds triggers immediate penalties.

## Penalties

- **Administrative fines**: Monetary fines are imposed for BAC above the legal limit, even without an accident
- **License suspension**: Driving privileges are suspended for a period determined by the severity of the offense, typically 1 to 3 years
- **Vehicle impoundment**: The vehicle may be impounded at the scene by traffic police (DPMT)
- **Criminal charges**: If a DUI results in injuries or death, the driver faces criminal prosecution under the Penal Code (Decreto 1030), including charges of homicidio culposo (negligent homicide) carrying 2 to 4 years imprisonment, or lesiones culposas for injuries

## Breathalyzer and Sobriety Tests

Police officers are authorized to conduct **breathalyzer tests (prueba de alcoholemia)** and field sobriety tests at checkpoints or after traffic stops. Refusing to submit to a breathalyzer test carries legal consequences equivalent to a positive result, including automatic license suspension and fines.

## Practical Notes

If stopped at a DUI checkpoint, cooperate with officers and provide identification. If involved in an alcohol-related accident, contact the **PNC (Policia Nacional Civil)** at 911 immediately. Legal counsel is advisable before making statements if criminal charges are likely.
