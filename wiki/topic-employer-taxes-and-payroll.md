# Employer Taxes and Payroll Obligations in El Salvador

## Summary

Every employer in El Salvador is subject to a set of mandatory payroll contributions and employee benefits established by multiple laws. These obligations include social security (ISSS), pension fund (AFP), vocational training (INCAF, formerly INSAFORP), income tax withholding, aguinaldo (Christmas bonus), paid vacation, severance pay, and -- starting in 2027 -- the new "Quincena Veinticinco" benefit. Failure to comply with any of these obligations exposes the employer to administrative sanctions, interest charges, and judicial collection proceedings.

The total direct employer-side payroll tax burden is approximately **17.25%** of gross salary (ISSS 7.5% + AFP 8.75% + INCAF 1%), plus mandatory benefits that effectively add another 8-12% depending on employee tenure.

## Mandatory Employer Contributions

### 1. ISSS -- Social Security (7.5% employer / 3% employee)

The Instituto Salvadoreno del Seguro Social (ISSS) provides health care and short-term disability coverage to workers. The legal framework originates from the Ley del Seguro Social (D-1263/1953) and implementing regulations.

| Detail | Value |
|---|---|
| **Employer rate** | 7.5% of salary |
| **Employee rate** | 3.0% of salary |
| **Salary ceiling** | $1,000.00 per month |
| **Maximum employer contribution** | $75.00 per month per employee |
| **Maximum employee contribution** | $30.00 per month per employee |

**Key rules:**
- Contributions are calculated on the employee's gross monthly salary, up to the salary ceiling of $1,000/month. Earnings above this ceiling are not subject to ISSS contributions.
- The employer withholds the employee's 3% share from wages and remits both portions together to the ISSS.
- Contributions cover medical care, maternity benefits, and short-term disability subsidies.
- Reglamentos issued by the ISSS determine the specific rates and collection procedures (Art. 29-31 of D-1263/1953). The rates are set by regulation, not in the statute itself.
- The ISSS also serves as the collection agent for INCAF contributions (Art. 20, D-893/2023).

**Legal basis:** Ley del Seguro Social, Decreto Legislativo 1263 (1953), with multiple amendments. Contribution rates are set by ISSS reglamentos.

---

### 2. AFP -- Pension Fund (8.75% employer / 7.25% employee)

The pension system is a mandatory individual-account savings system administered by private Administradoras de Fondos de Pensiones (AFPs). The system was created by the Ley del Sistema de Ahorro para Pensiones (D-927/1996) and substantially reformed by the Ley Integral del Sistema de Pensiones (D-614/2022, complemented by D-615/2022 and D-787/2017).

| Detail | Value |
|---|---|
| **Employer rate** | 8.75% of salary |
| **Employee rate** | 7.25% of salary |
| **Total cotizacion** | 16.00% of salary |
| **Salary ceiling** | None (applies to full salary) |

**Key rules:**
- Affiliation to the pension system is mandatory for all workers in a subordinate employment relationship (Art. 7, D-614/2022).
- The employer must respect the worker's choice of AFP. If the worker does not choose within 20 days, the employer must affiliate them (Art. 9, D-614/2022).
- The employer deducts the employee's share from wages at the time of payment and remits both shares to the AFP (Art. 21, D-614/2022).
- Declaration and payment must be made monthly within the time periods established by regulation.
- Failure to declare and pay results in administrative collection proceedings by the AFP, which can escalate to judicial collection with executive force (Arts. 22-24, D-614/2022).
- The 2022 reform (D-615/2022) dissolved the INPEP (public pension institute) and consolidated all pension oversight under the Instituto Salvadoreno de Pensiones.
- The Cuenta de Garantia Solidaria, funded from a portion of employer contributions, provides a solidarity component to supplement low pensions.

**Legal basis:** Ley del Sistema de Ahorro para Pensiones (originally D-927/1996), reformed by D-787/2017, and replaced by D-614/2022 (Ley Integral del Sistema de Pensiones). Institutional framework: D-615/2022 (Instituto Salvadoreno de Pensiones).

### 3. INCAF -- Vocational Training Fund (1% employer only)

Employers with 10 or more workers must contribute 1% of their total monthly payroll to the national vocational training system. This fund was originally managed by INSAFORP under D-554/1993, but was replaced by the Instituto Nacional de Capacitacion y Formacion (INCAF) under D-893/2023.

| Detail | Value |
|---|---|
| **Employer rate** | 1% of total payroll |
| **Employee rate** | 0% (no employee contribution) |
| **Applies to** | Employers with 10+ employees |
| **Agricultural sector** | Up to 0.25% (1/4 of 1%), only on permanent workers |

**Key rules:**
- The contribution is 1% of the total monthly payroll of salaries and wages (Art. 19, numeral 3, D-893/2023).
- Agricultural employers pay a reduced rate of up to 0.25% and do not contribute on temporary worker payrolls.
- The ISSS collects INCAF contributions using its existing collection infrastructure (Art. 20, D-893/2023).
- Of the total 1% collected, 20% goes to INCAF for training programs; the remaining 80% is distributed: 40% to social integration programs and 40% to digital skills and technology training (Art. 19, D-893/2023).
- The employer cannot pass this cost to the worker in any form: "Para cubrir esta cotizacion patronal, no se afectara en ningun caso el salario de los trabajadores."

**Legal basis:** Originally Ley de Formacion Profesional (D-554/1993); replaced by D-893/2023 (Ley de Creacion del INCAF). INSAFORP's budget and functions were transferred to INCAF.

### 4. Income Tax Withholding (employer withholds from employee)

The employer does not pay income tax on behalf of the employee -- rather, the employer acts as a withholding agent (*agente de retencion*), deducting income tax from each employee's salary according to statutory tables and remitting it to the Direccion General de Impuestos Internos.

**Monthly withholding table for domiciled natural persons** (derived from Art. 37, D-134/1991, applied monthly):

| Monthly Taxable Income | Rate | On Excess Over | Plus Fixed Amount |
|---|---|---|---|
| $0.01 -- $472.00 | Exempt | -- | -- |
| $472.01 -- $895.24 | 10% | $472.00 | $17.67 |
| $895.25 -- $2,038.10 | 20% | $895.24 | $60.00 |
| $2,038.11 and above | 30% | $2,038.10 | $288.57 |

*Note: The annual table in Art. 37 uses brackets of $0-$4,064 (exempt), $4,064-$9,142.86 (10%), $9,142.87-$22,857.14 (20%), and $22,857.15+ (30%). The monthly table above divides these by 12. Some sources use the updated brackets from subsequent reforms with a higher exempt threshold of $6,600 annually ($550/month).*

**Key rules:**
- The employer deducts the withholding from each salary payment and remits it within the first 10 business days of the following month (Art. 152, D-230/2000, Codigo Tributario).
- Employers must also make monthly advance payments (*pago a cuenta*) of 1.75% of their own gross income (Art. 72, D-134/1991).
- Corporate income tax rates: 30% for companies with revenue over $150,000; 25% for companies with revenue of $150,000 or less (Art. 41, D-134/1991).
- Non-resident payees: the employer must withhold income tax on any payment to non-domiciled persons or entities, at rates specified in the law (Art. 158, D-230/2000).

**Legal basis:** Ley de Impuesto sobre la Renta (D-134/1991, with multiple reforms). Procedural rules in Codigo Tributario (D-230/2000).

## Mandatory Employee Benefits

### Aguinaldo (Christmas Bonus)

Every employer must pay an annual bonus (*prima de aguinaldo*) to all workers. The amount depends on the employee's length of service. The legal text is in Arts. 196-202 of the Codigo de Trabajo (D-15/1972), most recently reformed by D-433/2025.

| Years of Service | Minimum Aguinaldo |
|---|---|
| 1 year but less than 3 years | 15 days of salary |
| 3 years but less than 10 years | 19 days of salary |
| 10 years or more | 21 days of salary |

**Key rules:**
- Workers with less than 1 year of service receive a proportional amount based on time worked (Art. 197).
- The aguinaldo must be paid between October 20 and December 20 of each year. The employer chooses the specific date within this window (Art. 200, as reformed by D-433/2025).
- No worker may lose the right to aguinaldo for disciplinary reasons, unjustified absences, or any other cause (Art. 201).
- If the contract is terminated before October 20, the worker is entitled to a proportional aguinaldo (Art. 202).
- The aguinaldo is calculated on the basic salary at the date of payment (for time-based salaries) or the average of the preceding 6 months (for other pay structures) (Art. 199).
- Aguinaldo is exempt from income tax.

**Legal basis:** Codigo de Trabajo (D-15/1972), Arts. 196-202, reformed by D-433/2025.

### Vacaciones (Paid Vacation)

After one year of continuous service, every worker is entitled to 15 calendar days of paid vacation, with a premium of 30% above regular salary.

| Detail | Value |
|---|---|
| **Vacation days** | 15 calendar days per year |
| **Pay premium** | Regular salary + 30% |
| **Minimum days worked to qualify** | 200 days in the year |
| **Employer deadline to grant** | Within 4 months (if <100 employees) or 6 months (if 100+ employees) after the anniversary |

**Key rules:**
- The employer must notify the worker at least 30 days in advance of the vacation start date (Art. 182).
- Vacation pay must be paid before the vacation begins (Art. 185).
- Vacations cannot be compensated in money or accumulated; the worker is obligated to take them (Art. 188).
- If the employer provides housing, meals, or both, and these are interrupted during vacation, the vacation pay must be increased by 25% for each benefit interrupted (Art. 184).
- Proportional vacation pay is owed when a contract terminates with employer responsibility, or on unjust dismissal, even if the full year has not been completed. If the year of service was completed, vacation pay is owed regardless of the cause of termination (Art. 187).
- Days of rest and holidays (*asuetos*) that fall within the vacation period do not extend it, but vacations may not begin on such days (Art. 178).

**Legal basis:** Codigo de Trabajo (D-15/1972), Arts. 177-189.

### Indemnizacion (Severance Pay)

When an employee with an indefinite-term contract is dismissed without just cause, the employer must pay severance equal to 30 days of basic salary per year of service (pro-rated for partial years).

| Detail | Value |
|---|---|
| **Amount** | 30 days of basic salary per year of service |
| **Minimum** | 15 days of basic salary |
| **Salary cap for calculation** | 4x the daily legal minimum wage |
| **Applies to** | Indefinite-term contracts, dismissal without just cause |

**Key rules:**
- Dismissal without just cause is legally presumed; the burden of proof falls on the employer (Art. 55).
- For fixed-term contracts, severance equals the salary for the remaining term, but cannot exceed the amount that would apply to an indefinite-term contract (Art. 59).
- The salary base for the calculation is capped at four times the legal daily minimum wage (Art. 58).
- The employer is not required to provision or pay severance during active employment; it is triggered only by unjust dismissal.

**Legal basis:** Codigo de Trabajo (D-15/1972), Arts. 55-60.

---

### Quincena Veinticinco (New -- Starting 2027)

Decreto 499 (2026) created a new mandatory benefit called "Quincena Veinticinco" -- an additional income payment that must be made between January 15 and January 25 of each year, starting in 2027. It applies to both public and private sector workers.

**Key rules:**
- The benefit must be paid in full, without any deductions.
- The rules regarding proportional payment and payment upon termination follow the same principles as the aguinaldo.
- For the 2026 fiscal year, only public employees and municipal workers receive the benefit (private sector starts 2027).
- The specific amount is to be determined following the same methodology as the aguinaldo or equivalent compensation.

**Legal basis:** Ley Especial Quincena Veinticinco (D-499/2026).

## Total Employer Cost Summary

The following example shows the total monthly cost for an employer paying a gross salary of **$500.00/month** to an employee with 2 years of service:

### Monthly Payroll Contributions

| Concept | Employer Pays | Employee Pays (withheld) | Legal Basis |
|---|---|---|---|
| **ISSS (Social Security)** | $37.50 (7.5%) | $15.00 (3%) | D-1263/1953 |
| **AFP (Pension)** | $43.75 (8.75%) | $36.25 (7.25%) | D-614/2022 |
| **INCAF (Training)** | $5.00 (1%) | $0.00 | D-893/2023 |
| **Income Tax Withholding** | $0.00 | ~$2.80* | D-134/1991 |
| **Subtotal monthly** | **$86.25** | **$54.05** | |

*Income tax withholding estimated for a $500 monthly salary after deducting ISSS and AFP employee contributions: taxable = $500 - $15 - $36.25 = $448.75, which falls in the exempt bracket under the updated table ($550/month exempt threshold). Under the original table ($472/month exempt), the tax would be approximately ($448.75 - $472) = exempt or minimal. Actual withholding depends on the applicable table version and personal deductions claimed.*

### Annualized Benefit Costs (Employer Only)

| Concept | Annual Cost | Monthly Equivalent | Calculation |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Aguinaldo** (15 days, 1-3 yrs) | $250.00 | $20.83 | 15 days x ($500/30) |
| **Vacation premium** (30% on 15 days) | $75.00 | $6.25 | 15 days x ($500/30) x 30% |
| **ISSS on aguinaldo** | $18.75 | $1.56 | 7.5% x $250 |
| **AFP on aguinaldo** | $21.88 | $1.82 | 8.75% x $250 |
| **Subtotal annual benefits** | **$365.63** | **$30.46** | |

### Total Employer Cost Per Employee

| Component | Monthly Amount |
|---|---|
| Gross salary | $500.00 |
| ISSS employer (7.5%) | $37.50 |
| AFP employer (8.75%) | $43.75 |
| INCAF (1%) | $5.00 |
| Aguinaldo provision (monthly) | $20.83 |
| Vacation premium provision (monthly) | $6.25 |
| Contributions on aguinaldo (monthly) | $3.38 |
| **Total monthly employer cost** | **$616.71** |
| **Effective cost multiplier** | **1.233x gross salary** |

The employee takes home approximately **$445.95** after ISSS ($15.00), AFP ($36.25), and income tax withholding (~$2.80) deductions.

**Important:** This example does not include the contingent cost of severance (indemnizacion), which would add $500 per year of service in the event of unjust dismissal. Some employers provision for this monthly as a reserve. Starting in 2027, the Quincena Veinticinco will add an additional annual cost.

## Payment Deadlines Summary

| Obligation | Deadline |
|---|---|
| ISSS & INCAF contributions | Monthly, per ISSS calendar (typically within 10 business days after month end) |
| AFP contributions | Monthly, per AFP regulatory calendar |
| Income tax withholding | Within 10 business days of the following month (Art. 152, D-230/2000) |
| Aguinaldo | Between October 20 and December 20 (Art. 200, D-15/1972) |
| Vacation | Within 4-6 months after each service anniversary (Art. 182, D-15/1972) |
| Annual income tax return | By April 30 (Art. 74, D-134/1991) |
| Quincena Veinticinco | Between January 15 and January 25 (Art. 2, D-499/2026) |

## Common Questions

### 1. What is the total payroll tax percentage an employer pays in El Salvador?
The total mandatory employer-side contributions are approximately **17.25%** of gross salary: ISSS (7.5%) + AFP (8.75%) + INCAF (1%). This does not include the cost of mandatory benefits like aguinaldo and vacation.

### 2. Is there a salary ceiling for pension (AFP) contributions?
No. Unlike ISSS, which caps contributions at a salary of $1,000/month, AFP contributions are calculated on the employee's full salary with no ceiling.

### 3. Do small businesses (fewer than 10 employees) pay the INCAF/INSAFORP contribution?
No. The 1% INCAF contribution applies only to employers with 10 or more employees. Agricultural employers pay a reduced rate of 0.25% and only on permanent (not temporary) workers.

### 4. Can an employer choose not to pay aguinaldo to a poorly performing employee?
No. Article 201 of the Labor Code explicitly states that no worker may lose the right to aguinaldo for disciplinary reasons, unjustified absences, or any other cause. The only variable is the amount (based on tenure: 15, 19, or 21 days).

### 5. Is the aguinaldo subject to income tax?
No. The aguinaldo is exempt from income tax under Salvadoran law. However, ISSS and AFP contributions are generally due on the aguinaldo amount.

### 6. What happens if an employer fails to pay AFP contributions on time?
The AFP is legally obligated to initiate administrative collection proceedings against the employer (Art. 23, D-614/2022). If administrative collection fails, the AFP issues an enforcement document with executive force (*fuerza ejecutiva*) that does not require signature verification, and pursues judicial collection before civil and commercial courts (Art. 24, D-614/2022).

### 7. How is severance (indemnizacion) calculated if the employee earns above the minimum wage?
The salary base for severance is capped at four times the daily legal minimum wage (Art. 58, D-15/1972). So even if an employee earns a high salary, the severance calculation uses this capped amount. The result is 30 days of capped salary per year of service, with a minimum of 15 days.

### 8. What is the "Quincena Veinticinco" and when does it start for private employers?
The Quincena Veinticinco is a new mandatory benefit created by D-499/2026. It is an additional income payment made between January 15-25 each year. For 2026, only public-sector workers receive it. Private-sector employers must begin paying it starting January 2027. The amount follows the same methodology as the aguinaldo.

### 9. Can vacation be paid in money instead of time off?
No. Article 188 of the Labor Code prohibits compensating vacation in money or in kind. Both the employer and the employee are obligated: the employer must grant the vacation, and the worker must take it. Vacation cannot be accumulated or fractioned (except by mutual agreement into periods of at least 7-10 days).

### 10. What monthly withholding tables should an employer use for income tax?
The employer should apply the withholding tables derived from Art. 37 of the Income Tax Law (D-134/1991), as reformed. The current annual brackets are: Tramo I ($0-$4,064, exempt), Tramo II ($4,064.01-$9,142.86, 10%), Tramo III ($9,142.87-$22,857.14, 20%), Tramo IV ($22,857.15+, 30%). Some reforms have adjusted the exempt threshold. The Direccion General de Impuestos Internos publishes updated monthly withholding tables that employers should consult.
