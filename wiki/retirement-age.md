# Retirement Age and Pensions in El Salvador

## Retirement Age

The legal retirement age in El Salvador is:
- **60 years old for men**
- **55 years old for women**

These ages apply to the mandatory pension system (AFP — Administradoras de Fondos de Pensiones) established by Decreto 927 (1998), the Pension Savings System Law (Ley del Sistema de Ahorro para Pensiones).

## How the Pension System Works

El Salvador uses a **private pension fund system (AFP)**. All formal workers are required to contribute:

- **Employee contribution**: 7.25% of salary
- **Employer contribution**: 7.75% of salary
- **Total**: 15% of salary goes to your individual pension account

The two main AFPs are **AFP Crecer** and **AFP Confía**.

## Types of Pensions

- **Old-age pension (pensión por vejez)**: Available at retirement age with minimum contribution years
- **Disability pension (pensión por invalidez)**: For workers who become permanently disabled
- **Survivors pension (pensión por sobrevivencia)**: For dependents of a deceased worker

## Early Retirement

Workers may retire before the standard age if they have accumulated sufficient funds in their AFP account to finance a minimum pension.

## Minimum Pension

The government guarantees a minimum pension for workers who reach retirement age but don't have enough in their AFP account, provided they meet minimum contribution requirements.

## ISSS (Health Insurance in Retirement)

Retirees continue to receive healthcare through **ISSS** (Instituto Salvadoreño del Seguro Social). The AFP pension system covers retirement income, while ISSS covers medical care.

For specific pension calculations or to check your AFP balance, contact your AFP administrator directly or visit their offices.
