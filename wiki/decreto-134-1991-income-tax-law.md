# Decreto 134 (1991) -- Income Tax Law

## Summary
The Income Tax Law of El Salvador imposes a tax on all income (*renta*) obtained, received, or accrued by individuals and legal entities, whether in cash or in kind, from any source including employment, business activities, investments, and capital gains. The law replaced the 1963 income tax law to modernize the tax base and adapt to the country's economic conditions. It establishes tax rates, deductions, exemptions, filing requirements, and withholding obligations.

## Key Provisions
- **Article 1**: Income tax (*impuesto sobre la renta*) is imposed on all income obtained within the territory of El Salvador.
- **Article 2**: Taxable income (*renta obtenida*) includes all products or profits, in cash or in kind, from any source: employment (salaries, wages, fees, commissions), business activities, capital gains, interest, dividends, rents, and any other compensation.
- **Article 4**: The following are NOT considered taxable income: per diem allowances for travel, work tools, uniforms, and office equipment provided by the employer when necessary for producing the employer's income.
- **Article 6**: Entities excluded from income tax include the State, municipalities, autonomous agencies, religious institutions (for non-commercial activities), and non-profit entities (under specific conditions).
- **Article 14**: The tax period (*ejercicio de imposicion*) is the calendar year, January 1 to December 31.
- **Article 29**: Net income (*renta neta*) is determined by deducting from gross income the costs and expenses necessary for producing income and maintaining its source, as permitted by the law.
- **Article 37**: Personal income tax is calculated on a progressive scale. Key brackets (for natural persons domiciled in El Salvador):
  - Income up to $4,064 annually: exempt
  - $4,064.01 to $9,142.86: 10% on the excess over $4,064 plus a fixed amount
  - $9,142.87 to $22,857.14: 20% on the excess plus a fixed amount
  - Over $22,857.14: 30% on the excess plus a fixed amount
- **Article 41**: Corporate income tax (*personas juridicas*): the rate is 30% on taxable income. Companies with gross income of $150,000 or less pay 25%.
- **Article 42**: Capital gains (*ganancia de capital*) are taxed at 10% on the gain.
- **Article 54-56**: Withholding at source (*retencion*) is required on salaries, professional fees, and other payments. Employers must withhold income tax from employee salaries according to withholding tables.
- **Article 72**: Advance tax payments (*pago a cuenta*): taxpayers must make monthly advance payments of 1.75% of gross monthly income.
- **Article 74**: Individuals and legal entities must file an annual income tax return (*declaracion*) within the first four months following the end of the tax year (by April 30).
- **Article 91**: The legal reserve (*reserva legal*) of corporations, constituted on net profits of each fiscal year up to the minimum required by law, is deductible.

## Who This Affects
All individuals and legal entities (corporations, partnerships, trusts) that obtain income in El Salvador, whether domiciled in the country or not. This includes salaried employees, self-employed professionals, businesses, investors, and foreign entities with Salvadoran-source income.

## Related Laws

- Decreto 15 (1972) -- Labor Code: employer obligations for salary withholding
- Codigo Tributario -- General tax code that provides procedural rules for audits, appeals, and enforcement

## Common Questions
- **Q: What is the income tax rate for individuals?** -- A: Progressive rates from 0% to 30%. Income up to about $4,064/year is exempt. The top rate of 30% applies to income over approximately $22,857 (Art. 37).
- **Q: What is the corporate tax rate?** -- A: 30% for companies with income over $150,000; 25% for companies with income of $150,000 or less (Art. 41).
- **Q: When are tax returns due?** -- A: By April 30 of the year following the tax period (Art. 74).
- **Q: Are capital gains taxed?** -- A: Yes, at a rate of 10% on the gain (Art. 42).
- **Q: What are advance tax payments (pago a cuenta)?** -- A: Monthly advance payments of 1.75% of gross monthly income, credited against the annual tax liability (Art. 72).
- **Q: Are employer-provided travel allowances taxable?** -- A: No, reasonable per diem amounts for transportation, meals, and lodging provided by the employer for business purposes are not considered taxable income (Art. 4).
