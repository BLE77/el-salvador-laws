# Decreto 562 (1964) — Code of Military Justice (Codigo de Justicia Militar)

## Summary

This code exclusively governs military criminal offenses and punishments for members of the Armed Forces of El Salvador on active duty. It covers crimes against the international personality of the state (treason, espionage), crimes against internal security (rebellion, sedition), crimes against military discipline and hierarchy, and establishes the military justice system's procedures and penalties.

## Key Provisions

- **Article 1**: This code applies **exclusively** to members of the Armed Forces on active duty (Fuerza Armada en servicio activo) for purely military crimes and infractions (delitos y faltas puramente militares).
- **Article 2**: The general provisions of Book I of the common Penal Code apply supplementarily to military criminal offenses, insofar as their nature permits and they do not conflict with this code.
- **Article 3**: A state of necessity defense applies to military personnel who, during war or military missions, and deprived of subsistence for themselves or their troops, take others' property to meet strict necessities.
- **Article 9**: The death penalty may only be imposed for crimes specified in the Constitution. It is executed by firing squad (fusilacion).
- **Article 11**: The penalty of imprisonment (reclusion) ranges from six months to twenty-five years.
- **Article 20**: For attempted crimes, the judge considers proximity to completion and the subjective dangerousness of the perpetrator, imposing from the legal minimum to the midpoint of the penalty for the completed crime.
- **Article 27**: The statute of limitations for military crimes is governed by the common Penal Code, except for treason and espionage: the criminal action expires at 20 years, and the imposed sentence at 30 years.
- **Article 28 - Definitions**: "Armed Forces" (Fuerza Armada) includes the Army, Air Force, National Navy, National Guard, National Police, and Treasury Police.
- **Article 54**: Military personnel who commit treason in time of international war face the death penalty. Treason in peacetime that endangers national independence or integrity, or causes grave damage to military forces, also carries the death penalty (though this provision has been declared unconstitutional in its peacetime application).
- **Article 55 - Acts of Treason**: Include taking arms against El Salvador under enemy flag, facilitating enemy entry into territory, surrendering positions, coercing commanders to surrender, impeding troop reinforcements, recruiting for enemy forces, serving as enemy guides, and obstructing military communications.
- **Article 64**: Espionage (espionaje) is committed by foreign nationals who clandestinely or under false pretenses seek military information to communicate to a foreign power.
- **Article 76 - Rebellion** (rebelion): Military personnel who take up arms against the Constitutional Government to depose public powers, prevent their constitutional formation, or exercise constitutional powers themselves. However, taking up arms to prevent violation of presidential term limits is not punishable rebellion.
- **Article 68**: Military personnel who in wartime burn, destroy buildings/property, or commit acts of violence against civilians without military necessity face 15-20 years imprisonment.
- **Article 69**: Mistreatment of prisoners of war, attacking Red Cross facilities, and destroying cultural monuments (temples, libraries, museums, aqueducts) carry 10-15 years imprisonment.

## Who This Affects

All active-duty members of El Salvador's Armed Forces, military judges and tribunals, and persons accused of purely military offenses. Civilians are not subject to this code.

## Related Laws

- Constitution of El Salvador — Defines the Armed Forces' role and limits on the death penalty
- Codigo Penal (Common Penal Code) — Applies supplementarily per Article 2
- Geneva Conventions — Related to the war crimes provisions in Articles 68-69

## Common Questions

- **Q: Does this code apply to civilians?** — A: No. Article 1 states it applies exclusively to members of the Armed Forces on active duty for purely military crimes.
- **Q: Is the death penalty still applicable?** — A: The code provides for death by firing squad for certain offenses like wartime treason (Article 54), but various provisions have been declared unconstitutional, and El Salvador's constitutional framework limits death penalty application.
- **Q: What counts as treason?** — A: Article 55 lists specific acts including fighting under enemy flags, facilitating enemy invasion, surrendering military positions, impeding reinforcements, recruiting for enemy forces, and espionage-related activities.
- **Q: What is the maximum prison sentence?** — A: Twenty-five years of imprisonment (reclusion), per Article 11.
