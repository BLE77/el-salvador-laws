# Decreto 253 (2021) — General Water Resources Law (Ley General de Recursos Hidricos)

## Summary

This landmark law recognizes the human right to water and sanitation in El Salvador, declaring that water cannot be privatized under any condition. It establishes a comprehensive framework for integrated water resource management, creates the Salvadoran Water Authority (Autoridad Salvadorena del Agua, ASA) as the governing body, and regulates the use, protection, and conservation of all water resources.

## Key Provisions

- **Article 1**: El Salvador recognizes the human right to water (derecho humano al agua) -- the right of all persons to have quality, sufficient, safe, accessible, and affordable water. Also recognizes the human right to sanitation (saneamiento). **Water cannot be privatized under any condition** (el agua no podra ser privatizada bajo ninguna condicion).
- **Article 2**: The law regulates the integrated management of water (gestion integral de las aguas), its sustainability, and guarantees the human right to water and water security for better quality of life.
- **Article 7**: Declares as public utility and social interest (utilidad publica e interes social) all State works for conservation, protection, improvement, use, and management of water resources, especially watershed management, aquifer recharge, and maintaining hydrological balance.
- **Article 8 - Principles**: The law is guided by principles including:
  - Water as a common, vital, finite, and vulnerable good (bien comun, vital, finito y vulnerable)
  - Co-responsibility (corresponsabilidad) between government, municipalities, and community organizations
  - Efficiency (eficiencia) in water use
  - Polluter pays (el que contamina paga)
  - Watershed approach (enfoque de cuenca) as the primary management unit
  - Rights-based approach (enfoque de derechos)
- **Article 10**: Creates the Salvadoran Water Authority (ASA) as an autonomous public law institution (institucion oficial autonoma de derecho publico) with technical, administrative, financial, and budgetary autonomy, under the Ministry of Environment.
- **Article 11**: ASA is the governing body (ente rector) for integrated water resource management, incorporating gender perspective and climate change adaptation.
- **Article 12**: ASA's structure includes a President (appointed by the President of the Republic), a Board of Directors (Junta Directiva), an Executive Directorate, and specialized regional bodies.
- **Article 14**: Persons who have pending debts with the State, are members of political party boards, or are shareholders/directors of water service companies cannot serve as ASA officials.
- **Article 15**: ASA must contract an annual external audit by a specialized firm and is subject to public entity oversight.
- **Public Hydraulic Domain (Article 6)**: All continental, insular, estuarine, and marine waters -- surface and underground -- are part of the public hydraulic domain, along with riverbeds, lake beds, beaches, aquifers, and all mangrove forests.

## Who This Affects

All inhabitants of El Salvador (as water rights holders), municipal water providers, agricultural and industrial water users, the Ministry of Environment, ASA as the new regulatory authority, and any entity involved in water extraction, distribution, or sanitation services.

## Common Questions

- **Q: Can water be privatized in El Salvador?** — A: No. Article 1 explicitly states that water cannot be privatized under any condition.
- **Q: What is ASA?** — A: The Salvadoran Water Authority (Autoridad Salvadorena del Agua), created by Article 10 as an autonomous institution that serves as the governing body for all water resource management in the country.
- **Q: Is access to water a legal right?** — A: Yes. Article 1 recognizes both the human right to water and the human right to sanitation as fundamental rights.
- **Q: Who is responsible if someone pollutes water?** — A: Article 8 establishes the polluter-pays principle (el que contamina paga), making any person or entity responsible for the costs of repairing, mitigating, compensating, and restoring water damage they cause.
