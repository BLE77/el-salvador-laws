# Wrongful Termination in El Salvador

Wrongful or unjust termination (despido injusto) in El Salvador is regulated by the **Labor Code (Decreto 15)**. The law provides strong protections for workers, including mandatory severance pay and special protections for certain categories of employees.

## Just Causes for Termination

Article 50 of the Labor Code lists the specific **just causes** that allow an employer to terminate a worker without severance. These include:

- Serious misconduct or dishonesty
- Repeated unjustified absences
- Insubordination or refusal to follow lawful instructions
- Causing intentional damage to the employer's property
- Disclosing trade secrets
- Attending work under the influence of drugs or alcohol

If the employer cannot prove a just cause, the termination is considered **unjust**.

## Severance Pay (Indemnizacion)

For unjust termination, the employer must pay **30 days of base salary for each year of service** (or proportional fraction). This severance is mandatory and cannot be waived by contract. The calculation uses the worker's most recent ordinary salary.

## Additional Damages

Beyond severance, a wrongfully terminated worker may claim **additional damages** through labor courts, including unpaid wages, proportional aguinaldo, and accrued vacation pay with the 30% premium.

## Special Protections

- **Pregnant workers**: Women enjoy fuero maternal, meaning they **cannot be terminated during pregnancy** or maternity leave without judicial authorization, regardless of cause
- **Union leaders**: Workers holding union positions have **fuero sindical**, requiring prior judicial approval before termination

## Filing a Claim

Workers must file wrongful termination claims within the statute of limitations period. Claims are brought before **labor courts (juzgados de lo laboral)** or initiated through conciliation at the Ministry of Labor.
