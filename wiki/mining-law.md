# Mining Law in El Salvador

In 2017, El Salvador became the **first country in the world** to enact a complete ban on metal mining. The **Ley de Prohibicion de la Mineria Metalica (Decreto 639 of 2017)** prohibits all exploration, extraction, and processing of metallic minerals throughout the national territory.

## The Metal Mining Ban

The ban was driven by environmental and public health concerns, particularly the contamination of water sources by mining operations. Key provisions include:

- **Total prohibition** of metallic mineral exploration and exploitation, including gold, silver, and other metals
- **Nullification** of all existing metal mining permits and concessions
- **Criminal penalties** for anyone who engages in or facilitates metal mining activities
- The ban applies to both domestic and foreign companies

The legislation passed with overwhelming support in the Legislative Assembly, reflecting broad public opposition to mining after years of community mobilization and international legal disputes, including the high-profile case brought by Pacific Rim Mining against El Salvador at ICSID (which El Salvador won).

## Non-Metallic Mining

**Non-metallic mining** remains legal and is permitted with proper authorization. This includes extraction of:

- Sand and gravel (arena y grava)
- Stone and aggregate for construction
- Clay and other non-metallic minerals

Permits for non-metallic mining are issued by the **Ministry of Environment and Natural Resources (MARN)**. Operations must comply with environmental impact assessments and ongoing monitoring requirements.

## Enforcement

The Ministry of Environment oversees compliance. Illegal mining operations are subject to criminal prosecution, fines, and seizure of equipment. Communities are encouraged to report suspected illegal mining to MARN or the PNC.
