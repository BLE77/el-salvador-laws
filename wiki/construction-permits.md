# Construction Permits in El Salvador

Construction permits in El Salvador are governed by the **Ley de Ordenamiento y Desarrollo Territorial (Decreto 644 of 2011)** and the **Municipal Code (Decreto 274)**. The permitting authority depends on the project's location.

## Permitting Authorities

- **OPAMSS (Oficina de Planificacion del Area Metropolitana de San Salvador)**: Handles construction permits within the Greater San Salvador metropolitan area (14 municipalities)
- **Municipal offices (alcaldias)**: Issue permits for construction outside the metropolitan area, through their urban development or engineering departments

## Requirements for a Construction Permit

Applicants must submit:

- **Architectural plans** prepared and signed by a **licensed architect** (arquitecto colegiado) registered with the Colegio de Arquitectos
- **Structural engineering plans** for buildings above a certain size
- **Soil study (estudio de suelos)** for multi-story or large-footprint projects
- **Environmental permit**: Required from the Ministry of Environment (MARN) for projects that may have environmental impact, as determined by the Ley de Medio Ambiente
- **Property documentation**: Proof of ownership or authorization from the property owner
- **Alignment and setback plans** complying with local zoning regulations

## Process

1. Submit the complete application with all required documents
2. Technical review by the permitting authority (typically 30 to 60 days)
3. Payment of permit fees based on project size and type
4. **Permit issued** with conditions and timeline for construction

## Inspections

The permitting authority conducts **inspections during construction** to verify compliance with approved plans. Building without a permit or deviating from approved plans can result in fines, stop-work orders, or mandatory demolition. A final inspection and **habitability certificate (permiso de habitabilidad)** is required before occupancy.
