# Notary Public (Notario) System

In El Salvador, the notary public (notario) plays a central role in legal transactions. Unlike in some countries, Salvadoran notaries are **licensed attorneys** who have obtained special authorization from the Supreme Court to exercise notarial functions.

## Role of the Notary

The notary's primary function is to give **public faith** (fe publica) to legal acts and documents. A notarized document carries a presumption of authenticity and legality. Notaries are required to be impartial and serve as neutral third parties.

## When a Notary Is Required

Notarization is mandatory for many legal transactions, including:

- **Real estate transfers** (compraventa de inmuebles).
- **Company formation** and corporate documents.
- **Powers of attorney** (poderes).
- **Wills** (testamentos).
- **Marriage ceremonies** (notaries can officiate marriages).
- **Certified copies** and authentication of documents.
- **Sworn declarations** (declaraciones juradas).

## The Protocol Book

- Every notary maintains a **protocol book (protocolo)**, which is an official bound volume where all notarized instruments are recorded.
- Documents entered in the protocol are called **escrituras publicas** (public deeds).
- The notary issues certified copies (testimonios) from the protocol to the parties.
- Protocol books must be submitted to the **Supreme Court** for safekeeping after completion.

## Fees

- Notary fees are **regulated by law** and vary depending on the type and value of the transaction.
- For real estate transactions, fees are typically calculated as a percentage of the property value.
- Parties should request a fee estimate before engaging a notary.

## Responsibility

Notaries are personally liable for errors or fraud in their notarial acts and can face disciplinary action, civil liability, or criminal prosecution.
