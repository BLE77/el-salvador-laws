# Decreto 582 (2001) --- Obras Públicas y Transporte

## Summary
Decreto 582 of 2001, commonly referred to as the Civil Aviation Law (Ley de Aviación Civil), establishes the comprehensive legal framework governing all civil aviation activities within the Republic of El Salvador. The statute affirms Salvadoran sovereignty over its national airspace and creates the regulatory structure for the exploitation, use, and management of this airspace in relation to air transport services. It further sets standards for the construction, rehabilitation, and operation of airports and aerodromes, while incorporating international standards established by the International Civil Aviation Organization (ICAO).

## Key Provisions
- **Article 1**: Defines the law's object and scope, establishing that it governs all civil aviation activity conducted in Salvadoran territory, including the airspace, airports, aircraft, and aviation personnel.
- **Article 2**: Declares the state's exclusive sovereignty over the airspace of El Salvador, extending vertically over the territory and territorial waters.
- **Article 3**: Grants the Civil Aviation Authority (Autoridad de Aviación Civil - AAC) the powers to regulate, oversee, and enforce all matters related to civil aviation, including issuing certificates and licenses.
- **Article 5**: Classifies air transport services into commercial, private, and state categories, subjecting each to specific operational and licensing requirements.
- **Article 8**: Regulates the planning, construction, rehabilitation, and operation of airports and airfields, requiring compliance with technical standards for safety and environmental protection.
- **Article 12**: Establishes the National Aircraft Registry and mandates that all aircraft operating commercially must be registered in El Salvador or hold valid foreign registration recognized by the AAC.
- **Article 15**: Requires air operators to obtain an Air Operator Certificate (AOC) demonstrating compliance with safety management systems, maintenance protocols, and insurance requirements.
- **Article 18**: Governs the certification and licensing of flight crew members, mechanics, and air traffic controllers, requiring periodic recertification and medical fitness checks.
- **Article 22**: Mandates adherence to aviation security protocols aligned with ICAO Annex 17, including measures for screening passengers, baggage, and cargo.
- **Article 30**: Addresses environmental protection, requiring noise and emissions monitoring and adherence to international standards for aviation environmental impact.

## Who This Affects
This law applies to all domestic and foreign airlines operating within Salvadoran airspace, airport operators and concessionaires, aircraft owners, pilots and aviation personnel, as well as any entity providing ground handling, maintenance, or air navigation services in the country.

## Related Laws
- **Decreto 37 (1995)**: Previously established regulations regarding public works and transport infrastructure that interact with airport construction provisions.
- **Convención de Chicago (1944)**: International treaty incorporated by reference regarding principles of international civil aviation and airspace rights.
- **Código de Comercio de El Salvador**: Governs commercial liability and contractual obligations for air transport services where not specifically addressed by Decreto 582.
