# Cooperative Law in El Salvador

Cooperatives in El Salvador are regulated by the **Ley General de Asociaciones Cooperativas** and supervised by **INSAFOCOOP (Instituto Salvadoreno de Fomento Cooperativo)**. Cooperatives are member-owned organizations that operate on principles of democratic governance and shared benefit.

## Types of Cooperatives

- **Savings and credit cooperatives (cooperativas de ahorro y credito)** — function similarly to credit unions, providing financial services to members. These are among the most common cooperatives in El Salvador.
- **Production cooperatives (cooperativas de produccion)** — members collectively engage in agricultural, industrial, or artisanal production
- **Housing cooperatives (cooperativas de vivienda)** — members pool resources to develop or acquire housing
- **Consumer cooperatives** — purchase goods in bulk for distribution to members at reduced prices
- **Service cooperatives** — provide shared services such as transportation or marketing

## Formation Requirements

To establish a cooperative:

- A **minimum number of members** is required (typically 15 or more, depending on the type)
- Members must draft **bylaws** (estatutos) governing the cooperative's operations
- The cooperative must be **registered with INSAFOCOOP**, which reviews the bylaws and approves the formation
- An initial **general assembly** must be held to elect the board of directors and oversight committee

## Governance

Cooperatives operate on the principle of **one member, one vote**, regardless of each member's capital contribution. Governance bodies include the general assembly, board of directors (consejo de administracion), and oversight board (junta de vigilancia).

## Tax Benefits

Cooperatives may qualify for tax exemptions on income derived from transactions with their own members. INSAFOCOOP provides guidance on applicable benefits and compliance requirements.
