# Importing Vehicles to El Salvador

Importing a vehicle into El Salvador involves customs duties, inspections, and compliance with age and emissions restrictions. The process is governed by the **Central American Uniform Customs Code (CAUCA)**, national customs regulations, and tax laws including **Decreto 296 (IVA Law)** and the **Income Tax Law (Decreto 134)**.

## Import Duties and Taxes

The following taxes and fees apply when importing a vehicle:

- **DAI (Derechos Arancelarios a la Importacion)**: Import tariff rates vary by vehicle type, typically ranging from **5% to 30%** of the customs value (CIF - cost, insurance, freight).
- **IVA (Impuesto a la Transferencia de Bienes Muebles y a la Prestacion de Servicios)**: The standard **13% VAT** applies on the CIF value plus the DAI.
- Additional fees may include document processing, inspection fees, and port charges.

## Vehicle Age Restrictions

- El Salvador imposes restrictions on the **age of imported vehicles**. Generally, vehicles older than **8 years** from the date of manufacture face additional restrictions or may be prohibited from import, depending on the vehicle type.
- Exceptions may apply to classic/antique vehicles, though these require special permits.
- Commercial and heavy-duty vehicles may have different age thresholds.

## Inspection Requirements

- All imported vehicles must pass a **technical inspection** (revision tecnica) to verify mechanical condition, emissions compliance, and safety standards.
- The Vehicle Registration Office (part of the Vice Ministry of Transport) oversees registration.
- Vehicles must clear customs inspection at the port of entry or border crossing.

## Documentation Required

- Original title or ownership certificate
- Commercial invoice or bill of sale
- Bill of lading or freight documentation
- Passport and tax identification (NIT) of the importer
- Import declaration (declaracion de mercancias)

## Tax Exemptions

Certain exemptions or reduced rates may apply for:
- **Diplomatic vehicles** under international treaties
- Vehicles imported under **free trade zone** (Decreto 405) provisions
- Returning Salvadoran residents under specific conditions (menaje de casa provisions)

## Process Overview

1. Obtain vehicle documentation from the country of origin
2. Ship the vehicle to a Salvadoran port (typically Puerto de Acajutla) or drive through a border crossing
3. File a customs declaration through a licensed customs broker (agente aduanal)
4. Pay all applicable duties and taxes
5. Pass technical inspection
6. Register the vehicle with the appropriate government office and obtain Salvadoran plates

## Practical Notes

Using a licensed **customs broker** (agente aduanal) is effectively mandatory for vehicle imports due to the complexity of the process. Budget for total import costs (duties + IVA + fees) of roughly 25-40% of the vehicle's value. Processing times vary but typically take 1-3 weeks after the vehicle arrives in-country.
