# Identity Theft and Cyber Crimes

Identity theft and computer-related offenses in El Salvador are addressed by the **Penal Code (Codigo Penal), Decreto 1030**, which covers fraud (estafa), and the **Special Law Against Computer Crimes (Ley Especial contra los Delitos Informaticos), Decreto 260**.

## Decreto 260: Computer Crimes Law

This specialized law criminalizes a range of digital offenses, including:

- **Unauthorized access** to computer systems or networks (hacking).
- **Data theft** and unauthorized copying or interception of electronic information.
- **Identity fraud** using another person's digital credentials or personal data.
- **System sabotage** and introduction of malicious software (viruses, ransomware).
- **Online fraud** conducted through electronic means.

Penalties vary by severity but include **prison sentences and fines**, with aggravated penalties when the crime targets financial systems, government databases, or involves minors.

## Penal Code Provisions

- **Estafa (fraud):** Using deception to obtain property or money from another person, including through impersonation or forged documents.
- **Falsificacion de documentos:** Forgery of public or private documents, including digital records.
- These traditional offenses complement the Computer Crimes Law and may be charged alongside it.

## How to Report

- File a criminal complaint (denuncia) with the **Policia Nacional Civil (PNC)**, specifically the **Division de Delitos Informaticos** (Cyber Crimes Unit).
- You can also report directly to the **Fiscalia General de la Republica (FGR)**.
- Preserve all evidence: screenshots, emails, transaction records, and communications related to the incident.

## Prevention

- Guard personal information, especially DUI numbers, bank details, and online credentials.
- Use strong passwords and enable two-factor authentication on financial accounts.
- Monitor bank statements regularly for unauthorized transactions.
