# How to File Noise Complaints in El Salvador

Noise disturbances in El Salvador are handled at the **municipal level** under the Municipal Code (Decreto 274) and can escalate to criminal matters under the **Penal Code (Decreto 1030)**.

## Who to Contact

- **Municipal police (CAM)** — the primary authority for noise violations within city limits. Contact your local alcaldia for the CAM number.
- **PNC (Policia Nacional Civil)** — call **911** for immediate disturbances, particularly late at night or when the municipal police are unavailable.
- **Municipal government** — for ongoing issues with commercial establishments (bars, factories, construction sites), file a formal written complaint at the alcaldia.

## Process for Filing a Complaint

1. **Call 911 or the local CAM** for immediate noise disturbances
2. **Document evidence** — take audio or video recordings with timestamps, and note the dates and times of recurring disturbances
3. **Gather witnesses** — neighbors who can corroborate the noise issue strengthen your complaint
4. **File a formal written complaint** (denuncia) at the municipal government if the issue persists
5. The municipality investigates and can issue **fines** to repeat offenders or revoke operating permits for businesses

## Criminal Charges for Severe Cases

Under the Penal Code, persistent or extreme noise may constitute **perturbacion de la tranquilidad** (disturbance of the peace). This is classified as a misdemeanor (falta) and can lead to fines or short-term detention. Filing a criminal complaint requires going to the **Fiscalia General de la Republica** (Attorney General's Office).

## Practical Tips

Attempt neighborly resolution first. Mediation through the municipal government is an option before escalating to formal complaints. Enforcement varies — urban municipalities like San Salvador tend to respond faster than rural areas.
