# Kidnapping Laws in El Salvador

Kidnapping (secuestro) is one of the most severely punished crimes in El Salvador, governed by the **Penal Code (Decreto 1030 of 1997)**. The law reflects the country's aggressive stance against this offense.

## Basic Kidnapping

The crime of secuestro, defined as unlawfully depriving a person of their liberty with the intent to demand ransom, obtain a concession, or cause harm, carries a penalty of **30 to 45 years imprisonment**. This is among the harshest sentences in the Penal Code.

## Aggravated Kidnapping

The penalty increases to **up to 50 years** when aggravating factors are present:

- The victim is a **minor or elderly person**
- The victim is a **public official** or their family member
- A **ransom** is demanded and collected
- The victim suffers serious physical or psychological harm
- The kidnapping lasts an extended period
- The perpetrator belongs to an organized criminal group

## Express Kidnapping (Secuestro Expres)

Express kidnapping, where victims are briefly held to extract money through ATM withdrawals or immediate ransom, is also heavily penalized. Though shorter in duration, it carries significant prison time and is prosecuted as a serious felony.

## Related Offenses

- **Unlawful detention (privacion de libertad)**: Depriving someone of freedom without ransom intent, carrying 3 to 6 years
- **Human trafficking**: Overlapping cases may be prosecuted under the Special Law against Trafficking (Decreto 824)

## Reporting and Response

Kidnapping should be reported immediately to the **PNC** at 911 or the **Fiscalia General de la Republica**. The PNC has specialized anti-kidnapping units (Division Antiextorsiones). Families are strongly advised not to negotiate directly and to involve authorities immediately.
