# Cybercrime Laws in El Salvador

Cybercrimes in El Salvador are governed by the **Ley Especial contra los Delitos Informaticos y Conexos (Decreto 260)**, which establishes criminal penalties for offenses committed through electronic systems and the internet.

## Covered Offenses

The law criminalizes a range of digital offenses:

- **Unauthorized computer access**: Illegally accessing computer systems, networks, or databases without authorization, carrying 2 to 4 years imprisonment
- **Data theft**: Extracting, copying, or intercepting electronic data without consent, penalized with 3 to 6 years
- **Online fraud (estafa informatica)**: Using technology to deceive and obtain financial gain, carrying 3 to 6 years
- **Identity theft**: Impersonating another person through digital means to cause harm or obtain benefits, penalized with 2 to 4 years
- **Child pornography**: Production, distribution, or possession of child sexual abuse material through electronic means carries **6 to 8 years**, with aggravated penalties when the perpetrator is involved in production
- **System sabotage**: Intentionally damaging or disrupting computer systems or data, carrying 3 to 5 years

## Aggravating Factors

Penalties increase when offenses are committed against government systems, financial institutions, critical infrastructure, or when the perpetrator is a public official or system administrator abusing their access.

## Reporting

Report cybercrimes to the **PNC Division de Delitos Informaticos** (Cybercrime Division). Preserve all digital evidence: screenshots, emails, URLs, transaction records, and chat logs. Do not delete or modify any communications related to the offense.

## Practical Notes

Victims of online fraud should also report to their financial institution immediately to attempt transaction reversal. The Fiscalia General handles prosecution of cybercrime cases.
