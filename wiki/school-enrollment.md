# School Enrollment

Education in El Salvador is **compulsory for children ages 4 through 17**, as established by the Ley General de Educacion (Decreto 917). Public education is free of charge at all levels from parvularia (preschool) through bachillerato (secondary).

## Required Documents for Enrollment

Parents or guardians must present the following when enrolling a child:

- **Birth certificate** (partida de nacimiento) -- see [[birth-registration]]
- **Vaccination card** (esquema de vacunacion completo) issued by the Ministry of Health (MINSAL)
- **Previous school records** (certificado de notas or constancia de estudio) if transferring from another school
- **DUI of the parent or guardian** presenting the child

## Mandatory Vaccinations

The Ministry of Health requires a complete vaccination schedule for school enrollment. Key vaccines include:

- **BCG** (tuberculosis) -- administered at birth
- **Polio** (OPV/IPV) -- multiple doses in infancy
- **DPT** (diphtheria, pertussis, tetanus) -- multiple doses with boosters
- **MMR** (measles, mumps, rubella) -- also known as SRP (sarampion, rubeola, paperas)
- **Hepatitis B** -- series beginning at birth

Vaccination is free at public health units (unidades de salud) throughout the country. Parents who have lost the vaccination card can request a replacement at their local health unit.

## Public vs. Private Schools

Public schools cannot charge tuition or enrollment fees. Private schools set their own fees and may have additional admission requirements, but must still comply with the national curriculum standards set by the Ministry of Education (MINED).
