# Environmental Law

Environmental protection in El Salvador is governed by the **Ley del Medio Ambiente (Environmental Law), Decreto 233**, enacted in 1998. The **Ministry of Environment and Natural Resources (MARN)** is the primary regulatory authority.

## Environmental Impact Assessments

- An **Environmental Impact Assessment (Estudio de Impacto Ambiental, EIA)** is required before beginning construction projects, industrial operations, mining activities, or any project that may significantly affect the environment.
- The EIA must be submitted to MARN for review and approval before permits are granted.
- Projects that proceed without an approved EIA face fines and potential shutdown.

## Pollution and Waste

- The law prohibits **contamination of water, air, and soil** beyond established limits.
- Industrial facilities must comply with emission standards and treat wastewater before discharge.
- **Illegal dumping** of waste, including hazardous materials, carries administrative fines and criminal penalties.
- Municipalities manage solid waste collection under the Municipal Code (Decreto 274).

## Protected Areas

- El Salvador maintains a system of **protected natural areas** (areas naturales protegidas) managed by MARN.
- Activities within protected areas are strictly regulated, including hunting, logging, and construction.
- The Ley de Areas Naturales Protegidas provides the legal framework for conservation.

## Penalties

- Administrative penalties include fines, suspension of operations, and mandatory remediation of environmental damage.
- Severe violations, such as large-scale pollution or destruction of protected ecosystems, can result in **criminal prosecution** under the Penal Code.
- Individuals and communities affected by environmental harm may pursue **civil damages** through the courts.
