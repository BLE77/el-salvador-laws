# Emergency Numbers in El Salvador

El Salvador uses **911** as its national emergency number, connecting callers to police, fire, and medical emergency services. Below is a reference of the most important emergency and public safety numbers.

## Primary Emergency Numbers

| Service | Number |
|---------|--------|
| **National Emergency (police, fire, ambulance)** | **911** |
| **Fire Department (Cuerpo de Bomberos)** | **913** |
| **Emergency Medical Services** | **132** |
| **Anonymous Crime Reporting** | **123** |

## Police

The **Policia Nacional Civil (PNC)** is the national police force. For emergencies, call **911**. The PNC responds to crimes in progress, traffic accidents, and public safety threats. For non-emergency police matters, visit your local PNC delegacion.

## Fire Department

The **Cuerpo de Bomberos de El Salvador** can be reached at **913** for fires, rescues, hazardous material incidents, and natural disaster emergencies.

## Medical Emergencies

Call **132** for emergency medical assistance. Public hospitals (hospitales nacionales) have emergency rooms that treat patients regardless of ability to pay. The main public hospitals include Hospital Rosales and Hospital Bloom in San Salvador.

## Red Cross

The **Cruz Roja Salvadorena** (Salvadoran Red Cross) provides emergency medical transport and assistance. Contact them at **2222-5155**.

## Anonymous Crime Reporting

To report criminal activity anonymously, call **123**. This line allows citizens to provide tips without identifying themselves.

## Practical Tips

- **911 operates 24 hours**, 7 days a week
- Calls to 911 are **free** from any phone, including mobile phones without credit
- When calling, clearly state the **type of emergency**, your **location**, and any relevant details
- Save these numbers in your phone for quick access
