# Child Labor Laws

Child labor in El Salvador is regulated by the **Labor Code (Codigo de Trabajo), Decreto 15**, and the **LEPINA (Ley de Proteccion Integral de la Ninez y Adolescencia), Decreto 431**. These laws set strict limits on when and how minors may work.

## Minimum Working Age

The minimum age for employment is **14 years**. Children under 14 are prohibited from any form of work. Even at 14, significant restrictions apply.

## Restrictions by Age Group

**Ages 14 to 16:**
- Maximum **6 hours per day** and **34 hours per week**.
- **No night work** (between 7:00 PM and 6:00 AM).
- **No hazardous work**, including mining, construction, handling chemicals, or operating heavy machinery.
- Work must not interfere with school attendance.

**Ages 16 to 18:**
- Maximum **8 hours per day**, same as adults.
- Night work restrictions still apply in hazardous industries.
- Hazardous occupations remain prohibited.

## Employer Obligations

- Employers must obtain **written parental or guardian authorization** before hiring a minor.
- A work permit from the **Ministry of Labor (Ministerio de Trabajo)** is required.
- Employers must ensure that work conditions do not harm the minor's physical, mental, or educational development.

## Penalties

Violations of child labor laws carry fines imposed by the Ministry of Labor. Severe cases, such as exploiting children in dangerous work, can result in **criminal prosecution** under the Penal Code. ISNA and the Procuraduria General de la Republica (PGR) may also intervene to protect affected children.
