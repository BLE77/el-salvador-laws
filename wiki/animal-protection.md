# Animal Protection Laws in El Salvador

El Salvador enacted the **Ley de Proteccion Animal** to establish legal protections for animals against cruelty, abuse, and neglect. The law defines animal cruelty broadly and imposes penalties on violators.

## Prohibited Conduct

The law prohibits:

- **Physical abuse** — beating, torturing, mutilating, or causing unnecessary suffering to any animal
- **Neglect** — failing to provide food, water, shelter, or veterinary care
- **Abandonment** — leaving domestic animals without care or supervision
- **Animal fighting** — organizing, promoting, or attending animal fights for entertainment or profit
- **Overworking** — using work animals beyond their physical capacity

## Cockfighting and Cultural Exemptions

While the law generally prohibits animal fighting, **cockfighting** (pelea de gallos) occupies a culturally complex space. Some areas have historically tolerated cockfighting as a traditional practice. However, the trend has moved toward stricter enforcement and reduced tolerance for these activities.

## Penalties

Violations carry **fines** scaled to the severity of the offense. Serious cases of cruelty — particularly those involving torture or death of the animal — can result in **imprisonment**. Repeat offenders face escalating penalties.

## Stray Animal Management

**Municipalities** are responsible for managing stray animal populations within their jurisdiction. This includes capture programs, sterilization campaigns, and coordination with animal welfare organizations. Municipalities may establish shelters and work with NGOs on adoption programs.

## Reporting Cruelty

Animal cruelty can be reported to the **PNC (Policia Nacional Civil)** or to the local municipal government. Animal welfare organizations such as APAS (Asociacion de Proteccion de Animales de El Salvador) also assist with reports and rescues.
