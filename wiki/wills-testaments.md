# Wills and Testaments in El Salvador

Wills (testamentos) in El Salvador are governed by the **Civil Code (Codigo Civil)**, which establishes the types of wills, requirements for validity, and rules on inheritance distribution.

## Types of Wills

- **Open will (testamento abierto)** — the most common form. Executed before a **notary** and **witnesses**. The testator declares their wishes aloud, and the notary records them in a public deed. This is the recommended format for clarity and enforceability.
- **Closed will (testamento cerrado)** — the testator writes or has written their wishes in a sealed document, which is then presented to a notary and witnesses. The contents remain secret until death.
- **Holographic will (testamento olografo)** — entirely handwritten, dated, and signed by the testator. Less common in practice and subject to stricter verification requirements after death.

## Requirements for Validity

- The testator must be of **legal age** (18 years or older)
- The testator must be of **sound mind** (sano juicio) at the time of execution
- The will must be free from coercion, fraud, or undue influence
- Proper formalities (notary, witnesses) must be observed depending on the type

## Forced Heirship Rules

El Salvador applies **forced heirship** (legitima). A testator **cannot freely disinherit** their children or certain other heirs. A portion of the estate — the legitima — is reserved by law for forced heirs (descendants, and in some cases, ascendants and the surviving spouse). The testator may freely dispose of the remaining portion.

## Registration and Execution

After death, the will is opened and executed through judicial proceedings. A notary or court oversees the distribution of assets according to the will's terms, subject to forced heirship rules. The property transfers are registered at the **CNR (Centro Nacional de Registros)**.
