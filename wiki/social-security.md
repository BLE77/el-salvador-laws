# Social Security System

El Salvador's social security system consists of two main pillars: **ISSS** (Instituto Salvadoreno del Seguro Social) for health coverage and **AFP** (Administradora de Fondos de Pensiones) for retirement savings. Enrollment is mandatory for all formal-sector workers under the **Labor Code, Decreto 15**, and related social security legislation.

## Contributions

Contributions are shared between employer and employee, calculated on gross salary:

| Component | Employer | Employee |
|-----------|----------|----------|
| ISSS (health) | 7.5% | 3.0% |
| AFP (pension) | 7.75% | 7.25% |

Employers are responsible for withholding employee shares and remitting all contributions monthly.

## Health Coverage (ISSS)

- Covers **medical consultations, hospitalization, surgery, prescriptions**, and laboratory services.
- **Maternity benefits:** Paid leave of 16 weeks (4 before and 12 after birth) at 75% of salary.
- **Temporary disability:** Cash benefits for work-related and common illness.
- Coverage extends to the worker's **dependents** (spouse and children under 12).

## Pension System (AFP)

- Workers contribute to individual retirement accounts managed by private AFPs.
- **Retirement age:** 60 years for men, 55 years for women, with a minimum of 25 years of contributions for a full pension.
- Benefits include **old-age pension, disability pension**, and **survivor benefits** for dependents of deceased workers.

## Death Benefits

- In the event of a worker's death, the AFP provides a **survivor pension** to eligible dependents.
- ISSS covers funeral expenses up to a legally established amount.
- Both work-related and non-work-related deaths are covered, though benefits may differ.
