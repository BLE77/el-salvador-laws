# Extortion Laws in El Salvador

Extortion (extorsion) is a serious crime in El Salvador governed by the **Penal Code (Decreto 1030 of 1997)**. It has been one of the most commonly reported crimes in the country, particularly linked to gang activity.

## Definition and Penalties

Extortion is defined as compelling a person through intimidation, threats, or violence to hand over money, property, or perform an act against their will. The base penalty is **10 to 15 years imprisonment**.

Aggravated extortion carries higher penalties when:

- Committed by an **organized criminal group** or gang
- The victim is a **minor, elderly person, or public official**
- Committed against **businesses** (including the practice known as "renta")
- Threats involve death or harm to family members
- Firearms or other weapons are used

## Gang-Related Extortion (Renta)

The practice of "renta" — regular payments demanded by gangs from businesses, transport operators, and individuals — has been prosecuted aggressively, especially under the **state of exception** declared since March 2022. This crackdown has resulted in mass arrests and expedited prosecutions of extortion suspects.

## Reporting

- Call the anonymous tip line at **123**
- Report directly to the **Fiscalia General de la Republica**
- File a report at any PNC station
- Anonymous reporting is protected by law

## Witness Protection

El Salvador has a **witness protection program** (Programa de Proteccion de Victimas y Testigos) for those who report or testify in extortion cases. The program can provide relocation, identity protection, and security measures.

## Practical Advice

Do not attempt to negotiate with extortionists. Preserve any evidence of threats (messages, calls, recordings) and report immediately. Businesses should maintain records of any demands received.
