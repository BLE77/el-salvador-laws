# Street Vendor Regulations in El Salvador

Street vending in El Salvador is regulated at the municipal level under the authority granted by the **Municipal Code (Codigo Municipal, Decreto 274 of 1986)**. Each municipality has the power to establish rules governing commercial activity in public spaces, including streets, sidewalks, and plazas.

## Required Permits

To operate legally as a street vendor, the following documents and approvals are generally required:

- **Municipal vendor permit (permiso de ventas)**: Issued by the local alcaldia (municipal government). The application process and fees vary by municipality.
- **Health permit**: Required for food vendors. Issued by the municipal health department or the Ministry of Health, depending on the jurisdiction. Food vendors must comply with basic hygiene and food safety standards.
- **Designated location approval**: Vendors must operate in locations approved by the municipality. Not all areas are open to street commerce.

## Zoning and Restrictions

Municipalities have the authority to designate specific zones where street vending is permitted or prohibited. High-traffic areas, historic districts, and zones near schools or hospitals may have special restrictions. Municipalities periodically update these designations, so vendors should confirm current rules with their local alcaldia.

## Penalties for Non-Compliance

Operating without the required permits can result in:

- **Fines** imposed by the municipal government.
- **Confiscation of goods** by municipal enforcement officers.
- **Revocation of future permit eligibility** in cases of repeated violations.

Vendors who have goods confiscated may petition the municipality for their return, though perishable items are typically not recoverable.
