# Domestic Violence: Detailed Guide

Domestic violence in El Salvador is addressed comprehensively by the **Ley Especial Integral para una Vida Libre de Violencia para las Mujeres (Decreto 520 of 2011)**, known as the LEIV. This law provides broad protections beyond the Penal Code.

## Types of Violence Recognized

The LEIV recognizes multiple forms of violence:

- **Physical violence**: Any act causing bodily harm or physical suffering
- **Psychological violence**: Threats, intimidation, humiliation, isolation, or controlling behavior
- **Sexual violence**: Forced sexual acts within or outside of marriage
- **Economic violence**: Controlling finances, withholding money, preventing employment, or destroying property
- **Patrimonial violence**: Destruction or retention of personal documents, property, or belongings

## Protection Orders

Victims can obtain **protection orders (medidas de proteccion)** through the courts. Key features:

- Orders can be issued **within 24 hours** of the request
- Measures include: removal of the aggressor from the home, prohibition of contact, temporary custody arrangements, and financial support orders
- Violation of a protection order is a separate criminal offense

## Where to Report

- **PNC (Policia Nacional Civil)**: Any police station or by calling 911
- **Fiscalia General de la Republica**: Files criminal charges
- **Call 126**: National hotline for violence against women, available 24 hours
- **Ciudad Mujer centers**: Government facilities providing legal, psychological, and social services

## Shelters and Support

The government operates shelters (albergues) for victims and their children. Legal aid is available at no cost through the Procuraduria General de la Republica and Ciudad Mujer centers. Psychological counseling is provided through the public health system.
