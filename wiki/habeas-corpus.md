# Habeas Corpus and Amparo

El Salvador's Constitution provides two fundamental legal remedies to protect individual rights against government abuse: **habeas corpus** and **amparo**. Both are available to any person and are free to file.

## Habeas Corpus (Exhibicion Personal)

- **Purpose:** Challenges **illegal or arbitrary detention**. Any person who is unlawfully deprived of liberty, or whose conditions of detention violate constitutional rights, can seek habeas corpus.
- **Who can file:** The detained person, their family members, or any third party on their behalf.
- **Where to file:** Petitions are submitted to the **Constitutional Chamber (Sala de lo Constitucional) of the Supreme Court** or to any Chamber of Second Instance.
- **Response time:** The court must respond within **24 hours** of receiving the petition.
- **Effect:** If the court finds the detention unlawful, it orders the **immediate release** of the detainee or correction of the illegal conditions.

## Amparo

- **Purpose:** Protects against **any act by a government authority** that violates constitutional rights, other than illegal detention (which is covered by habeas corpus).
- **Scope:** Broadly applicable -- can challenge laws, regulations, administrative decisions, or actions by public officials.
- **Where to file:** Filed before the **Constitutional Chamber of the Supreme Court**.
- **Provisional measures:** The court may grant **suspension of the challenged act** (medidas cautelares) while the case is decided, to prevent irreparable harm.

## Practical Considerations

- Neither remedy requires a lawyer, though legal representation is recommended for complex cases.
- Both procedures are designed to be **swift and accessible**, reflecting their role as emergency constitutional safeguards.
- Decisions on habeas corpus and amparo by the Constitutional Chamber are final and binding on all government authorities.
