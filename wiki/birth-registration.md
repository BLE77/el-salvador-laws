# Birth Registration

In El Salvador, every birth must be registered at the **Registro Civil** (Civil Registry) of the municipality where the birth occurred. Registration is free of charge and should be completed within **90 days** of the date of birth.

## Required Documents

To register a birth, parents must present the following at the municipal Registro Civil office:

- **Hospital birth certificate** (constancia de nacimiento) issued by the attending physician or midwife
- **DUI** (Documento Unico de Identidad) of both parents
- **Marriage certificate** (if the parents are married) -- this establishes the child's filiation and determines the surname

If the parents are not married, the father must appear in person to voluntarily acknowledge paternity, or paternity may be established through a separate legal process.

## Legal Framework

Birth registration is governed primarily by the **Ley del Nombre de la Persona Natural** (Law of the Name of the Natural Person), which establishes the rules for assigning names and surnames. The Civil Registry system operates under the authority of each municipality's alcaldia (mayor's office).

## Late Registration

If the 90-day deadline is missed, the birth cannot be registered through the normal administrative process. Instead, the parents must initiate a **judicial process** (inscripcion tardia) before a family court. This requires additional documentation, including witness testimony, and can take several weeks or months to resolve.

## Importance

A registered birth certificate (partida de nacimiento) is essential for obtaining a DUI, enrolling in school, accessing healthcare, and virtually all other legal and administrative processes in El Salvador.
