# Human Trafficking Laws in El Salvador

Human trafficking in El Salvador is addressed by the **Ley Especial contra la Trata de Personas (Decreto 824)**, a comprehensive law that criminalizes all forms of trafficking and establishes victim protection mechanisms.

## Forms of Trafficking Criminalized

The law covers all recognized forms of human trafficking:

- **Sexual exploitation**: Forced prostitution, sexual servitude, and commercial sexual exploitation of minors
- **Forced labor**: Compelled work through coercion, deception, or abuse of vulnerability
- **Organ trafficking**: Removal of organs through force or exploitation
- **Forced begging**: Exploiting persons, especially minors, for begging
- **Forced marriage**: Compelling a person into marriage for exploitation purposes
- **Illegal adoption**: When conducted for purposes of exploitation

## Penalties

Trafficking offenses carry **10 to 14 years imprisonment**. Penalties increase when:

- The victim is a minor
- The perpetrator is in a position of authority
- Organized criminal networks are involved
- The victim suffers serious physical or psychological harm

## Victim Protection

The law establishes a comprehensive victim protection framework:

- **Shelters and safe houses** for trafficking survivors
- Legal assistance and psychological support at no cost
- Temporary residency permits for foreign victims
- Victims are not prosecuted for crimes committed as a direct result of being trafficked

## Institutional Framework

A **Special Investigation Unit** within the Fiscalia handles trafficking cases. El Salvador cooperates internationally through **INTERPOL** and bilateral agreements with neighboring countries. The Consejo Nacional contra la Trata de Personas coordinates prevention and response efforts across government agencies.

## Reporting

Report suspected trafficking to the **Fiscalia General de la Republica**, the PNC at 911, or the specialized hotline. Anonymous reports are accepted.
