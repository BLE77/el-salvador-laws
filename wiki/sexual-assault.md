# Sexual Assault and Rape Laws in El Salvador

Sexual assault offenses in El Salvador are governed by the **Penal Code (Decreto 1030 of 1997), Title IV, Crimes Against Sexual Freedom**. The law criminalizes various forms of sexual violence with significant prison sentences.

## Rape (Violacion)

Rape (violacion) is defined as sexual penetration by force, threat, or when the victim is unable to consent. The penalty is **6 to 10 years imprisonment**. Consent obtained through deception of a minor or person with mental disability also constitutes rape.

## Aggravated Rape (Violacion Agravada)

The penalty increases to **14 to 20 years** when aggravating circumstances exist:

- The victim is a **minor under 15 years old**
- A weapon is used during the assault
- **Multiple perpetrators** are involved
- The perpetrator is in a position of authority or trust over the victim
- The assault results in serious injury

## Statutory Rape

Sexual contact with a person **under 15 years of age** is criminalized regardless of apparent consent. The age of the perpetrator relative to the victim may affect the classification and penalty.

## Marital Rape

**Marital rape is a crime** in El Salvador. Marriage does not constitute a defense or mitigating factor for sexual assault.

## Other Sexual Offenses

The Penal Code also penalizes sexual assault without penetration (agresion sexual), sexual harassment (acoso sexual), and indecent acts (actos obscenos), each with distinct penalties.

## Reporting

Victims should report to the **PNC (Policia Nacional Civil)** or directly to the **Fiscalia General de la Republica** (Attorney General's Office). Reports can be filed at any police station. Medical evidence should be preserved by seeking immediate examination at a public hospital.
