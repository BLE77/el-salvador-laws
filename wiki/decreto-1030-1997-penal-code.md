# Decreto 1030 (1997) -- Penal Code

## Summary
The Penal Code of El Salvador defines all criminal offenses and their punishments. It replaced the 1973 code to align with the 1983 Constitution and modern criminal law doctrine, treating criminal law as a last resort for resolving social conflicts. The code covers everything from homicide and theft to corruption and environmental crimes, and establishes the general principles of criminal responsibility, sentencing, and alternative penalties.

## Key Provisions
- **Article 1**: The purpose of the code is to protect legally significant interests (juridical goods) of individuals and the community (*bienes juridicos*).
- **Article 3**: No one may be punished for an act that was not previously defined as a crime by law (*principio de legalidad*).
- **Article 4**: No penalty may be imposed other than one previously established by law for the specific offense.
- **Article 5**: Penalties must be proportional to the severity of the act committed (*principio de necesidad*).
- **Article 17**: Criminal law applies equally to all persons over 18 years old. Minors are subject to a special regime.
- **Article 18**: Salvadoran courts have jurisdiction over crimes committed within the national territory, regardless of the offender's nationality.
- **Article 45**: Principal penalties include prison (6 months to 60 years), weekend arrest (4-150 weekends), house arrest (1-30 days), fines (5-360 day-fines), and community service (4-150 weekly sessions).
- **Article 46**: Accessory penalties include absolute disqualification, special disqualification, expulsion from the country (for foreigners), and loss of driving privileges.
- **Article 71**: In cases of multiple offenses (*concurso real*), all penalties are imposed and served consecutively, but the total may not exceed 60 years.
- **Article 74**: Prison sentences of 6 months to 1 year may be replaced with equivalent alternative penalties (weekend arrest, community service, or fines).
- **Article 77**: Conditional suspension of the sentence (*suspension condicional*) may be granted for prison terms not exceeding 3 years, with a probation period of 2-5 years.
- **Article 86**: Conditional release (*libertad condicional*) may be granted after serving two-thirds of the sentence, subject to good behavior and other requirements.
- **Article 128**: Simple homicide (*homicidio simple*) is punishable by 10-20 years in prison.
- **Article 129**: Aggravated homicide (*homicidio agravado*) -- including killing a family member, killing to facilitate another crime (kidnapping, rape, robbery, extortion, terrorism), or killing with extreme cruelty -- carries 30-50 years in prison.
- **Article 142**: Causing bodily injury (*lesiones*) resulting in 5-20 days of incapacity is punishable by 1-3 years in prison.
- **Article 158**: Rape (*violacion*) -- sexual intercourse by force or while the victim is unconscious or under 15 -- carries 6-10 years in prison.
- **Article 159**: Rape of a minor under 15 carries 14-20 years in prison.
- **Article 165**: Sexual harassment (*acoso sexual*) is punishable by 3-5 years in prison; against a minor under 15, 4-8 years.
- **Article 207**: Theft (*hurto*) -- taking movable property belonging to another with intent to profit -- carries 2-5 years in prison.
- **Article 212**: Robbery (*robo*) -- theft with violence or intimidation -- carries 6-10 years.
- **Article 215**: Fraud/swindle (*estafa*) is punishable by 2-5 years in prison.
- **Article 249-A**: Tax evasion (*evasion de impuestos*) -- omitting taxable income or declaring fictitious expenses -- carries 4-6 years in prison.
- **Article 255**: Environmental contamination (*contaminacion ambiental*) -- causing pollution in soil, atmosphere, or waters -- is punishable by 4-8 years in prison.
- **Article 329**: Public officials who exploit their position in government contracts (*negociaciones ilicitas*) face prison and disqualification.
- **Article 335**: Embezzlement by a public official (*peculado*) carries prison and disqualification from office.
- **Article 345**: Illicit associations (*asociaciones ilicitas*), including gangs and organized crime groups of three or more persons formed with criminal intent, are punishable by 8-12 years in prison.

## Who This Affects
Every person in El Salvador over 18 years of age. It applies equally regardless of nationality to crimes committed on Salvadoran territory. Minors are governed by a separate juvenile justice regime.

## Common Questions
- **Q: What is the maximum prison sentence in El Salvador?** -- A: 60 years. This is the absolute cap even when multiple sentences are combined (Art. 71).
- **Q: Can prison sentences be suspended?** -- A: Yes, for sentences of 3 years or less, the judge may grant conditional suspension with a probation period of 2-5 years (Art. 77).
- **Q: What is the penalty for murder?** -- A: Simple homicide carries 10-20 years. Aggravated homicide (killing a relative, killing to cover another crime, extreme cruelty, etc.) carries 30-50 years (Arts. 128-129).
- **Q: At what age does criminal responsibility begin?** -- A: At 18. Minors are subject to a special regime (Art. 17).
- **Q: What crimes never expire (no statute of limitations)?** -- A: Torture, terrorism, kidnapping, genocide, war crimes, forced disappearance, political persecution, and sexual crimes against minors (Art. 99).
- **Q: Is gang membership a crime?** -- A: Yes. Participating in an illicit association (including maras/pandillas) of three or more persons with criminal intent is punishable by 8-12 years (Art. 345).
