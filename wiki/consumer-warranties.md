# Product Warranties and Returns

Consumer rights regarding product quality and warranties in El Salvador are protected by the **Consumer Protection Law (Ley de Proteccion al Consumidor), Decreto 776**. The **Defensoria del Consumidor** is the government agency responsible for enforcement.

## Product Quality Standards

- All products sold in El Salvador must meet the **quality and specifications advertised** by the seller or manufacturer.
- Sellers cannot misrepresent the characteristics, origin, or condition of goods.
- Products must be safe for their intended use and comply with applicable technical standards.

## Implied Warranty

- An **implied warranty (garantia implicita)** applies to all goods sold, even when no explicit warranty is offered.
- This means products must function properly and be free from hidden defects at the time of purchase.
- The implied warranty covers defects that appear within a reasonable period after purchase.

## Consumer Remedies for Defective Products

When a product is defective or does not meet advertised specifications, the consumer has the right to:

- **Repair** of the defective product at no cost.
- **Replacement** with an identical or equivalent product.
- **Refund** of the purchase price if repair or replacement is not possible or satisfactory.

The consumer chooses which remedy to request, and the seller must comply within a reasonable timeframe.

## Filing a Complaint

- Consumers can file complaints with the **Defensoria del Consumidor**, which mediates disputes and can impose sanctions on non-compliant businesses.
- The complaint process is **free** and does not require a lawyer.
- Bring proof of purchase (receipt, invoice) and evidence of the defect.
- If mediation fails, the Defensoria can issue binding resolutions or the consumer may pursue the matter in court.

## Seller Obligations

Businesses must clearly display warranty terms, return policies, and provide receipts for all transactions.
