# Legal Drinking Age in El Salvador

The legal drinking age in El Salvador is **18 years old**.

## The Law

It is illegal to sell, serve, or provide alcoholic beverages to anyone under 18 years of age. This is enforced through multiple laws:

- **Penal Code (Decreto 1030, 1997)**: Selling alcohol to minors can constitute the crime of "corrupción de menores" (corruption of minors), which carries criminal penalties including imprisonment.
- **Health Code (Decreto 955, 1988)**: Regulates the sale and distribution of alcoholic beverages, including prohibitions on sales to minors.
- **Municipal ordinances**: Local governments set rules on alcohol sales hours, proximity to schools, and licensing requirements for bars and liquor stores.

## Key Rules

- **Buying alcohol**: Must be 18+ to purchase any alcoholic beverage
- **Public consumption**: Many municipalities prohibit drinking alcohol in public spaces (streets, parks, beaches)
- **Sale hours**: Municipalities regulate when alcohol can be sold — typically prohibiting late-night sales
- **Near schools**: Alcohol cannot be sold within a certain distance of schools and educational institutions
- **ID required**: Establishments can and should request identification (DUI) to verify age

## Penalties

- **Selling to minors**: Criminal penalties under the Penal Code, plus administrative fines and potential loss of business license
- **Public intoxication**: Can result in administrative detention and fines under municipal ordinances
- **Providing alcohol to minors**: Parents or adults who provide alcohol to minors can face criminal charges

The age of majority in El Salvador is 18 (established by the Constitution and multiple laws), which is the threshold for alcohol consumption, voting, and full legal capacity.
