# Neighbor Disputes in El Salvador

Disputes between neighbors over property boundaries, construction damage, water runoff, noise, and trees are governed primarily by the **Civil Code (Codigo Civil)** of El Salvador. The law establishes rights and obligations between adjoining property owners and provides mechanisms for resolving conflicts.

## Common Types of Disputes

- **Flooding and water runoff**: Property owners are prohibited from directing water drainage onto a neighbor's land in a manner that causes damage. If construction or grading changes the natural flow of water, the responsible party may be held liable.
- **Walls and boundaries**: Shared boundary walls (paredes medianeras) are subject to specific rules regarding maintenance costs and modifications. Neither party may alter a shared wall without the other's consent.
- **Noise**: Excessive noise that disturbs neighbors can be addressed through municipal ordinances and police complaints.
- **Trees and vegetation**: Overhanging branches or roots that encroach on a neighbor's property may be trimmed by the affected neighbor. Disputes over tree removal or damage caused by falling branches are handled under civil liability rules.

## How to Resolve a Dispute

1. **Direct negotiation** between neighbors is always the recommended first step.
2. **File a complaint with the municipality or police** for immediate issues such as noise disturbances or ongoing property damage.
3. **Request mediation** through the **Procuraduria General de la Republica**, which offers free mediation services for civil disputes.
4. **File a civil lawsuit** (demanda civil) for damages if informal resolution fails. The court can order compensation for proven losses and injunctions to stop ongoing harm.
