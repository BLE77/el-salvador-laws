# Tenant Rights in El Salvador

Tenant rights in El Salvador derive primarily from the **Civil Code** (Codigo Civil), Book IV, governing lease contracts (arrendamiento). While there is no standalone tenant protection statute, established legal principles provide significant safeguards.

## Right to Habitable Conditions

Landlords are legally obligated to deliver and maintain the property in a **habitable condition** suitable for its intended use. This includes functioning plumbing, electrical systems, and structural integrity. If the landlord fails to make necessary repairs after written notice, the tenant may request judicial authorization to make repairs and deduct the cost from rent.

## Right to Privacy

A landlord **cannot enter the rented property without prior notice** and the tenant's consent, except in genuine emergencies. The tenant's right to peaceful enjoyment (goce pacifico) means the landlord must respect the tenant's privacy throughout the lease term.

## Eviction Protections

- Tenants are entitled to **advance written notice** before eviction proceedings begin
- Eviction requires a **judicial process** (juicio de desahucio); landlords cannot bypass the courts
- **Self-help evictions are illegal**: a landlord cannot change locks, cut water or electricity, or remove the tenant's belongings to force departure
- Tenants facing illegal eviction actions can file a complaint with civil courts

## Security Deposit

The security deposit (deposito de garantia) must be **returned at the end of the lease**, minus legitimate deductions for unpaid rent or damages beyond normal wear. Landlords who withhold deposits without justification can be compelled to return them through civil proceedings.

## Remedies for Breach

If a landlord breaches the lease, the tenant may seek **contract termination with damages**, rent reduction for uninhabitable conditions, or judicial enforcement of repair obligations. Claims are filed in civil courts under the Civil Procedure Code (Decreto 712).
