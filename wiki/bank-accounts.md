# Opening Bank Accounts in El Salvador

Foreigners **can** open bank accounts in El Salvador, subject to documentation requirements and anti-money-laundering regulations. The banking sector is regulated by the **Banking Law (Ley de Bancos, Decreto 697 of 1999)** and supervised by the **Superintendencia del Sistema Financiero (SSF)**.

## Requirements for Foreigners

To open an account, a foreign national typically needs:

- **Valid passport** (some banks may also accept a foreign national ID for residents of certain countries)
- **Proof of address** in El Salvador (utility bill, rental contract, or similar)
- **Proof of income or source of funds** (employment letter, tax returns, bank statements from home country)
- **NIT (Numero de Identificacion Tributaria)** - tax identification number, obtainable from the Ministry of Finance
- Some banks require a **minimum opening deposit**, varying by institution and account type
- A **reference** from an existing client may be requested by some banks

## Types of Accounts

- **Cuenta de ahorro** (savings account): Most accessible for foreigners; lower requirements.
- **Cuenta corriente** (checking account): May require residency or higher documentation standards.
- **Cuenta en dolares**: El Salvador's official currency is the **US Dollar** (since 2001), so accounts are dollar-denominated. Bitcoin wallets are separate from traditional banking.

## Major Banks

The Salvadoran banking sector includes both domestic and international institutions:
- Banco Agricola (Bancolombia group)
- Banco Cuscatlan (Grupo Terra)
- Banco Davivienda
- Banco de America Central (BAC)

## Anti-Money-Laundering Compliance

Banks must comply with the **Law Against Money Laundering (Ley Contra el Lavado de Dinero y de Activos)** and international standards. This means:
- **Know Your Customer (KYC)** procedures apply to all account openings
- Banks may request additional documentation for large deposits or transfers
- Suspicious transaction reporting is mandatory
- US FATCA compliance applies, as El Salvador has agreements with the United States

## Regulatory Oversight

The **SSF (Superintendencia del Sistema Financiero)**, governed in part by **Decreto 592 (2011, Financial Supervision Law)**, oversees all banks and financial institutions. Deposits are protected by the **Instituto de Garantia de Depositos (IGD)**, which provides deposit insurance up to a statutory limit.

## Practical Notes

Opening an account as a foreigner is straightforward at major banks, particularly if you have residency or a NIT. The process can take a single visit or several days depending on the bank's internal verification. Speaking Spanish or bringing a Spanish-speaking companion is advisable, as English-language services are limited outside premium banking divisions. Some banks may be reluctant to open accounts for non-residents without a clear connection to El Salvador.
