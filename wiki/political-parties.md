# Political Parties and Electoral Law in El Salvador

Political parties in El Salvador are regulated by the **Electoral Code (Codigo Electoral, Decreto 413 of 2013)**, which governs party registration, financing, campaigns, and the electoral process.

## Party Registration

To register as a political party, an organization must:

- Collect a minimum of **50,000 signatures** from eligible citizens
- Submit founding documents including the party's statutes, principles, and organizational structure to the TSE
- Designate a legal representative and party authorities
- Demonstrate a functioning organizational presence across the country

Parties that fail to obtain a minimum percentage of votes in a general election may lose their legal registration.

## Public Financing

Registered political parties receive **public financing (deuda politica)** from the state based on the number of votes obtained in the most recent elections. This funding is intended to cover operational and campaign expenses. Parties must submit financial reports to the TSE demonstrating proper use of public funds.

## Campaign Regulations

- **Campaign spending limits** are established by the TSE for each election cycle
- Campaigns are restricted to an official campaign period before elections
- Political advertising in media must comply with TSE regulations on content and timing
- Foreign contributions to political campaigns are prohibited

## Electoral Oversight

The **Tribunal Supremo Electoral (TSE)** is the independent body that oversees all elections. It is responsible for:

- Administering voter registration and the electoral roll
- Organizing and supervising election day operations
- Certifying election results
- Resolving electoral disputes

## Legislative Representation

Members of the **Asamblea Legislativa** (84 seats) are elected through a **proportional representation system** based on departmental districts. Citizens can vote for party lists or individual candidates within lists through preferential voting. Presidential elections use a direct popular vote with a second-round runoff if no candidate obtains more than 50%.
