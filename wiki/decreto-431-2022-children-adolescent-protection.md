# Decreto 431 (2022) — Comprehensive Protection of Children and Adolescents Law

## Summary

This law guarantees the full exercise and enjoyment of rights for all children and adolescents in El Salvador, regardless of nationality. It establishes a comprehensive protection system based on the principle of the best interest of the child, covering rights to life, health, identity, education, family, and protection from all forms of abuse. It replaced the earlier LEPINA (D.L. 839/2009).

## Key Provisions

- **Article 3**: All children and adolescents are full subjects of rights (sujetos plenos de derechos). Rights apply from the moment of conception until age 18.
- **Article 4**: Childhood (ninez) spans from conception to before turning 12; adolescence from 12 to 18. Early childhood (primera infancia) covers gestation to age 8.
- **Article 5**: In case of doubt about age, the person is presumed to be a child over an adolescent, or an adolescent over an adult (presuncion de ninez y adolescencia).
- **Article 6**: The law applies to all children and adolescents, whether national or foreign, within El Salvador's territory.
- **Article 10**: The family is the fundamental base of society (base fundamental de la sociedad). Parents have shared, equal, and non-renounceable duties in care, education, and protection of their children.
- **Article 12**: The best interest of the child (interes superior) is mandatory in all judicial, administrative, and legislative decisions. Factors include the child's condition as a rights-holder, their opinion, stage of development, and physical/psychological/moral well-being.
- **Article 16**: Right to life from the moment of conception (derecho a la vida desde el instante de la concepcion). The family, society, and the State must ensure dignified life and integral development.
- **Article 22**: Right to a dignified life (vida digna), including adequate food, safe housing, clean water, healthcare, education, clothing, and recreation.
- **Article 27**: The State must implement policies for prevention and care of pregnancy in children and adolescents (embarazo en ninas y adolescentes). Pregnant minors have the right to be protected from discrimination.
- **Article 32**: The State guarantees protection of the mental health (salud mental) of children and adolescents, including specific programs and specialized treatment services.
- **Article 36**: Children and adolescents with disabilities are guaranteed universal and inclusive access to comprehensive health care (atencion integral en salud para ninas, ninos y adolescentes con discapacidad).
- **Article 38**: Right to a healthy environment (ambiente sano), ecologically sustainable and adequate for development.
- **Article 39**: Right to free development of personality (libre desarrollo de la personalidad).
- **Article 40**: Right to identity (derecho a la identidad), including name, nationality, and right to know one's origin.
- **Article 42**: The State guarantees immediate, free, and expeditious birth registration (inscripcion del nacimiento).

## Who This Affects

All children and adolescents (persons under 18) in El Salvador, their parents and guardians, educational institutions, healthcare providers, government agencies, and the judiciary. The law also applies to foreign minors present in El Salvador.

## Related Laws

- Decreto 839 (2009) — Previous LEPINA (replaced by this law)
- Convention on the Rights of the Child (ratified by El Salvador in 1990)
- Decreto 286 (2019) — Migration Law, which includes protections for migrant children

## Common Questions

- **Q: At what age does a person stop being covered by this law?** — A: At age 18. Childhood is defined as conception to 12 years; adolescence from 12 to 18.
- **Q: What is the "best interest of the child" principle?** — A: Article 12 requires that all judicial, administrative, and legislative decisions prioritize the child's integral development and enjoyment of rights, considering their opinion, developmental stage, and overall well-being.
- **Q: Does this law apply to foreign children in El Salvador?** — A: Yes. Article 6 explicitly applies the law to all children regardless of nationality.
- **Q: What happens if there is doubt about a person's age?** — A: Article 5 establishes a presumption in favor of the younger status -- a person is presumed to be a child rather than an adolescent, or an adolescent rather than an adult.
