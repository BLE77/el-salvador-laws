# Decreto 379 (2013) — Public-Private Partnership Law (Ley de Asocios Publico Privados)

## Summary

This law establishes the legal framework for public-private partnerships (Asocios Publico Privados or APP) in El Salvador, enabling the private sector to participate in the provision of public infrastructure and services. It defines contract types, institutional oversight through PROESA, fiscal safeguards through the Ministry of Finance, and labor protections, all within a framework of legal certainty and public interest.

## Key Provisions

- **Article 1**: The law establishes the framework for PPP projects for the provision of infrastructure and public services of general interest, efficiently and effectively. The private sector contributes economic resources, skills, and knowledge.
- **Article 2**: Authorized contracting entities include the Executive Branch and its agencies, autonomous institutions (including the Hydroelectric Commission, CEL), and municipalities (municipalidades).
- **Article 3**: The law applies to contracts where state entities commission a private investor to design, construct, repair, improve, equip, operate, and maintain infrastructure. Contracts may also include public service provision. The minimum project threshold is 45,000 times the monthly minimum wage.
- **Article 4 - Contract Modalities**: PPP contracts may take several forms:
  - **Public works concessions** (concesion de obra publica): The state delivers public domain assets for the private party to construct, expand, equip, repair, or maintain
  - **Public service concessions** (concesion de servicio publico): The private party uses its own assets to provide public services
  - **Activities of general interest**: Exploitation or execution of activities that promote strategic economic sectors
- **Article 6 - Financial Classification**:
  - **Self-sustaining** (autosostenibles): Projects where user tariffs cover all costs and provide adequate return without state resources or guarantees
  - **Co-financed** (cofinanciados): Projects requiring state fiscal resources or guarantees for sustainability
- **Article 8 - Guiding Principles**: Include legality, constitutional supremacy, public interest supremacy (interes publico prevalecera sobre el interes particular), state oversight (rectoria del Estado), transparency, efficiency, and value for money.
- **Article 9 - Labor Protections**: The law does not weaken labor protections. Private participants must guarantee freedom of association, collective bargaining, elimination of forced labor, abolition of child labor, and non-discrimination in employment. These obligations extend to subcontractors.
- **Article 14**: PROESA's Board of Directors approves PPP projects, their bidding rules, and contracts. It proposes PPP policies to the President and reports annually to the President and the Legislative Assembly.
- **Article 15**: PROESA manages the PPP project pipeline, provides technical assistance, and promotes investment.
- **Article 20**: The Ministry of Finance (Ministerio de Hacienda) evaluates risk allocation and fiscal impacts, issues financial opinions on bidding documents and contracts, and verifies fiscal consistency of commitments.
- **State Liability**: The State's responsibility in PPP contracts is always limited to its investment (Article 6).

## Who This Affects

Government agencies and municipalities seeking private-sector partnerships, private investors and construction companies, public service users, workers employed under PPP projects, PROESA as the oversight body, and the Ministry of Finance for fiscal evaluation.

## Related Laws

- Constitution of El Salvador, Articles 101-102, 113 — Economic order, free enterprise, and economic associations
- Ley de Adquisiciones y Contrataciones de la Administracion Publica (LACAP) — Public procurement (PPP contracts are excluded from LACAP per this law)
- Labor Code — Labor protections referenced in Article 9

## Common Questions

- **Q: What is the minimum size for a PPP project?** — A: The investment and updated operation/maintenance costs must exceed 45,000 times the current monthly minimum wage (Article 3).
- **Q: Who approves PPP projects?** — A: PROESA's Board of Directors approves projects, their bidding rules, and contracts (Article 14). The Ministry of Finance must issue a favorable financial opinion on fiscal implications.
- **Q: Is the State liable for PPP project debts?** — A: The State's responsibility is always limited to its investment (Article 6). The private party bears commercial and operational risks per the contract terms.
- **Q: Are labor rights protected in PPP projects?** — A: Yes. Article 9 requires private participants and their subcontractors to guarantee freedom of association, collective bargaining, elimination of forced labor, abolition of child labor, and non-discrimination.
- **Q: Can municipalities use PPPs?** — A: Yes. Article 2 explicitly authorizes municipalities alongside the Executive Branch and autonomous institutions.
