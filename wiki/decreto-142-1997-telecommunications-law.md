# Decreto 142 (1997) — Telecommunications Law (Ley de Telecomunicaciones)

## Summary

This law regulates El Salvador's entire telecommunications sector, including telephony (declared a public service), radio spectrum management, broadband, broadcasting, and information and communication technologies. The Superintendency General of Electricity and Telecommunications (SIGET) is the regulatory authority responsible for enforcement. The law has been extensively reformed to address modern ICT issues.

## Key Provisions

- **Article 1**: The law regulates and supervises telecommunications and ICT activities (tecnologias de la informacion y la comunicacion), including radio spectrum management, access to essential resources, the numbering plan, public telephony, network administration, service quality, coverage, continuity, and user rights protection. SIGET is the responsible enforcement authority.
- **Article 2-A - Guiding Principles**: The sector is governed by principles of:
  - Equality (igualdad) in service conditions
  - Inclusion (inclusion) for persons with disabilities and the elderly
  - Equity (equidad) in access to scarce resources like spectrum
  - Privacy of information (privacidad de la informacion)
- **Article 3**: The law applies to all natural and legal persons involved in telecommunications activities, including broadcasting, telephony, data transmission, broadband, and spectrum use.
- **Article 4**: Prices and conditions for telecom services between operators are freely negotiated, except for access to essential resources (recursos esenciales).
- **Article 5**: All telecommunications equipment must comply with standards recommended by the International Telecommunication Union (UIT) or other recognized international organizations.
- **Article 7**: Telephony is a public service (servicio publico). Operators must obtain a concession from SIGET, granted automatically for 30 years upon meeting registration requirements. Concessions are unlimited in number and geography.
- **Article 8-A**: SIGET determines maximum tariffs for fixed and mobile public telephony and basic interconnection charges, based on cost studies and international price comparisons using ITU-recognized methodology. Annual tariff reviews are required.
- **Article 10**: SIGET manages the radio spectrum (espectro radioelectrico), including granting operating permits, updating technical regulations, administering the National Frequency Allocation Table (CNAF), and coordinating international spectrum use.
- **Article 13**: The radio spectrum is classified into three categories:
  - **Free use** (uso libre): Bands available to the public under conditions set by SIGET
  - **Official use** (uso oficial): Bands reserved for government institutions and future applications
  - **Regulated use** (uso regulado): All other bands requiring a concession, including commercial, community, and private use

## Who This Affects

All telecommunications operators and service providers in El Salvador, mobile and fixed-line carriers, internet service providers, broadcasters, equipment manufacturers, and consumers of telecommunications services. SIGET as the regulatory body plays a central role.

## Related Laws

- Ley de Creacion de SIGET — Establishes the Superintendency General of Electricity and Telecommunications
- Decreto 807 (1996) — Previous Telecommunications Law (replaced by this law)
- Competition Law (Ley de Competencia) — SIGET may consult with the Superintendency of Competition on spectrum transfers

## Common Questions

- **Q: Who regulates telecommunications in El Salvador?** — A: SIGET (Superintendencia General de Electricidad y Telecomunicaciones) is the sole regulatory authority.
- **Q: How long is a telephony concession?** — A: 30 years, granted automatically upon meeting registration requirements (Article 7). Concessions can be renewed.
- **Q: Can telecom operators set their own prices?** — A: Prices between operators are generally freely negotiated (Article 4), but SIGET sets maximum tariffs for public telephony and basic interconnection charges (Article 8-A).
- **Q: Can spectrum concessions be transferred?** — A: Yes, subject to SIGET authorization regarding technical aspects, financial feasibility, and continuity of service (Article 9-B). The Superintendency of Competition may be consulted.
