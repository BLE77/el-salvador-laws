# Parking Regulations in El Salvador

Parking in El Salvador is regulated under the **Traffic and Road Safety Law (Ley de Transito y Seguridad Vial, Decreto 420)** and supplemented by municipal ordinances.

## Prohibited Parking Areas

Vehicles may **not** be parked in:

- **Red zones** (zonas rojas) — marked curbs indicating no parking at any time
- **Fire lanes** — areas designated for emergency vehicle access
- **Crosswalks** (pasos peatonales) — parking on or immediately adjacent to pedestrian crossings is prohibited
- **Bus stops** (paradas de buses) — reserved exclusively for public transport
- **Disabled parking spots** — reserved for vehicles displaying a valid disability permit; unauthorized use results in fines
- **Double parking** — blocking other vehicles or traffic lanes
- **Sidewalks** — parking on pedestrian walkways is prohibited

## Towing and Fines

Illegally parked vehicles can be **towed** (remolcados) by traffic police. The owner must pay towing fees plus storage charges to recover the vehicle. Fines vary by municipality and the nature of the violation. Unpaid fines can prevent vehicle registration renewal.

## Private Parking Lots

Commercial parking lots and garages are regulated under the **Consumer Protection Law (Decreto 776)**. Operators must:

- Clearly display rates before entry
- Issue a receipt or ticket
- Accept liability for damage or theft occurring on their premises (though many attempt to disclaim this through posted signs, such disclaimers may not hold up under consumer protection standards)

## Municipal Regulations

Each municipality may impose additional parking rules, including metered parking zones, residential permit areas, and time-limited parking. San Salvador and other major cities have municipal parking enforcement officers (agentes de transito) who issue citations.
