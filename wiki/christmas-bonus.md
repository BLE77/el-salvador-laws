# Aguinaldo (Christmas Bonus) in El Salvador

The aguinaldo is a mandatory annual bonus that all employers in El Salvador must pay, governed by the **Labor Code (Decreto 15), Articles 196-202**. It is one of the most important labor rights in the country.

## Who Is Entitled

**All workers** are entitled to the aguinaldo, including:

- Full-time and part-time employees
- Domestic workers (trabajadores domesticos)
- Temporary and seasonal workers (proportional to time worked)
- Workers on probationary periods

The only requirement is that the worker has been employed for at least the relevant period. Workers who have not completed a full year receive a proportional amount.

## Amount by Seniority

The aguinaldo amount is based on the worker's length of continuous service:

- **1 to 3 years of service**: 15 days of base salary
- **3 to 10 years of service**: 19 days of base salary
- **10 or more years of service**: 21 days of base salary

The calculation uses the worker's **ordinary base salary** at the time of payment.

## Payment Deadline

Employers must pay the aguinaldo **between December 12 and December 20** of each year. Failure to pay by the deadline constitutes a labor violation.

## Employer Obligations and Penalties

- The aguinaldo **cannot be waived** by the worker, even by written agreement
- It cannot be offset against other payments or debts
- Employers who fail to pay face **administrative fines** from the Ministry of Labor and may be subject to labor court claims
- Workers can file complaints with the **Ministerio de Trabajo** or pursue claims in labor courts (juzgados de lo laboral)

The aguinaldo is exempt from income tax up to the legally established threshold.
