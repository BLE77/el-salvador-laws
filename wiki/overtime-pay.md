# Overtime Pay

Overtime compensation in El Salvador is regulated by the **Labor Code (Codigo de Trabajo), Decreto 15**. The law sets clear limits on working hours and mandates premium pay for work beyond standard hours.

## Standard Working Hours

- **Daytime shift:** Maximum **8 hours per day** and **44 hours per week** (Monday through Friday, with a half-day Saturday).
- **Night shift (7:00 PM to 6:00 AM):** Maximum **7 hours per day** and **39 hours per week**.
- **Mixed shift:** Maximum 8 hours if fewer than 3.5 hours fall within the night period.

## Overtime Rates

- All hours worked beyond the daily or weekly maximum are considered overtime.
- Overtime is paid at **double the regular hourly rate** (100% premium on top of base pay).
- There is no distinction between weekday overtime and other overtime -- all overtime carries the same double rate.

## Night Work Premium

- Workers on night shifts (7:00 PM to 6:00 AM) receive a **25% premium** over the regular daytime wage, even when not working overtime.

## Sundays and Holidays

- Work performed on **Sundays or official holidays** is compensated at **double the regular rate**.
- If a worker's regular schedule includes Sunday, the employer must provide a compensatory rest day during the week.

## Employer Restrictions

- Employers **cannot force excessive overtime** as a regular practice. Overtime should be exceptional, not routine.
- Workers have the right to refuse overtime that exceeds legal limits.
- Failure to pay overtime premiums is a labor violation reportable to the **Ministry of Labor (Ministerio de Trabajo)**.
