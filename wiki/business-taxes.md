# Business Tax Obligations in El Salvador

Businesses operating in El Salvador face several tax obligations, primarily governed by the **Income Tax Law (Decreto 134)**, the **IVA Law (Decreto 296)**, and the **Tax Code (Decreto 230)**.

## Income Tax (Impuesto sobre la Renta)

- **Corporate rate**: 25% on net taxable income exceeding $150,000 USD
- **Lower rate**: 30% applies to taxable income up to $150,000 (graduated scale)
- Annual income tax returns must be filed by **April 30** of the following year
- Advance payments (pago a cuenta) of 1.75% on gross income are due monthly

## Value Added Tax / IVA (Impuesto a la Transferencia de Bienes Muebles y Prestacion de Servicios)

- Standard rate: **13%** on the sale of goods and services
- IVA returns are filed **monthly** by the 10th business day of the following month
- Businesses must issue IVA-compliant invoices (comprobantes de credito fiscal or facturas de consumidor final)
- Export sales are zero-rated

## Municipal Taxes

Businesses pay **municipal taxes (impuestos municipales)** to the local alcaldia based on the company's assets or revenue. Rates vary by municipality and are established under the Municipal Code (Decreto 274).

## Withholding Obligations

Employers and certain businesses must withhold and remit:

- Income tax on employee salaries
- IVA withholding for certain transactions (1% for large taxpayers)
- Social security (ISSS) and pension (AFP) contributions

## Penalties for Non-Compliance

The Tax Code (Decreto 230) establishes penalties for late filing, late payment, and underreporting, including **fines, surcharges, and interest**. Repeated non-compliance may trigger audits by the Direccion General de Impuestos Internos (DGII). Criminal prosecution is possible for tax evasion.
