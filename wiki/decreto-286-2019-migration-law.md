# Decreto 286 (2019) — Special Law on Migration and Foreigners

## Summary

This law provides a comprehensive framework for migration and immigration in El Salvador, regulating the entry, exit, transit, and stay of both nationals and foreigners. It covers visa types, residency permits, naturalization, deportation procedures, and the rights of migrants, all within a human rights framework. It consolidated and replaced multiple older migration-related laws.

## Key Provisions

- **Article 1**: The law guarantees efficient migration management, regulating entry, exit, transit, and stay of nationals and foreigners in a framework of human rights (derechos humanos).
- **Article 2**: The law's purpose is to guarantee rights and establish migration obligations through agile, effective, and efficient procedures (procedimientos agiles, eficaces y eficientes).
- **Article 3**: Applies to all Salvadoran nationals entering, leaving, and returning, and to all foreigners entering, transiting, staying, and leaving the territory.
- **Article 4**: Diplomatic agents, consular officials, their spouses and children, and members of diplomatic missions are excluded from this law.
- **Article 6 - Principles**: The law is governed by key principles:
  - Human dignity (dignidad humana)
  - Human mobility (movilidad humana)
  - Best interest of the child (interes superior de la nina, nino y adolescente)
  - Due process (debido proceso)
  - Equality and non-discrimination (igualdad)
- **Article 7**: The Ministry of Justice and Public Security (Ministerio de Justicia y Seguridad Publica) executes migration policy, controls migration through the Directorate General, and can order expulsion of foreigners.
- **Article 12**: The General Directorate of Migration and Foreigners (Direccion General de Migracion y Extranjeria) is the primary institution applying this law, with functions including border control, visa authorization, and residency decisions.
- **Article 14**: Directors and senior staff must be Salvadoran by birth with university degrees and proven ethical conduct.
- **Article 20 - Protected Rights**: Foreigners have specially protected rights including personal integrity, personal liberty, access to civil registry, individual treatment in proceedings, and non-discrimination.
- **Article 22**: Foreigners have guarantees including access to justice, due process, consular communication, minimum conditions in detention centers, and access to social security.
- **Article 23**: Salvadoran nationals who arrive without travel documents can verify their nationality through other means available to the Directorate General.

## Who This Affects

All Salvadoran citizens traveling internationally, all foreign nationals entering or residing in El Salvador, immigration officials, employers of foreign workers, and organizations involved in migration services. Diplomatic personnel are generally excluded.

## Related Laws

- Previous laws replaced: Migration Law (D.L. 2772/1958), Foreigners Law (D.L. 299/1986), Passport Law (D.L. 1020/1982)

## Common Questions

- **Q: Who handles immigration matters in El Salvador?** — A: The General Directorate of Migration and Foreigners (Direccion General de Migracion y Extranjeria), under the Ministry of Justice and Public Security.
- **Q: What rights do foreigners have in migration proceedings?** — A: Article 20 guarantees personal integrity, liberty, access to civil registry, individual treatment (no collective expulsions), and non-discrimination. Article 22 adds access to justice, consular assistance, and social security.
- **Q: Can a Salvadoran enter the country without a passport?** — A: Article 23 allows nationals to verify their nationality through other means if they lack travel documents at the border.
- **Q: Are diplomats subject to this law?** — A: No. Article 4 excludes diplomatic agents, consular officials, and their family members.
