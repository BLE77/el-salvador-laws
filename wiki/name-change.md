# Legal Name Change in El Salvador

## Legal Framework

The legal name change process in El Salvador is governed by the **Family Code, Decreto 677** (Codigo de Familia) along with civil registry regulations. The procedure involves the family court system and the civil registry (Registro Civil), which maintains official records of births, marriages, and other vital events.

## Who Can Request a Name Change

Any Salvadoran citizen or legal resident may petition for a legal name change. Common reasons include correcting errors in the birth record, adopting a name that aligns with personal identity, changing a name following marriage or divorce, or modifying a name that causes embarrassment or hardship. The court has discretion to evaluate whether the stated reason is sufficient.

## Requirements

To initiate the process, the petitioner must gather the following documentation:

- **Certified birth certificate** (partida de nacimiento) issued by the corresponding municipal registry.
- **Valid identification document** (DUI - Documento Unico de Identidad).
- **Written petition** stating the desired new name and the reason for the change.
- **Supporting evidence**, which may include affidavits, medical documentation, or other relevant records depending on the reason for the change.
- **Legal representation**: While not strictly required in all cases, hiring an attorney is strongly recommended to navigate the judicial process.

## Procedure

1. **Filing the petition**: The request is submitted to the **Juzgado de Familia** (Family Court) with jurisdiction over the petitioner's domicile.
2. **Judicial review**: The court examines the petition and supporting documents. A hearing may be scheduled where the petitioner explains the reasons for the change.
3. **Publication**: If the court approves the change, the resolution is published in the **Diario Oficial** (Official Gazette) to provide public notice.
4. **Registry update**: Following publication, the civil registry updates the birth record and issues corrected documentation.

## Timeline and Costs

The entire process typically takes between **2 and 6 months**, depending on the court's caseload and the complexity of the case. Costs include court filing fees, attorney fees if applicable, and the publication fee for the Diario Oficial notice.

## After the Name Change

Once the name change is finalized, the individual must update all personal documents, including the DUI, passport, bank accounts, and property records to reflect the new legal name.
