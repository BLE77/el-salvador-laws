# Buying Real Estate in El Salvador: Step-by-Step

Purchasing property in El Salvador follows a structured legal process governed by the **Civil Code** and registered at the **CNR (Centro Nacional de Registros)**. Understanding each step helps avoid common pitfalls.

## Step-by-Step Process

### 1. Find the Property
Work with a real estate agent or search independently. There is no MLS system — properties are listed through agents, online platforms, and classified ads.

### 2. Due Diligence at CNR
Before committing, conduct a **title search** (estudio de titulo) at the CNR to verify:
- The seller is the **legal owner**
- There are no **liens, mortgages, or encumbrances** (gravamenes) on the property
- There are no pending **legal disputes** affecting the title
- Property boundaries and measurements match what is being sold

### 3. Negotiate Price and Terms
Agree on the sale price, payment terms, and timeline. All amounts are typically denominated in **US dollars**.

### 4. Sign Promesa de Venta
A **promise to sell** (promesa de venta) is a preliminary contract that commits both parties to the transaction. It typically includes a deposit (arras) and a deadline for completing the sale.

### 5. Execute Escritura Publica
The final sale is formalized through an **escritura publica** (public deed) executed before a Salvadoran **notary**. Both buyer and seller must be present (or represented by power of attorney).

### 6. Pay Transfer Tax
The property transfer tax is **3% of the sale price** for urban properties. Rural property rates may vary. The buyer typically pays this tax.

### 7. Register at CNR
The notary submits the escritura to the CNR for **registration**. Only after registration is the buyer recognized as the legal owner. Registration fees apply.

## Costs to Expect

The buyer generally pays: notary fees (negotiable, often 1-2% of the sale price), registration fees at CNR, and the transfer tax. Sellers pay their own capital gains tax if applicable.
