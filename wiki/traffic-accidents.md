# Traffic Accidents

Traffic accidents in El Salvador are governed by the **Traffic and Road Safety Law (Ley de Transporte Terrestre, Transito y Seguridad Vial), Decreto 420**, with criminal aspects falling under the **Penal Code (Decreto 1030)**.

## What to Do After an Accident

1. **Stop immediately** at the scene. Leaving the scene of an accident is a criminal offense.
2. **Call the police** (PNC) and, if there are injuries, emergency services (dial 911).
3. **Do not move the vehicles** until police arrive and document the scene, unless they are blocking emergency access or creating an immediate safety hazard.
4. **Exchange information** with the other party: name, DUI (identification), vehicle registration, insurance details.
5. **Take photographs** of vehicle damage, the accident scene, and any injuries.

## Police Report

- Police officers respond to the scene and prepare an **accident report (parte policial)**, documenting the circumstances, positions of vehicles, witness statements, and apparent causes.
- This report is essential for insurance claims and any subsequent legal proceedings.
- Request a copy of the report for your records.

## Insurance Claims

- All vehicles must carry **mandatory liability insurance** (seguro obligatorio).
- File a claim with your insurance company promptly, providing the police report and supporting documentation.
- Insurance covers property damage and bodily injury to third parties, depending on policy coverage.

## Criminal Liability

- If the accident results in **injuries or death**, a criminal investigation may be opened under the Penal Code for negligent injury (lesiones culposas) or negligent homicide (homicidio culposo).
- Driving under the influence of alcohol or drugs significantly increases criminal penalties.
- Minor property-damage-only accidents are typically handled as administrative or civil matters.
