# Decreto 57 (2021) — Bitcoin Law (Ley Bitcoin)

## Summary

This law established Bitcoin as legal tender in El Salvador alongside the US dollar, making El Salvador the first country in the world to do so. It regulates Bitcoin's use in transactions and sets the framework for its acceptance. A 2025 reform (D.L. 199/2025) made Bitcoin acceptance voluntary for private parties only, removing the mandatory acceptance requirement.

## Key Provisions

- **Article 1**: Bitcoin is regulated as legal tender (curso legal) with unlimited liberating power, but acceptance is **voluntary** for natural and legal persons with total private participation only. This does not affect the Monetary Integration Law (Ley de Integracion Monetaria). *(Reformed by D.L. 199/2025)*
- **Article 2**: The exchange rate between Bitcoin and the US dollar is set freely by the market (tipo de cambio establecido libremente por el mercado).
- **Article 3**: All prices may be expressed in Bitcoin (todo precio podra ser convertido en Bitcoin).
- **Article 4**: Repealed (derogado) by D.L. 199/2025.
- **Article 5**: Bitcoin exchanges are not subject to capital gains taxes (impuestos sobre las ganancias de capital).
- **Article 6**: The US dollar remains the reference currency for accounting purposes (moneda de referencia).
- **Article 7**: Only natural persons or legal entities with total private participation may accept Bitcoin as payment when offered by the buyer, or for any monetary obligation. *(Reformed by D.L. 199/2025)*
- **Articles 8-9**: Repealed (derogado) by D.L. 199/2025.
- **Article 10**: The Executive Branch will create the institutional structure necessary to implement this law (estructura institucional).
- **Article 11**: The Central Reserve Bank (Banco Central de Reserva) and the Superintendency of the Financial System (Superintendencia del Sistema Financiero) will issue corresponding regulations.
- **Article 12**: Government monetary obligations, domestic and foreign, must be paid in the currencies in which they were contracted.
- **Article 13**: All pre-existing dollar-denominated obligations may be paid in Bitcoin.
- **Article 14**: The State guarantees automatic and instant conversion of Bitcoin to dollars through a trust (fideicomiso) at BANDESAL.
- **Article 15**: This law has special character and overrides conflicting provisions in other laws.

## Who This Affects

All individuals and private businesses in El Salvador who may choose to accept or use Bitcoin for transactions. Government entities pay their obligations in the currency originally contracted. The 2025 reform clarified that no one is forced to accept Bitcoin.

## Related Laws

- Decreto 137 (2021) — Bitcoin Trust Law (Ley de Creacion del Fideicomiso Bitcoin), creates the trust guaranteeing Bitcoin-to-dollar conversion
- Decreto 201 (2000) — Monetary Integration Law (Ley de Integracion Monetaria), which adopted the US dollar
- D.L. 199 (2025) — Reform that made Bitcoin acceptance voluntary and repealed several articles

## Common Questions

- **Q: Is Bitcoin acceptance mandatory in El Salvador?** — A: No. After the 2025 reform (D.L. 199), acceptance is voluntary for private natural and legal persons only. The original 2021 text required acceptance by all economic agents, but this was changed.
- **Q: Do I have to pay capital gains tax on Bitcoin?** — A: No. Article 5 explicitly exempts Bitcoin exchanges from capital gains taxes.
- **Q: Can I pay my debts in Bitcoin?** — A: Yes. Article 13 allows pre-existing dollar obligations to be paid in Bitcoin, and Article 7 allows Bitcoin for any monetary obligation when the parties agree.
- **Q: Who sets the Bitcoin exchange rate?** — A: The market. Article 2 states the exchange rate is established freely by the market.
