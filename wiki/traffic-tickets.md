# Contesting Traffic Tickets in El Salvador

Traffic infractions in El Salvador are governed by the **Traffic and Road Safety Law (Ley de Transporte Terrestre, Transito y Seguridad Vial, Decreto 420)**. Enforcement falls under the **Vice Ministerio de Transporte (VMT)**, which oversees citations, fines, and the appeals process.

## How Traffic Citations Work

When a traffic officer issues a citation, the driver receives a written notice specifying the infraction, the applicable fine amount, and instructions for payment or appeal. Fines vary by infraction type, ranging from minor violations (such as expired registration stickers) to serious offenses (such as reckless driving or driving under the influence).

## Appealing a Traffic Ticket

Drivers who believe a citation was issued in error can contest it through the following process:

1. **Receive the citation** and review the stated infraction and fine amount.
2. **File a written appeal** with the VMT within the designated business days after receiving the citation. The appeal should include the citation number, a description of the dispute, and any supporting evidence.
3. **Attend the hearing** before the VMT administrative authority, where both the officer and the driver may present their case.

If the VMT ruling is unfavorable, the driver may escalate the matter by filing an appeal with the **administrative courts (tribunales contencioso administrativos)**.

## Consequences of Unpaid Fines

Unpaid traffic fines can prevent **vehicle registration renewal** (refrendo de matricula). Drivers should resolve outstanding fines before attempting to renew registration, as the VMT system flags vehicles with pending violations.
