# Visa and Entry Requirements

El Salvador's immigration framework is governed by the **Ley Especial de Migracion y Extranjeria** (Decreto 286), administered by the **Direccion General de Migracion y Extranjeria**.

## Tourist Entry

Citizens of the United States, European Union member states, Canada, and many other countries **do not need a visa** for tourist stays of up to **90 days**. This is facilitated by the **CA-4 Agreement** (Convenio Centroamericano de Libre Movilidad), which allows free movement between El Salvador, Guatemala, Honduras, and Nicaragua. The 90-day period applies to the entire CA-4 region collectively, not per country.

### Requirements at Entry

- **Valid passport** with at least 6 months of remaining validity
- **Tourist card** (tarjeta de turismo) -- issued at the port of entry for certain nationalities, with a fee of approximately **$12 USD**
- Proof of onward travel or return ticket may be requested

## Overstaying

Remaining in the country beyond the authorized period results in **fines** calculated per day of overstay. Fines must be paid at Migracion before departure. Repeated or prolonged overstays may result in entry bans.

## Work and Residency Visas

Foreign nationals who wish to work or reside in El Salvador must apply for the appropriate visa through the Direccion General de Migracion y Extranjeria. Common categories include:

- **Temporary residency** (residencia temporal) -- typically valid for one year, renewable
- **Permanent residency** (residencia definitiva) -- available after several years of temporary residency
- **Work permits** -- employers often sponsor these; see [[work-permits]]

Applications require authenticated documents, background checks, and proof of financial means. Processing times vary but generally take several weeks.
