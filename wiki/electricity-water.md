# Electricity and Water Services

Utility services in El Salvador are provided by a mix of public and private entities, with government regulatory oversight ensuring service standards and pricing.

## Electricity

Electricity distribution is handled by regional companies, including **CAESS** (San Salvador metropolitan area), **DELSUR** (southern region), **EEO** (western region), and others. All electricity providers are regulated by **SIGET** (Superintendencia General de Electricidad y Telecomunicaciones), which oversees rates, service quality, and dispute resolution.

### How to Connect

To request a new electricity connection, visit the local office of your regional distributor with:

- **Property deed or lease agreement** proving your right to occupy the premises
- **DUI** (identification document)
- **Completed application form** provided by the distributor

Connection fees and timelines vary by provider and location. SIGET sets maximum allowable charges for standard residential connections.

### Paying Your Bill

Electricity bills can be paid at distributor offices, authorized banks, convenience stores (such as Super Selectos), and through online platforms offered by each company.

## Water

Water and sewer service is provided by **ANDA** (Administracion Nacional de Acueductos y Alcantarillados), the national water authority. ANDA operates throughout the country, though some rural areas may rely on community-managed systems.

### How to Connect

To apply for water service, visit your local ANDA office with your property deed or lease and DUI. ANDA charges a connection fee that varies based on the type of service requested.

### Paying Your Bill

ANDA bills can be paid at ANDA offices, partner banks, authorized payment centers, and online through ANDA's website. Failure to pay may result in service disconnection after proper notice.
