# Declaring Cash at the Airport in El Salvador

Travelers entering or leaving El Salvador are required to declare cash and monetary instruments exceeding **$10,000 USD** (or its equivalent in other currencies). This obligation applies to all travelers, including Salvadoran citizens, residents, and foreign visitors. The requirement is enforced under the **Anti-Money Laundering Law (Ley contra el Lavado de Dinero y de Activos, Decreto 498)** and customs regulations.

## What Must Be Declared

The declaration requirement covers:

- **Cash** in any currency (bills and coins).
- **Monetary instruments** such as traveler's checks, money orders, and bearer negotiable instruments.
- The threshold is **$10,000 USD or equivalent** per person. Amounts at or below this threshold do not require a declaration.

## How to Declare

**Declaration forms** are available at airport customs checkpoints at the Aeropuerto Internacional de El Salvador (SAL) in San Luis Talpa. Travelers should complete the form before proceeding through customs. The form asks for personal identification details, the amount and type of currency, the origin of the funds, and the intended purpose.

## Consequences of Failing to Declare

Failure to declare cash above the threshold is a **customs violation** and may trigger:

- **Seizure of undeclared funds** by customs authorities.
- **Investigation** under the Anti-Money Laundering Law, which can lead to criminal charges if the funds are linked to illicit activity.
- **Administrative fines** even if the funds are determined to be legitimate.

Travelers should retain proof of the lawful origin of large cash amounts (bank withdrawal receipts, business documentation) to facilitate the customs process.
