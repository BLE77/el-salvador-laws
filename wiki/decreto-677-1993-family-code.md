# Decreto 677 (1993) -- Family Code

## Summary
The Family Code establishes the legal framework for family relationships in El Salvador, as mandated by Article 32 of the Constitution which recognizes the family as the fundamental basis of society. It regulates marriage, divorce, parent-child relationships, adoption, child support, marital property regimes, guardianship, and the protection of minors. The code was enacted to harmonize family legislation with constitutional principles.

## Key Provisions
- **Article 2**: The family is the fundamental basis of society and shall have the protection of the State.
- **Article 7**: The State shall promote marriage (*matrimonio*). Actions to strengthen marriage stability shall be coordinated through the Attorney General's Office (*Procuraduria General de la Republica*).
- **Article 11**: Marriage is the legal union of a man and a woman, based on equality, with the purpose of living together, helping each other, and raising children.
- **Article 14**: The minimum age for marriage is 18 years.
- **Article 15**: The following persons may not marry each other: blood relatives in any degree of the direct line or siblings; an adopter and the adopted person's descendants; a person convicted of the homicide of the other's spouse.
- **Article 16**: Guardians (*tutores*) may not marry their wards until guardianship accounts have been judicially approved.
- **Article 31**: Marriage may be celebrated through a legal representative with a special power of attorney (*poder especial*).
- **Article 55**: Marital property may be governed by one of three regimes: separation of property (*separacion de bienes*), deferred community (*comunidad diferida*), or participation in profits (*participacion en las ganancias*). If the spouses do not choose, separation of property applies by default.
- **Article 67**: Expenses charged to the deferred community include family expenses, children's education, spousal support obligations to ascendants, and costs of administering common property.
- **Article 104**: A divorce may be requested by mutual consent or by one spouse based on statutory grounds.
- **Article 106**: A couple may divorce by mutual consent if they have been married for at least one year and present an agreement covering children's welfare, property division, and support obligations.
- **Article 109**: The judge must approve the divorce agreement only if it does not violate the rights of children and spouses regarding support, visitation, and similar matters.
- **Article 143**: A father may voluntarily recognize a child (*reconocimiento voluntario*) through the birth certificate, a separate notarized document, a will, or a court proceeding.
- **Article 218**: Both parents, even if not living with the child, must maintain affective relationships and personal contact that favor the child's normal development.
- **Article 247**: Child support (*alimentos*) includes food, clothing, housing, healthcare, education, recreation, and professional training.
- **Article 253**: When multiple persons owe child support for the same child, each pays in proportion to their economic capacity.
- **Article 254**: Child support obligations are enforceable through court orders; failure to pay may result in criminal prosecution.
- **Article 287**: In the absence of testamentary guardianship, legal guardianship of minors falls to: grandparents, siblings, uncles/aunts, and first cousins, in that order.
- **Article 400**: Comprehensive protection of minors by the State is carried out through the National System of Child Protection (*Sistema Nacional de Proteccion al Menor*), coordinated by the Salvadoran Institute for Child Protection.

## Who This Affects
All families in El Salvador -- married and unmarried couples, parents, children, adoptive families, and guardians. The code governs personal and property relationships between family members and establishes the framework for state protection of children.

## Common Questions
- **Q: What is the minimum age to get married?** -- A: 18 years (Art. 14).
- **Q: What are the grounds for divorce?** -- A: Divorce may be obtained by mutual consent (after at least 1 year of marriage) or by one spouse on the grounds established by law, including separation for more than one year (Arts. 104, 106).
- **Q: What does child support cover?** -- A: Food, clothing, housing, healthcare, education, recreation, and professional training (Art. 247).
- **Q: Who gets custody of the children after divorce?** -- A: The divorce agreement (mutual consent) or the judge determines custody, always prioritizing the best interest of the child. Both parents retain the right to maintain contact (Art. 218).
- **Q: What is the default marital property regime?** -- A: Separation of property (*separacion de bienes*), meaning each spouse keeps their own property unless they agree otherwise before or during marriage (Art. 55).
- **Q: How is paternity established?** -- A: Through voluntary recognition (at the civil registry, by notarized document, or in a will) or through a judicial paternity action (Art. 143).
