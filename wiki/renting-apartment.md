# Renting an Apartment

Rental agreements (arrendamiento) in El Salvador are governed by the **Civil Code** (Codigo Civil), which sets out the general framework for lease contracts between landlords (arrendadores) and tenants (arrendatarios).

## Documents Typically Required

Landlords generally ask prospective tenants to provide:

- **DUI or passport** (identification)
- **Proof of income** (constancia de salario, pay stubs, or bank statements)
- **Personal or employer references**
- **Security deposit** -- typically equivalent to 1-2 months' rent, returned at the end of the lease minus any deductions for damages

## The Lease Agreement

While verbal agreements are technically valid, it is strongly recommended that the lease be **in writing**. A written contract should specify the rent amount, payment schedule, duration, deposit terms, and conditions for termination. Both parties should retain a signed copy.

## Landlord Obligations

The landlord is required to deliver the property in **habitable condition** and maintain it throughout the lease. Essential services and structural repairs are generally the landlord's responsibility unless the contract states otherwise.

## Tenant Protections

El Salvador does not have a specific rent control law. However, lease terms that are grossly unfair or unconscionable (leoninos) can be challenged in court under general contract principles of the Civil Code. Tenants also have the right to quiet enjoyment of the property.

## Termination and Notice

Either party may terminate the lease according to the terms agreed upon in the contract. In the absence of a specific clause, the Civil Code requires reasonable notice. Eviction for non-payment requires a judicial process -- landlords cannot forcibly remove tenants without a court order.
