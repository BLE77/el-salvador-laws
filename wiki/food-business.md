# Starting a Food Business in El Salvador

Selling food in El Salvador — whether from a home kitchen, a restaurant, or a street cart — requires permits from both health and municipal authorities. The **Municipal Code (Decreto 274 of 1986)** grants municipalities the authority to regulate local businesses, including food establishments.

## Required Permits and Licenses

### Health Permit (Permiso Sanitario)

The **Ministry of Health (Ministerio de Salud)** issues health permits for food businesses. Requirements include:

- Inspection of the premises to verify sanitary conditions
- Proper food storage, refrigeration, and preparation areas
- Access to potable water and adequate waste disposal
- Compliance with food safety regulations

### Food Handler Certification

All persons handling food must obtain a **food handler certification** (carnet de manipulador de alimentos) from the Ministry of Health. This involves a brief training course on food hygiene and safety.

### Municipal Business License (Licencia de Funcionamiento)

The local **alcaldia** (municipality) issues the business operating license. To obtain it:

- Register your business with the municipal tax office
- Provide proof of the health permit
- Pay the applicable municipal fees
- The premises must comply with zoning regulations

## Home-Based Food Businesses

Operating a food business from home is possible but must meet the same sanitary standards as commercial establishments. The home kitchen must pass health inspection, and the business must hold a municipal license. Some municipalities have specific rules about signage and customer traffic in residential areas.

## Street Food Vendors

Street food vendors (vendedores ambulantes) need **municipal authorization** to operate in public spaces. Municipalities assign designated areas and may require vendors to meet basic health and hygiene standards.
