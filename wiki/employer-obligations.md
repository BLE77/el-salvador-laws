# Employer Obligations: ISSS, AFP, and Benefits

The **Labor Code (Decreto 15 of 1972)** and related social security legislation impose mandatory obligations on all employers in El Salvador. Failure to comply can result in fines, penalties, and legal liability.

## Social Security Registration (ISSS)

Employers must register all employees with the **Instituto Salvadoreno del Seguro Social (ISSS)**, which provides health insurance and medical care. Key details:

- **Employer contribution**: 7.5% of the employee's salary
- **Employee contribution**: 3% of salary (deducted from wages)
- Registration must occur within the first days of employment
- ISSS covers medical consultations, hospitalization, maternity benefits, and temporary disability

## Pension Fund (AFP)

Employers must also enroll workers in an **Administradora de Fondos de Pensiones (AFP)** for retirement savings:

- **Employer contribution**: 7.75% of the employee's salary
- **Employee contribution**: 7.25% of salary (deducted from wages)
- Employees choose between the two authorized AFPs (Crecer or Confiar)

## Mandatory Benefits

Beyond ISSS and AFP, employers must provide:

- **Aguinaldo** (Christmas bonus): Equivalent to 10-21 days of salary depending on years of service, paid between December 12 and 20
- **Paid vacation**: 15 days of paid vacation after each year of continuous work, plus a 30% vacation premium
- **Day of rest**: At least one paid rest day per week (typically Sunday)
- **Safe working conditions**: Compliance with occupational health and safety standards

## Reporting and Compliance

Employers must file monthly contribution reports with ISSS and AFP, and retain payroll records. The **Ministerio de Trabajo** can conduct inspections to verify compliance.
