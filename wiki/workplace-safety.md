# Workplace Safety

Workplace safety in El Salvador is governed by the **Labor Code (Codigo de Trabajo), Decreto 15**, and the **General Law on Prevention of Occupational Risks (Ley General de Prevencion de Riesgos en los Lugares de Trabajo)**. These laws establish employer duties and worker rights regarding safe working conditions.

## Employer Obligations

- Provide a **safe and healthy work environment** that meets legal standards.
- Supply appropriate **personal protective equipment (PPE)** at no cost to workers, including helmets, gloves, goggles, and safety footwear as required by the job.
- Conduct **safety training** for all employees, covering hazard identification, emergency procedures, and proper use of equipment.
- Maintain workplace facilities in good condition, including adequate ventilation, lighting, sanitation, and fire safety systems.
- Establish a **workplace safety committee** (comite de seguridad y salud ocupacional) in workplaces with 15 or more employees.

## Worker Rights

- Workers have the **right to refuse dangerous work** that poses an imminent threat to their life or health, without fear of retaliation.
- Workers must be informed about hazards present in their workplace.
- Injured workers are entitled to medical care through **ISSS** and may receive disability benefits.

## Reporting Violations

- Workers can report unsafe conditions to the **Ministry of Labor (Ministerio de Trabajo)**, which conducts workplace inspections.
- Complaints can be filed anonymously.
- Employers found in violation face **administrative fines** and may be ordered to correct hazards within a specified timeframe.
- Repeated or severe violations can lead to temporary closure of the workplace until conditions are remedied.
