# Decreto 955 (1988) -- Health Code

## Summary
The Health Code develops the constitutional principles related to public health and social assistance in El Salvador. It establishes the organizational structure of the public health system -- including the Superior Council of Public Health (*Consejo Superior de Salud Publica*), the Ministry of Health, and professional oversight boards (*Juntas de Vigilancia*) -- and regulates the practice of health professions, food safety, sanitation, disease control, pharmaceuticals, and environmental health.

## Key Provisions
- **Article 1**: The code develops constitutional principles related to public health and social assistance, and governs the organization and powers of the Superior Council of Public Health, the Ministry of Health, and related state agencies.
- **Article 2**: All state agencies, autonomous entities, public authorities, and private persons are obligated to cooperate with public health authorities and coordinate their activities to achieve public health objectives.
- **Article 7**: The Superior Council of Public Health (*Consejo Superior de Salud Publica*) is a public law corporation with legal capacity, enjoying autonomy in its functions and decisions. It and the oversight boards (*Juntas de Vigilancia*) operate autonomously.
- **Article 9**: Oversight boards (*Juntas de Vigilancia*) exist for the medical, dental, pharmaceutical, veterinary, clinical laboratory, and psychology professions.
- **Article 28-29**: Specialized, technical, and auxiliary activities are defined for each health profession (veterinary medicine, clinical laboratory science, psychology, nursing).
- **Article 40**: The Ministry of Health is responsible for coordinating all public health actions according to national health policy and constitutional principles.
- **Article 56**: The Ministry sets sanitary standards for urban and rural housing, including requirements for construction, lighting, ventilation, and hygiene.
- **Article 59**: When sanitary or hygiene deficiencies are found, the Ministry orders the responsible party to correct them.
- **Article 61**: Cities and urban areas must have potable water service. Where it does not exist, the State will provide it through specialized agencies.
- **Article 69**: All garbage, waste, and refuse in urban and populated areas must be collected, transported, and disposed of under sanitary conditions approved by the Ministry.
- **Article 83**: No food or beverage may be manufactured, stored, distributed, or sold without complying with the sanitary standards established by the Ministry and its regulations.
- **Article 86**: Food inspectors have free access to inspect any establishment involved in the production, storage, or sale of food and beverages, and may take samples for analysis.
- **Article 125-E**: Universities that obtain cadavers of unknown persons may not use them for study for 6 months, to give families time to claim them.
- **Article 163**: For zoonosis surveillance, diseases transmissible from animals to humans include brucellosis, cysticercosis, rabies, and others as determined by the Ministry.
- **Article 200**: The Ministry shall provide dental care to the public, with priority given to children and pregnant women.
- **Article 262**: All laboratory services are subject to fees; free services are prohibited, though preferential rates may be established for charitable institutions.
- **Article 290-307**: Health infractions are classified as less serious, serious, and very serious, each with corresponding fines and sanctions.
- **Article 307**: To obtain authorization to practice a health profession, applicants must submit credentials, photographs, and take an oath to comply with the Health Code and its regulations.
- **Article 327**: Any person over 16 who is harmed by, witnesses, or becomes aware of a health infraction may report it to the competent health authority.

## Who This Affects
Everyone in El Salvador -- as patients, consumers of food and beverages, and residents subject to sanitary regulations. It specifically governs healthcare professionals (doctors, dentists, pharmacists, nurses, psychologists, veterinarians, lab technicians), hospitals and clinics, pharmacies, food producers and sellers, and municipal water and sanitation systems.

## Related Laws
- Decreto 153 (2003) -- Drug Regulation Law: regulates controlled substances, which are also referenced in the Health Code
- Decreto 776 (2005) -- Consumer Protection Law: protects consumers regarding food safety, medications, and health products
- Decreto 1030 (1997) -- Penal Code: crimes against public health (adulterated food, unlicensed medical practice) are defined in Title XII

## Common Questions
- **Q: Who regulates doctors and other health professionals in El Salvador?** -- A: The Superior Council of Public Health (*Consejo Superior de Salud Publica*) and the corresponding professional oversight boards (*Juntas de Vigilancia*) for each profession (Art. 7, 9).
- **Q: Can health inspectors enter any food business?** -- A: Yes. Properly credentialed Ministry inspectors have free access to inspect any food establishment and may take samples (Art. 86).
- **Q: Is potable water required by law?** -- A: Yes. Cities and urban areas must have potable water service. Where it does not exist, the State must provide it (Art. 61).
- **Q: How are health violations punished?** -- A: Violations are classified as less serious, serious, and very serious, with corresponding fines. Repeat offenses or serious violations can lead to closure of establishments (Arts. 290-307).
- **Q: Who can report a health violation?** -- A: Any person over 16 years old, or the legal representatives of minors or incapacitated persons, may file a complaint with health authorities (Art. 327).
