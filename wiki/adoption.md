# Adoption in El Salvador

Adoption in El Salvador is governed by the **Family Code (Codigo de Familia), Decreto 677**, and supervised by **ISNA** (Instituto Salvadoreno para el Desarrollo Integral de la Ninez y la Adolescencia). The process prioritizes the best interests of the child and follows strict legal procedures.

## Types of Adoption

- **Full adoption (adopcion plena):** The adopted child gains all rights as a biological child, including inheritance. The legal bond with the biological family is completely severed. This is the most common form.
- **Simple adoption:** Creates a legal relationship between adopter and adopted but does not fully sever ties with the biological family. This form is rarely used in practice.

## Requirements for Adopters

- **Married couples** may adopt jointly. **Single adults** over **25 years of age** may also adopt.
- The adopter must be at least **15 years older** than the child being adopted.
- Adopters must demonstrate moral fitness, financial stability, and the ability to provide a suitable home environment.
- A social and psychological evaluation is conducted by ISNA.

## Process and Timeline

- Applications are filed through ISNA, which manages the matching process between prospective parents and children.
- The adoption is finalized through a **Family Court (Juzgado de Familia)** ruling.
- The entire process typically takes **1 to 2 years**, depending on case complexity.

## International Adoption

- El Salvador is a signatory to the **Hague Convention on Intercountry Adoption**. All international adoptions must comply with Hague procedures.
- International adopters must work through an accredited adoption agency and ISNA as the central authority.
- Additional documentation, including apostilled documents from the adopter's home country, is required.
