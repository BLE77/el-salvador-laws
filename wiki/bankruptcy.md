# Business Bankruptcy and Insolvency in El Salvador

## Legal Framework

Bankruptcy and insolvency proceedings in El Salvador are governed by **Book IV (Libro Cuarto) of the Commercial Code, Decreto 671** (Codigo de Comercio). The code establishes two main proceedings: *quiebra* (bankruptcy) and *concurso de acreedores* (creditor proceedings). These mechanisms provide a structured legal process for addressing situations where a business can no longer meet its financial obligations.

## Types of Bankruptcy

Decreto 671 distinguishes between two ways bankruptcy proceedings may be initiated:

- **Voluntary bankruptcy**: Filed by the debtor when they recognize their inability to pay debts as they come due. The debtor submits a petition to the competent commercial court along with financial statements, a list of creditors, and an inventory of assets.
- **Involuntary bankruptcy**: Filed by one or more creditors who can demonstrate that the debtor has ceased paying obligations. The court evaluates the evidence before formally declaring bankruptcy.

## The Bankruptcy Process

Once the court issues a **declaration of bankruptcy** (declaratoria de quiebra), the following steps occur:

1. **Appointment of a trustee** (sindico): The court designates a trustee to administer the bankrupt estate. The trustee takes control of the debtor's assets and business operations.
2. **Asset inventory**: The trustee conducts a full accounting of the debtor's assets, liabilities, and ongoing contracts.
3. **Creditor claims**: Creditors are invited to file their claims within a specified period. The trustee verifies and classifies each claim.
4. **Liquidation**: Assets are sold and the proceeds are distributed to creditors according to a legally defined **order of priority**. Secured creditors, employee wages, and tax obligations typically rank highest.

## Creditor Proceedings (Concurso de Acreedores)

As an alternative to full bankruptcy, the *concurso de acreedores* allows a debtor to negotiate repayment terms with creditors under judicial supervision. If an agreement is reached, the business may continue operating under modified conditions.

## Fraudulent Bankruptcy

Fraudulent bankruptcy is a **criminal offense** under **Penal Code Decreto 1030**. A debtor who conceals assets, falsifies records, or artificially creates debts to defraud creditors faces criminal prosecution and imprisonment in addition to civil liability.

## Practical Considerations

Business owners facing insolvency should seek legal counsel early. Voluntary filing before debts spiral further can result in more favorable outcomes and may preserve the possibility of restructuring rather than full liquidation.
