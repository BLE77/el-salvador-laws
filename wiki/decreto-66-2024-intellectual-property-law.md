# Decreto 66 (2024) — Intellectual Property Law (Ley de Propiedad Intelectual)

## Summary

This comprehensive law establishes the legal framework for protecting intellectual property rights in El Salvador, covering copyright (derecho de autor), patents, trademarks, industrial property, and related rights. It creates the Salvadoran Institute of Intellectual Property (Instituto Salvadoreno de la Propiedad Intelectual) as the central administrative authority, and aims to promote innovation, creativity, and technology transfer.

## Key Provisions

- **Article 1**: The law protects and enforces intellectual property rights and other industrial rights and privileges, as well as innovation and creativity. It facilitates the diffusion of information, knowledge, and technology.
- **Article 2 - Purposes**: Key goals include:
  - Establishing a legal framework for IP protection
  - Regulating acquisition, maintenance, and licensing of IP rights
  - Regulating unfair competition related to industrial property
  - Promoting creativity, inventive activity, and technology transfer
  - Balancing rights and obligations for sustainable national development
- **Article 3**: Defines key terms including copyright (derecho de autor), related rights (derechos conexos), moral rights (derechos morales, which are imprescriptible and inalienable), patrimonial rights (derechos patrimoniales), and the distinction between author (persona natural) and titular (natural or legal person).
- **Article 4**: Any natural or legal person, regardless of nationality or domicile, may acquire and enjoy rights under this law. No nationality or residency condition is required.
- **Article 6**: The Salvadoran Institute of Intellectual Property (Instituto Salvadoreno de la Propiedad Intelectual) is a dependency of the National Registry Center (Centro Nacional de Registros). Its Director must be a lawyer and notary with at least 5 years of IP experience.
- **Article 8**: The Institute's functions include processing IP applications, granting titles, registering transfers and licenses, promoting IP awareness, and supervising collective management entities.
- **Article 11**: Moral rights of the author are imprescriptible, inalienable, unseizable, and non-renounceable (imprescriptible, inalienable, inembargable e irrenunciable). They include the right to publish, use a pseudonym, destroy or retain unpublished work, claim authorship, oppose plagiarism, and protect the work's integrity.
- **Article 12**: Patrimonial rights (derechos patrimoniales) give the author exclusive power to use, authorize, prohibit, or transfer exploitation of the work and receive economic benefits.
- **Article 15**: The law protects works from the human intellect (intelecto humano) in any sensible form, regardless of mode, merit, or purpose, by the sole fact of creation.
- **Article 16 - Protected works**: Include literary and artistic works, software (programas de ordenador), video games, musical works, dramatic works, choreography, audiovisual works, drawings, paintings, architecture, sculpture, photographs, applied arts, maps, and architectural plans.

## Who This Affects

All creators, authors, inventors, and IP holders in El Salvador, whether national or foreign. Also affects businesses using trademarks, publishers, software developers, musicians, filmmakers, collective management organizations, and the Salvadoran Institute of Intellectual Property.

## Related Laws

- Ley de Procedimientos Administrativos — Applied supplementarily per Article 5
- Codigo Procesal Civil y Mercantil — Applied supplementarily per Article 5
- International treaties and conventions on IP ratified by El Salvador

## Common Questions

- **Q: Do I need to be Salvadoran to get IP protection?** — A: No. Article 4 states that any person, regardless of nationality or domicile, can acquire and enjoy IP rights.
- **Q: Are moral rights transferable?** — A: No. Article 11 establishes that moral rights are imprescriptible, inalienable, unseizable, and non-renounceable. After the author's death, heirs defend divulgation and integrity rights.
- **Q: Is software protected?** — A: Yes. Article 16 explicitly lists computer programs (programas de ordenador) and video games among protected works.
- **Q: Who administers IP registration in El Salvador?** — A: The Salvadoran Institute of Intellectual Property (Instituto Salvadoreno de la Propiedad Intelectual), a dependency of the National Registry Center (Article 6).
