# Foreigners Working in El Salvador

Employment of foreign nationals in El Salvador is regulated by the **Ley Especial de Migracion y Extranjeria (Decreto 286)** and the **Labor Code (Decreto 15)**. Both the worker and the employer have legal obligations to comply with.

## Work Authorization Requirements

Foreign nationals need one of the following to work legally:

- **Work visa (visa de trabajo)**: Applied for at a Salvadoran consulate before entry, or adjusted in-country through the Direccion General de Migracion y Extranjeria
- **Temporary residency permit with work authorization**: Allows employment for a defined period, typically one to two years, renewable
- **Permanent residency**: Grants unrestricted right to work

Tourist visas do **not** authorize employment. Working on a tourist visa is a migration violation that can result in fines and deportation.

## Employer Obligations

The **employer must request work authorization** from the Ministry of Labor on behalf of the foreign worker. Additionally:

- At least **90% of any employer's workforce must be Salvadoran nationals**, as required by the Labor Code
- Exceptions exist for **specialized technical positions** where qualified Salvadoran workers are unavailable
- The employer must demonstrate that the foreign worker fills a genuine need

## Equal Labor Rights

Once properly authorized, foreign workers enjoy the **same labor rights as Salvadoran nationals**, including minimum wage, aguinaldo, vacation, social security enrollment, and protection against wrongful termination.

## Practical Steps

1. Obtain a job offer from a Salvadoran employer
2. Employer files work authorization request with the Ministerio de Trabajo
3. Apply for the appropriate visa or residency at the Direccion General de Migracion
4. Register with the social security system (ISSS) and pension fund (AFP) upon starting work
