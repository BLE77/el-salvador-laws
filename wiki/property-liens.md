# How to Check Property Liens in El Salvador

Before purchasing or investing in real property in El Salvador, it is essential to verify whether the property has any liens (gravamenes), mortgages (hipotecas), or other encumbrances. The **Centro Nacional de Registros (CNR)** is the government institution responsible for maintaining the national property registry.

## Requesting a Certificacion Registral

The primary method to check property liens is to request a **certificacion registral** from the CNR. This official certificate shows:

- **Current ownership** of the property
- **Mortgages** (hipotecas) registered against the property
- **Liens and encumbrances** (gravamenes) including judicial liens, tax liens, and easements
- **Annotations** (anotaciones preventivas) such as pending legal disputes
- **Property boundaries** and registered measurements

To obtain this certificate, visit the CNR office or use their online portal at [www.cnr.gob.sv](https://www.cnr.gob.sv). You will need the property's registry number (numero de matricula) or the owner's name. The cost is a small administrative fee.

## Municipal Tax Verification

In addition to the CNR check, visit the **municipal tax office** (alcaldia) where the property is located. Unpaid municipal property taxes (impuestos municipales) can become liens on the property. Request a **solvencia municipal** to confirm no outstanding tax debts exist.

## Additional Due Diligence

- Check with **catastro** (cadastral office) for boundary and survey records
- Verify no pending **judicial proceedings** affect the property through court records
- Hire a licensed notary (notario) to conduct a full title search before any purchase

A thorough lien check protects buyers from inheriting debts or legal disputes tied to the property.
