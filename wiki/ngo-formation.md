# Forming an NGO or Non-Profit in El Salvador

Non-governmental organizations (NGOs) and non-profits in El Salvador are regulated by the **Ley de Asociaciones y Fundaciones sin Fines de Lucro** and registered through the **Ministry of Interior (Ministerio de Gobernacion)**.

## Types of Non-Profit Entities

- **Asociacion** (association) — a membership-based organization formed by a group of individuals pursuing a common non-profit purpose
- **Fundacion** (foundation) — an entity established with a dedicated patrimony (endowment) to pursue charitable, educational, or social purposes

## Formation Requirements

To register an NGO:

1. **Founding act** — execute a founding deed (escritura de constitucion) before a Salvadoran **notary**
2. **Bylaws (estatutos)** — draft bylaws specifying the organization's purpose, governance structure, membership rules, and dissolution procedures
3. **Minimum members** — associations require a minimum of **25 founding members**
4. **Board of directors** — elect a governing board at the founding assembly
5. **Submit application** to the Ministry of Interior with all required documentation
6. The Ministry reviews and, if approved, publishes the approval in the **Diario Oficial**

## Ongoing Obligations

- **Annual reporting** — NGOs must submit annual financial reports and activity summaries to the Ministry of Interior
- **Accounting records** — proper bookkeeping must be maintained
- **Tax compliance** — while non-profits can obtain **tax-exempt status** from the Ministry of Finance, they must still file annual tax declarations

## Foreign NGOs

Foreign organizations wishing to operate in El Salvador must **register separately** with the Ministry of Interior. They must appoint a legal representative domiciled in El Salvador and comply with the same reporting requirements as domestic NGOs.

## Practical Notes

The registration process can take several months. Working with a Salvadoran attorney experienced in non-profit law is strongly recommended to navigate the bureaucratic requirements.
