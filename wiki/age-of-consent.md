# Age of Consent in El Salvador

The age of consent in El Salvador is effectively **18 years old**. Sexual acts with persons under 18 can constitute criminal offenses under the **Penal Code (Codigo Penal, Decreto 1030 of 1997)**, depending on the circumstances.

## Key Legal Provisions

### Sexual Acts with Minors (Art. 159 and related)

- **Article 159** (Estupro) criminalizes sexual intercourse with a person aged 15 to 17 when achieved through deceit or by taking advantage of a position of trust or authority. This carries a prison sentence of 4 to 10 years.
- **Articles 158-163** address various sexual offenses against minors, with harsher penalties for younger victims.
- Sexual acts with children **under 15** are treated as aggravated offenses (violacion en menor o incapaz, Art. 159 and related provisions) regardless of apparent consent, carrying sentences of up to 20 years.

### Aggravating Factors

Penalties increase when the offender:
- Is in a position of trust, authority, or guardianship over the minor
- Uses force or intimidation
- The victim is under 12 years of age

## Age of Consent vs. Age of Majority

These are distinct legal concepts:

- **Age of consent** (for sexual activity): Effectively 18, with criminal provisions protecting all minors.
- **Age of majority** (mayoria de edad): **18 years**, as established by the **Family Code (Decreto 677)** and the Constitution. At 18, a person acquires full legal capacity for contracts, voting, and other civil acts.

## Protection of Minors

El Salvador's legal framework prioritizes child protection. The **Law for the Comprehensive Protection of Children and Adolescents (LEPINA, Decreto 839 of 2009)** reinforces protections for persons under 18, including against sexual exploitation. The **Decreto 431 (2022)** further strengthened protections for children and adolescents.

## Practical Considerations

Prosecutors actively pursue cases involving sexual offenses against minors. The lack of a close-in-age exemption ("Romeo and Juliet" law) means that technically, sexual relations between two 17-year-olds could fall within the scope of the law, though prosecution in such cases is uncommon.
