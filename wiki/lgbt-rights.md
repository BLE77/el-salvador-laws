# LGBT Rights in El Salvador

The legal framework in El Salvador provides limited protections for LGBT individuals. The **Family Code (Decreto 677 of 1993)** and the **Constitution** define marriage and family structures in traditional terms, and there is no comprehensive anti-discrimination legislation based on sexual orientation or gender identity.

## Marriage

Same-sex marriage is **not recognized** in El Salvador. The Constitution explicitly defines marriage as a union between a **man and a woman**. This constitutional provision means that legislative change alone would be insufficient — amending the Constitution would be required to permit same-sex marriage. Same-sex marriages performed abroad are not recognized domestically.

## Adoption

Same-sex adoption is **not permitted** under the Family Code (Decreto 677). The adoption framework is structured around married couples or single individuals, and since same-sex couples cannot legally marry, joint adoption by same-sex partners is not available.

## Anti-Discrimination Protections

There is **no general anti-discrimination law** based on sexual orientation or gender identity in El Salvador. However:

- An **executive decree issued in 2010** prohibited discrimination based on sexual orientation and gender identity in **public employment** within the executive branch
- This protection is limited in scope and does not extend to the private sector or other areas of public life
- Some municipal ordinances in San Salvador have included non-discrimination provisions

## Gender Identity

There is no legal framework for gender identity recognition or legal name and gender changes based on gender identity. Identification documents reflect the sex assigned at birth.

## Practical Considerations

LGBT individuals facing discrimination or violence can report incidents to the **Procuraduria para la Defensa de los Derechos Humanos (PDDH)**, the national human rights ombudsman.
