# Decreto 652 (2023) -- Public Procurement Law

## Summary

Decreto 652 of 2023 establishes the comprehensive legal framework governing the entire public procurement cycle in El Salvador, covering the planning, contractor selection, contracting, oversight, and settlement of all government acquisitions of works, goods, and services. The law creates the National Public Procurement System (SINAC) and mandates the use of the electronic procurement platform COMPRASAL for all public institutions. It replaces the prior procurement regime with modernized procedures emphasizing transparency, free competition, digital technology, anti-corruption certification, and sustainable purchasing practices.

## Key Provisions

**Art. 1 -- Purpose and Principles.** The law regulates the full public procurement cycle (planning, selection, contracting, oversight, settlement) for all works, goods, and services acquired by the public administration, governed by principles of transparency, free competition, equality, efficiency, and sustainable procurement.

**Art. 2 -- Covered Entities.** All natural and legal persons (domestic or foreign) that bid on or contract with the state are subject to this law. It applies to central government agencies, autonomous institutions (including CEL and ISSS), entities using public funds, municipalities, FOPROMID, and FOSEP.

**Art. 3 -- Exclusions.** Contracts funded by international treaties with their own procurement rules, inter-institutional agreements, personal service contracts creating employment relationships, banking and financial services (except insurance), energy distribution and water services, and real estate acquisitions are excluded.

**Art. 5 -- Governing Principles.** All public procurements must observe: transparency, technological currency, social and environmental sustainability, equality, free competition, economic equilibrium, rational public spending, efficacy and efficiency, and anti-corruption (probity).

**Art. 6 -- National Public Procurement System (SINAC).** Creates the SINAC, comprising the National Directorate of Public Procurement (DINAC), institutional Procurement Units (UCP), requesting units, financial units, providers, and the COMPRASAL electronic platform. SINAC interfaces with the Integrated Financial Administration System (SAFI).

**Art. 7 -- DINAC as Governing Body.** The National Directorate of Public Procurement (DINAC) serves as the national-level governing body for public procurement, responsible for centralized regulation while each institution exercises decentralized operations.

**Art. 8 -- Institutional Procurement Units (UCP).** Every public institution must establish a UCP responsible for managing procurement processes. The UCP head must be a Salvadoran citizen by birth, hold a university degree with relevant experience, and have verified training in public procurement.

**Art. 10-11 -- COMPRASAL Electronic System.** COMPRASAL is the mandatory electronic procurement platform for all public entities. It automates procurement operations, enables real-time information exchange, and serves as the centralized registry of all state procurements. It includes the National Contracting Registry (RUPES) and the Sanctions Registry.

**Art. 16 -- Anti-Bribery Certification.** Institutions designated by regulation must obtain and maintain Anti-Bribery Certification per international quality management standards. Institutional heads who fail to obtain certification within three years face removal from office.

**Art. 17-18 -- Annual Procurement Plan (PAC).** Every institution must prepare and publish an Annual Procurement Plan on COMPRASAL within 30 calendar days of the fiscal year start. The PAC lists all planned procurements with estimated values and is prepared jointly by the UCP, requesting units, and the institutional financial unit.

**Art. 19 -- Authority to Award.** The highest authority of each institution (Ministers, Board of Directors, Municipal Council) holds authority to award contracts, approve bid documents, declare processes void, and appoint evaluation panels. Delegation is permitted except for direct contracting exceeding 240 monthly commercial-sector minimum wages.

**Art. 25 -- Eligibility to Bid.** Any person with legal capacity may bid unless disqualified by prior criminal conviction for corruption or fiscal offenses, bankruptcy, contract termination for cause in the past five years, tax insolvency, or provision of false information.

**Art. 33 -- MSME Participation.** Public institutions must facilitate access for micro, small, and medium enterprises, and must endeavor to procure at least 25% of their annual goods and services budget from national MSMEs.

**Art. 40 -- Competitive Bidding (Licitacion Competitiva).** The primary procurement method: an open call for bids with published solicitation documents, evaluated against pre-established criteria to select the most advantageous offer.

**Art. 41 -- Price Comparison (Comparacion de Precios).** A streamlined method based on comparing at least three quotations obtained through open invitation, used for ready-to-use or standard-specification products and simple civil works.

**Art. 42-44 -- Direct Contracting.** Permitted only in exceptional circumstances: sole-source suppliers, urgency posing grave risk to the public interest, or declared emergencies (natural disasters, pandemics, national security). The institutional head must personally authorize and justify direct contracting above the threshold.

**Art. 45 -- Low-Value Purchases (Baja Cuantia).** For immediate, unforeseen needs payable from petty cash or revolving funds. Does not require standard bid documents; purchases are made directly and documented with commercial invoices.

**Art. 62-69 -- Consultancy Services.** Establishes separate methods for selecting consultants: quality-and-cost based selection, quality-based selection, least-cost selection, sole-source selection, and individual consultant selection. Consultants must maintain objectivity, avoid conflicts of interest, and cannot supervise their own designs.

**Art. 73 -- Framework Agreements (Convenio Marco).** DINAC conducts centralized selection of pre-qualified providers for inclusion in electronic catalogs, for purchases under 240 commercial-sector minimum monthly wages.

**Art. 74 -- Reverse Electronic Auction.** Institutions may procure goods and non-consultancy services through electronic reverse auctions on COMPRASAL or authorized private exchange platforms.

**Art. 124-129 -- Guarantees.** Requires bid guarantees, advance payment guarantees (100% of advance), performance guarantees (variable percentage of contract value), and construction quality guarantees (10% of final contract amount, minimum one year).

**Art. 159-161 -- Contract Modifications and Extensions.** Contract modifications must be requested by the contract administrator, processed by the UCP, and approved by the awarding authority. Supply contracts may be extended once for a period equal to or less than the original term.

**Art. 176-181 -- Penalties and Fines.** Late performance subjects contractors to daily penalty fines (up to 15% of contract value). Falsifying documents in COMPRASAL incurs fines up to 200 commercial-sector minimum monthly wages. Anticompetitive practices and disclosure of trade secrets carry separate fine regimes.

**Art. 182-184 -- Debarment.** Bidders and contractors face debarment from 1 to 5 years for offenses including unjustified bid withdrawal (1 year), partial non-compliance (2 years), providing false information or bribery (3 years), and total non-compliance or fraud (5 years).

## Who This Affects

- **All government agencies and autonomous institutions**, including CEL, ISSS, and municipalities, which must conduct procurement through COMPRASAL and comply with DINAC regulations.
- **Private companies and individuals** seeking to sell goods, services, or works to the Salvadoran government, who must register in RUPES and meet eligibility requirements.
- **Micro, small, and medium enterprises (MSMEs)** that benefit from the 25% procurement target and facilitated access provisions.
- **Foreign companies and consultants** that may bid on government contracts but must comply with all registration and eligibility rules.
- **Institutional heads and procurement officials**, who face personal liability, removal from office for failing to obtain anti-bribery certification, and sanctions for procurement irregularities.
- **Contract administrators and auditors** responsible for overseeing contract execution and maintaining consolidated procurement files for ten years.

## Related Laws

- **Constitution of El Salvador, Art. 234** -- Constitutional mandate requiring public bidding for state contracts.
- **Ley Organica de Administracion Financiera del Estado (AFI Law)** -- Establishes the SAFI system and Institutional Financial Units (UFI) referenced throughout.
- **Ley de Procedimientos Administrativos** -- Supplementary source of administrative law principles when the procurement law is silent.
- **Ley de Competencia (Competition Law)** -- Defines anticompetitive practices referenced in the sanctions regime (Art. 25, 26).
- **Ley Contra el Lavado de Dinero y de Activos** -- Anti-money laundering law whose offenses constitute grounds for bidder disqualification.
- **Decreto 3 (1983) -- Disposiciones Generales de Presupuestos** -- General budget provisions governing personnel contracting excluded from this procurement law (Art. 3(c)).
- **Ley de Creacion de la DINAC** -- Establishes the National Directorate of Public Procurement as the governing body.

## Common Questions

**1. What procurement methods does this law establish?**
The law provides five main methods: (a) Competitive Bidding for large procurements, (b) Price Comparison for standard goods and simple works, (c) Direct Contracting for sole-source, urgent, or emergency situations, (d) Low-Value Purchases for small immediate needs, and (e) Reverse Electronic Auction. For consultancies, it adds quality-and-cost selection, quality-based selection, least-cost selection, sole-source selection, and individual consultant selection. Framework Agreements through DINAC catalogs are also available.

**2. Is registration in COMPRASAL/RUPES mandatory?**
Yes. All persons wishing to participate in procurement processes must register in the Unified Registry of Procurement Providers (RUPES), with digital authentication. Exceptions exist only for emergency, urgent, or low-value purchases, though providers selected in those cases must register within 30 business days of being notified of the award.

**3. What are the anti-corruption requirements?**
Designated institutions must obtain and maintain international Anti-Bribery Certification within three years of the law's entry into force. Failure to do so is grounds for removal of the institutional head. Non-designated institutions must appoint a Compliance Officer. Providers bidding on external audit contracts must also hold anti-bribery certification.

**4. Can municipalities use this law?**
Yes. Municipal acquisitions and contracting are explicitly covered (Art. 2(d)). Municipalities may also associate to create shared Procurement Units (UCPs), which may include municipal council members and registered community association members.

**5. What happens if a procurement process receives fewer than three bids?**
For Price Comparison, the process may still be valid with fewer than three quotations if the institution determines the received prices are reasonable compared to market conditions, in accordance with the evaluation criteria in the solicitation documents.

**6. What grounds allow direct contracting without competition?**
Direct contracting is permitted for: patents or highly specialized services unavailable domestically, sole-source suppliers, urgent situations posing grave public risk, declared emergencies (natural disasters, pandemics, armed conflict), and when a prior competitively awarded contract was terminated for cause attributable to the contractor.

**7. How are contract disputes resolved?**
The law establishes a tiered process: (a) direct settlement between the parties within 15 business days, (b) a Review Appeal to the contracting institution's head, and (c) an Appeal to the Public Procurement Appeals Tribunal within three business days of the review decision.

**8. What protections exist for whistleblowers?**
Subordinate officials who report procurement infractions or crimes to the Attorney General's Office are guaranteed employment stability. They cannot be dismissed, transferred, or have their position eliminated as a result of their report. The Court of Accounts verifies compliance with this protection.
