# Constitution of El Salvador

The **Constitution of the Republic of El Salvador** was adopted in **1983** and serves as the supreme law of the country. All legislation, government actions, and judicial decisions must conform to its provisions.

## Form of Government

- El Salvador is a **democratic republic** with a presidential system.
- The government is organized around the principle of **separation of powers** into three branches: Executive, Legislative, and Judicial.
- **President:** Serves a single **5-year term** and cannot be immediately re-elected. The president is both head of state and head of government.
- **Asamblea Legislativa (Legislative Assembly):** A unicameral body of **84 deputies** elected for 3-year terms. Responsible for passing laws and approving the national budget.
- **Judiciary:** Headed by the **Corte Suprema de Justicia (Supreme Court)**, which oversees the court system and has the power of constitutional review.

## Fundamental Rights

The Constitution guarantees a broad set of individual rights, including:

- **Right to life** and physical integrity.
- **Liberty and personal security**, including protection against arbitrary detention.
- **Equality before the law** without discrimination.
- **Property rights**, with limits for social function and expropriation with compensation.
- **Freedom of expression** and access to information.
- **Freedom of assembly** and association.
- **Freedom of religion** and worship.
- **Right to education** and health.

## Constitutional Amendments

Amendments require approval by a **two-thirds majority** of the Legislative Assembly in one term, followed by ratification by a **simple majority** in the next legislative term. Certain core provisions, including the form of government and presidential term limits, cannot be amended.
