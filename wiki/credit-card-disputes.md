# Disputing Credit Card Charges in El Salvador

Consumers in El Salvador have the right to dispute unauthorized or incorrect credit card charges under the **Consumer Protection Law (Ley de Proteccion al Consumidor, Decreto 776)**. Financial institutions are further regulated by the **Superintendencia del Sistema Financiero (SSF)**, which oversees banking practices and consumer complaints related to financial services.

## Grounds for a Dispute

Common reasons for disputing a credit card charge include:

- **Unauthorized transactions** (charges made without the cardholder's consent, including fraud).
- **Incorrect amounts** (being charged more than the agreed price).
- **Duplicate charges** for the same transaction.
- **Charges for goods or services not received** or not delivered as described.

## Steps to Dispute a Charge

1. **Contact the issuing bank** as soon as the unauthorized or incorrect charge is identified. Most banks have a dedicated customer service line and a formal written complaint process.
2. **File a written complaint** with the bank, including the transaction date, amount, and a description of the dispute. Keep a copy for your records.
3. **Wait for the bank's investigation**: The bank has **30 days** to investigate and respond to the complaint.
4. **Escalate if unresolved**: If the bank's response is unsatisfactory, file a formal complaint with the **Defensoria del Consumidor (Consumer Protection Agency)**, which can mediate the dispute and impose sanctions on the financial institution if it finds a violation.

## Additional Protections

The SSF requires financial institutions to maintain transparent billing practices and provide cardholders with clear monthly statements. Consumers who suffer financial harm due to bank negligence may also pursue civil claims for damages.
