# Water Rights and Regulation in El Salvador

Water resources in El Salvador are governed by the **General Water Resources Law (Ley General de Recursos Hidricos, Decreto 253 of 2021)**. This law establishes water as a **public good** (bien publico) and creates a framework for its management and protection.

## Water as a Public Good

Under Decreto 253, water in all its forms — surface water, groundwater, and rainwater — is considered national public property. No individual or entity can claim private ownership of water sources. The state holds responsibility for ensuring equitable access and sustainable management.

## ANDA and Water Services

**ANDA (Administracion Nacional de Acueductos y Alcantarillados)** is the state agency responsible for providing potable water and sewage services. ANDA sets water tariffs, maintains infrastructure, and manages service connections. Water service **cannot be disconnected** without following due process, which requires prior notice and an opportunity for the user to address any unpaid balance.

## Permits and Extraction

- **Well drilling** requires a permit from the relevant authority. Unauthorized wells are subject to sanctions.
- **Water extraction** for commercial, agricultural, or industrial use requires a concession or permit specifying the allowed volume.
- Community water systems (juntas de agua) in rural areas must also register and comply with quality standards.

## Water Contamination

Contaminating water sources is a **criminal offense**. Dumping waste, chemicals, or pollutants into rivers, lakes, or aquifers can result in criminal prosecution under both environmental law and the Penal Code. The **Ministry of Environment (MARN)** monitors water quality and investigates contamination reports.

## Reporting Issues

Water service problems or contamination can be reported to ANDA directly or to MARN for environmental violations.
