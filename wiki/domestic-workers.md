# Domestic Worker Rights

Domestic workers (trabajadores domesticos) in El Salvador are protected under **Chapter 4 of the Labor Code (Codigo de Trabajo), Decreto 15**. These provisions cover household employees such as cleaners, cooks, nannies, gardeners, and caretakers working in private homes.

## Key Rights

- **Minimum wage:** Domestic workers are entitled to the national minimum wage established for the domestic service sector.
- **Social security:** Employers must register domestic workers with **ISSS** (Instituto Salvadoreno del Seguro Social) for health coverage and with an **AFP** for pension contributions.
- **Working hours:** The standard workday is **8 hours**, with a maximum of 44 hours per week, consistent with general labor law.
- **Weekly rest:** At least one full day of rest per week, typically Sunday.
- **Vacation:** 15 days of paid vacation per year after one year of continuous service.
- **Aguinaldo:** Annual bonus (aguinaldo) equivalent to 15 days of salary, paid between December 12 and 20.

## Live-In Workers

- Live-in domestic workers (internos) are entitled to **room and board** in addition to their salary. Room and board cannot be deducted from or substituted for wages.
- Live-in workers must still have defined rest periods and cannot be required to work around the clock.

## Employer Obligations

- Employers must provide a **written employment contract** specifying duties, salary, and working conditions.
- Termination without just cause requires payment of **severance (indemnizacion)** as established by the Labor Code.
- Failure to register domestic workers with ISSS exposes the employer to fines and liability for any medical costs.
