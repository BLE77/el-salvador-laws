# Police Search and Seizure in El Salvador

## Constitutional Foundation

The right to privacy of the home is protected under **Article 20 of the Constitution of El Salvador**, which declares the domicile as inviolable. This fundamental guarantee means that no authority may enter or search a private residence without following the procedures established by law. Any evidence obtained through an unlawful search may be deemed inadmissible in court.

## Legal Framework

The rules governing police searches are set out in the **Criminal Procedure Code, Decreto 733** (Codigo Procesal Penal). This code establishes the conditions under which law enforcement may conduct a search of a person, vehicle, or dwelling, and the procedural safeguards that must be observed.

## The Warrant Requirement

As a general rule, police must obtain a **judicial warrant** (orden de allanamiento) from a competent judge before searching a home. To obtain the warrant, prosecutors must present sufficient grounds showing probable cause that evidence of a crime, contraband, or a wanted person will be found at the location. The warrant must specify the address, the scope of the search, and the items or persons sought.

## Exceptions to the Warrant Requirement

Decreto 733 recognizes several circumstances under which police may conduct a search without a prior warrant:

- **Hot pursuit** (flagrancia): Officers may enter a dwelling when actively pursuing a suspect who has just committed a crime.
- **Consent**: A voluntary and informed consent given by the occupant of the premises allows a warrantless search.
- **Emergency**: When there is an imminent threat to life or a risk that evidence will be destroyed, officers may act without waiting for judicial authorization.

## Rights of the Person Being Searched

The person whose home is being searched has the right to be present during the operation, to request identification from the officers, and to receive a written inventory of any items seized. They also have the right to legal counsel and to challenge the legality of the search before a judge.

## Seized Property

All items seized must be documented in an official record (acta) and placed under judicial custody. Unlawfully seized evidence can be challenged through a motion to suppress during criminal proceedings.
