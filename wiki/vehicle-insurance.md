# Vehicle Insurance and Driving Requirements in El Salvador

## Legal Framework

Vehicle insurance and driving obligations are regulated by the **Traffic and Road Safety Law, Decreto 420** (Ley de Transporte Terrestre, Transito y Seguridad Vial). This law establishes the requirements that every vehicle owner and driver must meet to operate legally on Salvadoran roads.

## Mandatory Liability Insurance

All motor vehicles circulating in El Salvador must carry **liability insurance** (seguro de responsabilidad civil). This insurance covers damages caused to third parties, including bodily injury and property damage resulting from traffic accidents. The policy must be issued by an insurance company authorized to operate in the country.

Drivers are required to carry **proof of insurance** in the vehicle at all times. Failure to present proof during a traffic stop or after an accident constitutes an infraction under Decreto 420.

## Penalties for Driving Without Insurance

Operating a vehicle without valid liability insurance is classified as a **traffic infraction** subject to monetary fines. The vehicle may also be impounded until the owner provides proof of valid coverage. In the event of an accident, an uninsured driver bears full personal financial responsibility for all damages and may face additional legal consequences.

## Other Driving Requirements

Beyond insurance, Decreto 420 requires drivers and vehicle owners to comply with the following:

- **Valid driver's license**: All drivers must possess a current license issued by the Vice Ministry of Transportation (VMT), appropriate for the class of vehicle being operated.
- **Vehicle registration**: Every vehicle must be registered with the VMT and carry current registration documents (tarjeta de circulacion).
- **Technical inspection** (revision tecnica): Vehicles must pass a periodic mechanical and safety inspection. The inspection certificate must be current and available for presentation to authorities.
- **Emission standards**: Vehicles must meet established emission requirements as verified during the technical inspection.

## Insurance Coverage Options

While liability insurance is the legal minimum, vehicle owners may also purchase comprehensive coverage (seguro contra todo riesgo) that includes theft, natural disaster damage, and collision coverage for their own vehicle. Comprehensive policies are optional but widely recommended.

## Practical Advice

Keep all vehicle documents, including the insurance policy, registration, and inspection certificate, organized and accessible in the vehicle. Renew your insurance before it expires to avoid any gap in coverage.
