# Copyright and Intellectual Property

Intellectual property in El Salvador is protected under the **Intellectual Property Law (Ley de Propiedad Intelectual), Decreto 66**, and related international treaties. The law covers copyright (derechos de autor), trademarks, patents, and industrial designs.

## Copyright Protection

- Copyright protection arises **automatically upon creation** of the work. No registration is required for the right to exist.
- Protected works include: literary works, music, films, software, photographs, architectural designs, paintings, sculptures, and other artistic or scientific creations.
- The author holds exclusive rights to reproduce, distribute, perform, display, and create derivative works.

## Registration

- While not mandatory, registering works at the **Centro Nacional de Registros (CNR)** provides additional legal protection and serves as evidence of authorship in disputes.
- Registration is recommended for commercially valuable works.

## Duration

- Copyright lasts for the **author's lifetime plus 70 years** after death.
- For works by legal entities or anonymous works, protection lasts 70 years from publication.

## Enforcement and Penalties

- **Piracy** (unauthorized reproduction or distribution) is a criminal offense under the Penal Code.
- Civil remedies include injunctions, damages, and seizure of infringing copies.
- Customs authorities can detain goods suspected of IP infringement at the border.

## International Framework

El Salvador is a member of the **Berne Convention**, the **WIPO Copyright Treaty**, and has IP obligations under **CAFTA-DR** (Central America-Dominican Republic Free Trade Agreement), which strengthened IP enforcement standards.
