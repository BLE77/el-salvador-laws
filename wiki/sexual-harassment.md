# Sexual Harassment Laws

Sexual harassment (acoso sexual) in El Salvador is a criminal offense under the **Penal Code (Codigo Penal), Decreto 1030**. Additional protections exist under the **Special Law for a Life Free of Violence for Women (Ley Especial Integral para una Vida Libre de Violencia para las Mujeres), Decreto 520**.

## Criminal Provisions

Under Article 165 of the Penal Code, sexual harassment is defined as conduct of a sexual nature that is unwanted and creates an intimidating or hostile environment for the victim. This includes:

- Unwanted sexual advances or propositions.
- Physical contact of a sexual nature without consent.
- Threats or coercion tied to sexual demands, particularly in relationships of authority (employer-employee, teacher-student).

Penalties include **prison sentences**, with aggravated penalties when the perpetrator holds a position of authority over the victim.

## Decreto 520 Protections

The Special Law (Decreto 520) specifically addresses violence against women, including sexual harassment in public and private settings. It created **specialized courts** (Juzgados Especializados para una Vida Libre de Violencia) to handle these cases.

## Workplace Harassment

- Victims can report workplace sexual harassment to the **Ministry of Labor (Ministerio de Trabajo)**.
- Employers have a duty to prevent harassment and establish internal complaint mechanisms.
- Retaliation against employees who report harassment is prohibited.

## How to Report

- File a criminal complaint (denuncia) at the **Fiscalia General de la Republica (FGR)** or the nearest police station.
- Seek protective measures through the specialized courts under Decreto 520.
- Contact **Ciudad Mujer** centers for legal assistance and support services.
