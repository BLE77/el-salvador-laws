# Labor Unions

The right to form and join labor unions (sindicatos) is guaranteed by the **Constitution of El Salvador** (Article 47) and regulated by the **Labor Code (Codigo de Trabajo), Decreto 15**.

## Right to Organize

- All private-sector workers have the constitutional right to **freely organize** into unions.
- A minimum of **35 workers** is required to form a union in a given workplace or industry.
- Union formation requires registration with the **Ministry of Labor (Ministerio de Trabajo)**, which must approve the union's statutes.
- Public-sector workers may form associations but face additional restrictions on union activities.

## Collective Bargaining

- Unions have the right to **negotiate collective bargaining agreements (contratos colectivos de trabajo)** with employers.
- These agreements set terms for wages, benefits, working conditions, and dispute resolution procedures.
- Once signed, collective agreements apply to all workers in the covered workplace, not only union members.

## Right to Strike

- Workers have the **right to strike** (derecho a huelga) as a last resort after exhausting conciliation and mediation procedures.
- Strikes must follow legal procedures, including prior notification and a vote by the majority of workers.
- Essential services (hospitals, utilities, public safety) face restrictions on the right to strike.

## Protections Against Discrimination

- **Anti-union discrimination** is illegal. Employers cannot fire, demote, or penalize workers for union membership or activities.
- Union leaders (directivos sindicales) enjoy **special protections** (fuero sindical) and cannot be dismissed without authorization from a labor judge.
- Workers who experience anti-union retaliation can file complaints with the Ministry of Labor or pursue judicial remedies.
