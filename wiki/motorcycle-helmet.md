# Motorcycle Helmet Law in El Salvador

The **Traffic Law (Ley de Transporte, Transito y Seguridad Vial - Decreto 420)** requires all motorcycle riders and passengers in El Salvador to wear a protective helmet. This is a mandatory safety requirement, not a recommendation.

## Who Must Wear a Helmet

The helmet requirement applies to:

- **The driver** of the motorcycle
- **All passengers**, including those riding on the back seat

There are no exemptions based on age, distance traveled, or type of road. Whether riding on a highway or a local street, helmets are required at all times while the motorcycle is in motion.

## Helmet Standards

Helmets must meet recognized **safety standards**. While the law does not specify a particular international certification, helmets should provide adequate head protection. Open-face helmets and novelty helmets that do not provide genuine impact protection may not satisfy the legal requirement.

## Penalties for Non-Compliance

Riding without a helmet constitutes a **traffic infraction** (infraccion de transito) subject to:

- **Monetary fines** imposed by traffic police (agentes de transito)
- Potential **impoundment** of the motorcycle in some circumstances
- Points or notations on the driver's record

Traffic police conduct regular checkpoints and patrols where helmet compliance is verified.

## Additional Motorcycle Regulations

Beyond helmets, motorcycle riders in El Salvador must also:

- Hold a valid **motorcycle driver's license** (licencia de conducir para motocicleta)
- Keep the motorcycle's **registration and insurance** current
- Use **headlights** during both day and night
- Comply with passenger limits specified for the vehicle

## Practical Advice

Invest in a quality helmet that fits properly. Riding without one not only risks a fine but significantly increases the danger of serious head injury in an accident.
