# Child Custody Law in El Salvador

Child custody in El Salvador is governed by the **Family Code (Codigo de Familia, Decreto 677 of 1993)** and the **Children and Adolescent Protection Law (LEPINA, Decreto 431)**. The guiding principle in all custody decisions is the **best interest of the child** (interes superior del menor).

## Types of Custody

- **Sole custody (cuidado personal exclusivo)** — one parent has primary physical custody and daily care responsibilities. The non-custodial parent retains visitation rights.
- **Shared custody (cuidado personal compartido)** — both parents share physical custody and decision-making responsibilities. This arrangement requires a workable co-parenting relationship.

## Factors the Court Considers

Family courts evaluate multiple factors when determining custody:

- The **age** of the child, particularly for very young children
- **Emotional bonds** between the child and each parent
- Each parent's ability to provide a **stable environment**
- The child's established routines, school, and community ties
- History of **domestic violence** or substance abuse by either parent
- Each parent's willingness to facilitate the child's relationship with the other parent

## Child's Preference

Children **aged 12 and older** have the right to express their preference regarding custody arrangements. While the court takes this preference into account, it is not binding — the judge makes the final determination based on the overall best interest analysis.

## Modification of Custody

Custody orders are **not permanent**. Either parent may petition the family court to modify custody if circumstances have significantly changed — such as relocation, changes in a parent's conduct, or the child's evolving needs. The court will again apply the best interest standard.

## Enforcement

Violation of custody orders, including failure to return a child after visitation, can result in legal sanctions. In severe cases, parental kidnapping charges may apply.
