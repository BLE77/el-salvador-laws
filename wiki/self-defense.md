# Self-Defense Laws in El Salvador

Self-defense in El Salvador is governed by the concept of **legitima defensa** under the **Penal Code (Decreto 1030 of 1997), Article 27**. It is recognized as a legal justification (causa de justificacion) that exempts a person from criminal liability when defending themselves, their property, or a third party.

## Requirements for Legitimate Self-Defense

For a self-defense claim to succeed, the following conditions must be met:

- **Unlawful aggression**: There must be an actual or imminent unlawful attack against the person or their rights
- **Proportionality**: The defensive response must be proportional to the threat — excessive force will not be justified
- **Necessity**: The defensive action must be necessary, meaning no reasonable alternative existed
- **No provocation**: The defender must not have provoked the attack

## Pepper Spray and Defensive Sprays

Pepper spray (gas pimienta) is **not specifically regulated** by statute in El Salvador. Carrying defensive sprays is generally permitted for personal protection. There is no law explicitly prohibiting civilians from purchasing or carrying pepper spray, and it is commonly available at security supply stores.

## Firearms and Weapons

Weapons such as **firearms and knives** are strictly regulated under the **Ley de Control y Regulacion de Armas de Fuego (Decreto 655)**. Carrying a firearm requires a license, and using one in self-defense is subject to strict proportionality analysis. Carrying prohibited weapons can result in criminal charges regardless of defensive intent.

## Practical Considerations

If you use force in self-defense, you should report the incident to the **PNC (Policia Nacional Civil)** immediately. The burden of proving self-defense conditions rests with the defendant if criminal charges are filed. Courts will evaluate whether the response was reasonable given the circumstances.
