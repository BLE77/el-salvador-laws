# Work Permits and Remote Work in El Salvador

Foreign nationals who wish to work in El Salvador must obtain proper authorization under the **Migration and Aliens Law (Ley Especial de Migracion y Extranjeria, Decreto 286 of 2019)** and related labor regulations.

## Tourist Visa Limitations

- Tourists entering El Salvador (typically granted a **90-day stay** under the CA-4 agreement for many nationalities) are **not authorized to work**.
- Engaging in employment on a tourist visa is a violation of migration law and can result in fines, deportation, or bars on future entry.
- "Work" includes both employment by a Salvadoran company and self-employed economic activity within the country.

## Work Visa and Residency

To work legally, a foreigner generally needs one of the following:

- **Temporary residency with work authorization** (residencia temporal): Typically valid for 1-2 years, renewable. The employer usually sponsors this application.
- **Permanent residency** (residencia permanente): Available after several years of temporary residency or through marriage to a Salvadoran citizen, among other qualifying circumstances.
- The **Labor Code (Decreto 15)** also requires that employers hiring foreigners comply with quotas limiting foreign workers to a percentage of total staff (generally no more than 10%).

## Application Process

1. Secure a job offer or business purpose in El Salvador
2. Apply through the **Direccion General de Migracion y Extranjeria**
3. Submit: passport, criminal background check (apostilled), medical certificate, employment contract or business documentation, proof of financial solvency
4. Processing times vary from weeks to several months

## Remote Work and Digital Nomads

Remote work from El Salvador (working for a foreign employer or clients while physically present) occupies a **legal grey area**:

- The migration law does not explicitly address remote or digital nomad work.
- Technically, performing work of any kind while on a tourist visa requires authorization.
- In practice, enforcement against remote workers is minimal, and many digital nomads operate without formal work permits.
- El Salvador has not yet enacted a dedicated **digital nomad visa** program as of the most recent legislative updates, though there have been discussions about creating one.

## Bitcoin and Economic Incentives

El Salvador's adoption of Bitcoin as legal tender (**Decreto 57, Bitcoin Law**) has attracted significant interest from foreign tech workers and entrepreneurs. However, the Bitcoin Law does not itself create a special immigration status for remote workers.

## Practical Notes

Foreigners intending to work for Salvadoran companies should obtain proper work authorization before beginning employment. For remote workers, the risk of enforcement is low but the legal exposure exists. Consulting a Salvadoran immigration attorney is advisable for anyone planning an extended stay with work activity.
