# Noise Regulations in El Salvador

Noise regulation in El Salvador operates at both the **municipal** and **national** level. The **Municipal Code (Decreto 274 of 1986)** grants local governments broad authority over public order, while the **Penal Code (Decreto 1030 of 1997)** addresses noise as a criminal disturbance.

## Municipal Authority (Decreto 274)

Under the Municipal Code, municipalities (alcaldias) have the power to:

- Enact **local ordinances** (ordenanzas municipales) regulating noise levels
- Set **quiet hours** (horas de silencio), which typically run from **10:00 PM to 6:00 AM**, though specific hours vary by municipality
- Regulate commercial establishments including bars, nightclubs, and restaurants regarding music and noise output
- Issue permits for events that may generate elevated noise (fiestas, concerts, construction)
- Impose **fines** (multas) for violations of local noise ordinances

Each municipality sets its own specific regulations, so rules in San Salvador may differ from those in Santa Ana or San Miguel.

## Penal Code Provisions (Decreto 1030)

The Penal Code addresses noise through:

- **Perturbacion de la tranquilidad** (disturbance of the peace): Persistent or egregious noise that disturbs the tranquility of others can be prosecuted as a misdemeanor (falta). Penalties may include fines or short-term detention.
- More serious disturbances, particularly those involving repeated offenses or threatening behavior, can escalate to formal criminal charges.

## Environmental Regulations

The **Environmental Law (Ley del Medio Ambiente)** also touches on noise as a form of pollution. Industrial and commercial operations may be required to conduct environmental impact assessments that include noise levels, particularly in residential areas.

## Enforcement and Complaints

- Noise complaints are typically filed with the **local municipal police** (CAM - Cuerpo de Agentes Metropolitanos) or the **PNC (Policia Nacional Civil)**.
- For commercial establishments, complaints can be directed to the municipal government, which has authority to revoke operating permits for repeated violations.
- Mediation between neighbors is encouraged before formal complaints.

## Practical Notes

Enforcement varies significantly across municipalities. In urban areas like San Salvador, noise ordinances are more formalized. In rural areas, enforcement tends to be informal. Cultural events and religious celebrations often receive informal tolerance even when technically exceeding noise limits.
