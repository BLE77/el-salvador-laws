# Prenuptial Agreements in El Salvador

Prenuptial agreements, known as **capitulaciones matrimoniales**, are governed by the **Family Code (Codigo de Familia, Decreto 677 of 1993)**. They allow couples to define how their property will be managed during and after marriage.

## When and How to Execute

- Capitulaciones must be executed **before the marriage** takes place
- They must be formalized before a **notary** (notario) in a public deed
- Both parties must consent freely and without coercion
- The agreement is registered and becomes part of the marriage record

Prenuptial agreements **cannot be modified** after the marriage unless both spouses agree and follow the legal process for modification before a family court.

## Property Regimes

The Family Code establishes two main property regimes that couples may choose through their capitulaciones:

- **Community property (sociedad conyugal)** — all assets acquired during the marriage are jointly owned by both spouses. Upon divorce or death, community property is divided equally. Each spouse retains ownership of assets acquired before the marriage.
- **Separate property (separacion de bienes)** — each spouse retains full ownership and control of their own assets, both those acquired before and during the marriage.

## Default Regime

If a couple **does not execute a prenuptial agreement**, the default regime is **community property (sociedad conyugal)**. This means all income, purchases, and assets acquired during the marriage are presumed to be jointly owned.

## Practical Considerations

Prenuptial agreements are particularly advisable when one or both spouses own businesses, significant assets, or expect to receive inheritances. A qualified **family law attorney** should draft the capitulaciones to ensure they comply with Decreto 677 and adequately protect both parties' interests. Courts may review prenuptial terms during divorce proceedings to ensure fairness.
