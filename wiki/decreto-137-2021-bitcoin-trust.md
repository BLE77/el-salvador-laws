# Decreto 137 (2021) — Bitcoin Trust Law (Ley de Creacion del Fideicomiso Bitcoin)

## Summary

This law creates and regulates the Bitcoin Trust (Fideicomiso Bitcoin or FIDEBITCOIN), a financial vehicle managed by BANDESAL (Banco de Desarrollo de El Salvador) to guarantee the automatic and instant convertibility between Bitcoin and US dollars. The trust was initially funded with up to $150 million from the Ministry of Finance.

## Key Provisions

- **Article 1**: Creates the Bitcoin Trust (FIDEBITCOIN) to guarantee automatic and instant convertibility between Bitcoin and US dollars. The trust is constituted by law and does not expire.
- **Article 2**: Defines key terms including:
  - **State Digital Wallet (Billetera Digital Estatal)**: An application to convert, store, send, and receive bitcoins and dollars.
  - **Convertibility (Convertibilidad)**: Instant and automatic conversion of Bitcoin to dollars and vice versa at market value.
  - **Digital Exchange House (Casa de Intercambio Digital)**: A corporation authorized by the Superintendency for buying and selling Bitcoin through electronic platforms.
- **Article 3**: The trust must be formalized by public deed (escritura publica) registered at the Commercial Registry (Registro de Comercio). Registration fees are exempt.
- **Article 4**: Initial funding of up to **$150 million USD** from the Ministry of Finance (Ministerio de Hacienda). The trust patrimony can grow through operations, securities emissions, investment returns, and any other assets.
- **Article 5**: The trust is authorized to issue and place securities (titulos valores) in national and international markets.
- **Article 6**: A Board of Administration (Consejo de Administracion) governs the trust, presided by the Minister of Economy or a delegate.
- **Article 8**: The Board's functions include approving policies for financial sustainability, authorizing payments, approving conversion cost mechanisms, and approving internal regulations.
- **Article 10**: BANDESAL acts as trustee (fiduciario), keeping separate accounting records for the trust.
- **Article 13**: The trust is exempt from income tax (impuesto sobre la renta) and value-added tax (IVA), though it must comply with formal obligations.
- **Article 14**: The trust must have an external audit firm registered with the Superintendency and the Court of Accounts (Corte de Cuentas).
- **Article 15**: The Court of Accounts (Corte de Cuentas de la Republica) must audit the trust since it involves public funds.
- **Article 16**: The Board must publish approved regulations on BANDESAL's website for transparency.
- **Article 18**: This law prevails over any conflicting legislation due to its special character (caracter especial).
- **Article 19**: The decree entered into force on the day of its publication in the Official Gazette.

## Who This Affects

All users of the state digital wallet (originally branded as "Chivo Wallet"), BANDESAL as trustee, the Ministry of Finance as initial funder, and any entity participating in Bitcoin-to-dollar conversion services in El Salvador.

## Related Laws

- Decreto 57 (2021) — Bitcoin Law, the parent legislation that mandated creation of this trust (Article 14)
- Ley del Banco de Desarrollo (BANDESAL) — Authorizes BANDESAL to participate in trusts under Article 4(p)

## Common Questions

- **Q: How much money funds the Bitcoin Trust?** — A: The initial allocation was up to $150 million USD from the Ministry of Finance, with the ability to grow through operations and securities issuance.
- **Q: Who oversees the trust?** — A: A Board of Administration presided by the Minister of Economy, with external auditors and oversight by the Court of Accounts (Corte de Cuentas).
- **Q: Does the trust pay taxes?** — A: No. Article 13 exempts it from income tax and IVA, though it must file formal declarations.
- **Q: Can the trust raise funds internationally?** — A: Yes. Article 5 authorizes it to issue and place securities in both national and international markets.
