# Decreto 15 (1972) -- Labor Code

## Summary
The Labor Code of El Salvador governs employment relationships between employers (*patronos*) and workers (*trabajadores*). It establishes minimum rights for workers including maximum working hours, minimum wages, vacation, severance pay, and the right to unionize. The code is founded on principles aimed at improving the living conditions of workers and harmonizing employer-employee relations.

## Key Provisions
- **Article 1**: The code's main purpose is to harmonize relations between employers and workers, establishing their rights and obligations based on principles that improve working conditions.
- **Article 12**: The State will ensure respect for the principles of equal opportunity and treatment in employment and occupation, including access to professional training.
- **Article 17**: The employment contract (*contrato individual de trabajo*) is an agreement where one or more persons commit to performing work or providing services to an employer, under the employer's direction, in exchange for a salary.
- **Article 29**: Employer obligations include paying wages on time, providing safe working conditions, and giving workers time off for voting and medical appointments.
- **Article 31**: Worker obligations include performing agreed work diligently, obeying lawful instructions, maintaining trade secrets, and observing good conduct at work.
- **Article 50**: Termination without liability for either party may occur upon court authorization when the business closes definitively due to unprofitability lasting at least three months.
- **Article 54**: A worker may terminate the contract with employer liability for reasons including unjustified salary reduction, transfer to a lower position, employer fraud, or unsafe working conditions.
- **Article 58**: Severance pay (*indemnizacion*) is owed when the employer terminates the contract without just cause: the equivalent of 30 days' base salary for each year of service.
- **Article 70**: Apprentices are entitled to a minimum wage: no less than 50% of the minimum wage during the first year, 75% during the second year, and the full minimum wage from the third year onward.
- **Article 126**: Wages must be paid in legal currency. Payment in coupons, vouchers, tokens, or merchandise is prohibited.
- **Article 133**: The minimum wage is not subject to garnishment (*inembargable*), except for child support obligations.
- **Article 161**: Working hours are classified as daytime (6:00-19:00) and nighttime (19:00-6:00). The regular daytime shift may not exceed 8 hours per day or 44 hours per week. Night shifts may not exceed 7 hours per day or 39 hours per week.
- **Article 169**: Overtime (*horas extraordinarias*) must be paid at double the regular hourly rate.
- **Article 177**: Every worker is entitled to a weekly rest day, normally Sunday, with pay.
- **Article 183**: After one year of continuous service, workers are entitled to 15 days of paid annual vacation (*vacaciones*), plus a vacation bonus of 30% of the 15-day salary.
- **Article 190**: National holidays (*dias de asueto*) include January 1, Holy Thursday through Saturday, May 1, June 17, August 3-6 (for San Salvador), September 15, November 2, and December 25.
- **Article 196**: The Christmas bonus (*aguinaldo*) must be paid between October 20 and December 20 each year: 15 days' salary for 1-3 years of service, 19 days for 3-10 years, and 21 days for 10+ years.
- **Article 204**: Unions (*sindicatos*) may be formed by workers or employers; at least 35 workers are required to form a workers' union.
- **Article 308**: During illness, the employer must pay 75% of the worker's base salary for up to 60 days (for workers with 1+ years of service).

## Who This Affects
All employers and workers in El Salvador, both in the private and public sectors (with some exceptions for certain government employees governed by special statutes). It applies to all employment relationships regardless of what the parties call the contract.

## Common Questions
- **Q: How much severance pay is owed for unjust dismissal?** -- A: 30 days' base salary for each year of service (Art. 58).
- **Q: What are the maximum working hours?** -- A: 8 hours per day / 44 hours per week for daytime work; 7 hours per day / 39 hours per week for nighttime work (Art. 161).
- **Q: How much vacation do workers get?** -- A: 15 paid days per year after completing one year of service, plus a 30% vacation bonus (Art. 183).
- **Q: What is the Christmas bonus (aguinaldo)?** -- A: 15 days' salary for 1-3 years of service, 19 days for 3-10 years, 21 days for 10+ years, paid between October 20 and December 20 (Art. 196).
- **Q: Can an employer fire a worker for union membership?** -- A: No. The code protects freedom of association and prohibits retaliation for union activities.
- **Q: Is the minimum wage protected from garnishment?** -- A: Yes, the minimum wage cannot be garnished except for child support (Art. 133).
