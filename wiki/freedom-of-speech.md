# Freedom of Speech and Press in El Salvador

Freedom of expression is a constitutionally protected right in El Salvador, established in **Article 6 of the Constitution**. This right encompasses speech, writing, and press freedoms, subject to limited restrictions.

## Constitutional Protections

Article 6 guarantees:

- **Freedom of expression** — every person may freely express and disseminate their thoughts, provided they do not disturb public order or harm the rights of others
- **Freedom of the press** — no prior censorship (censura previa) is permitted
- **Right to information** — citizens have the right to access public information
- **Source protection** — journalists have the right to protect the confidentiality of their sources

The Constitution prohibits the government from seizing printing presses or other means of communication as a tool to suppress speech.

## Defamation Under the Penal Code

While speech is broadly protected, the **Penal Code (Decreto 1030 of 1997)** criminalizes certain speech acts:

- **Calumnia** (slander/defamation) — falsely accusing someone of a crime they did not commit. This carries potential prison time.
- **Injuria** (insult/injury to honor) — publicly making statements that harm someone's dignity or reputation.
- **Difamacion** (defamation) — spreading information known to be false that damages another person's reputation.

These offenses are prosecuted as criminal matters, not merely civil claims. Public officials may invoke these provisions, which has raised concerns among press freedom organizations about their potential chilling effect on journalism.

## Practical Considerations

Despite constitutional guarantees, press freedom organizations have noted challenges in El Salvador including government pressure on media outlets and restrictions on access to public information. The Inter-American Court of Human Rights standards on free expression apply as El Salvador is a signatory to the American Convention on Human Rights.
