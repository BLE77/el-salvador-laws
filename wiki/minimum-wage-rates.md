# Minimum Wage Rates in El Salvador

Minimum wage rates in El Salvador are established under the **Labor Code (Decreto 15)** and updated periodically by executive decree through the Consejo Nacional del Salario Minimo (National Minimum Wage Council). Rates vary by economic sector.

## Current Sector Rates

Minimum wages are set for the following sectors:

- **Commerce and services (comercio y servicios)**: The highest minimum wage tier, applicable to retail, hospitality, banking, and service industries
- **Industry (industria)**: Applies to manufacturing and industrial production workers
- **Maquila (textile and apparel assembly)**: A specific rate for the export-oriented garment and assembly sector
- **Agriculture (agropecuario)**: Applies to permanent agricultural workers
- **Seasonal agriculture (recoleccion de cosechas)**: A separate, typically lower rate for seasonal harvest workers such as coffee and sugarcane pickers

Exact dollar amounts are updated by decree every few years. Employers should consult the most recent Decreto de Salario Minimo published in the Diario Oficial for current figures.

## Employer Obligations

- Employers **cannot pay below the applicable minimum wage** for the sector, even by mutual agreement with the worker
- Minimum wage applies regardless of whether pay is calculated hourly, daily, weekly, or monthly
- The minimum wage includes only base salary; overtime, bonuses, and benefits are calculated separately on top of it

## Penalties for Violations

Employers who pay below minimum wage face **administrative fines** imposed by the Ministry of Labor (Ministerio de Trabajo). Workers may also file complaints directly with the labor inspectorate. Persistent violations may result in increased sanctions and mandatory back-pay orders covering the difference owed.
