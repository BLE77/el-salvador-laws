# Importing Pets to El Salvador

Bringing a pet (dog, cat, or other animal) into El Salvador requires compliance with both customs law and regulations from the **Ministry of Agriculture and Livestock (MAG)**.

## Requirements for Dogs and Cats

To import a dog or cat into El Salvador, you typically need:

1. **Health Certificate** — Issued by a licensed veterinarian in your country of origin within 10 days of travel. Must certify the animal is healthy and free of infectious diseases.

2. **Rabies Vaccination** — Dogs and cats must have a current rabies vaccination administered at least 30 days but not more than 12 months before arrival.

3. **Import Permit from MAG** — Contact the Dirección General de Sanidad Vegetal y Animal (DGSVA) to obtain an import permit. This may require advance application.

4. **Customs Declaration** — Pets must be declared at customs upon arrival. There is generally no import duty on personal pets, but commercial animal imports are subject to tariffs.

## Inspection at Entry

Animals are subject to inspection by MAG veterinary officials at the port of entry (usually Aeropuerto Internacional de El Salvador). If the animal does not meet health requirements, it may be quarantined or denied entry.

## Banned/Restricted Animals

Some exotic species and animals classified as endangered under CITES agreements cannot be imported. El Salvador enforces international wildlife trade regulations.

## Practical Tips

- Airlines have their own pet transport requirements — check with your carrier
- Microchipping is recommended but not always legally required
- Keep all veterinary documents in both English and Spanish
- Arrive during business hours so MAG officials are available for inspection

For the most current requirements, contact the **Ministerio de Agricultura y Ganadería (MAG)** directly or check with the Salvadoran consulate in your country.
