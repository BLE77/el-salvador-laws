# Tobacco and Smoking Regulations in El Salvador

El Salvador regulates tobacco use primarily through the **Health Code (Codigo de Salud, Decreto 955 of 1988)** and subsequent executive regulations. The country has progressively tightened smoking restrictions in line with international public health standards.

## Smoking Prohibitions

Smoking is **prohibited** in the following locations:

- **Enclosed public spaces** including government buildings, hospitals, schools, and shopping centers
- **Workplaces** — employers must ensure smoke-free environments for employees
- **Restaurants and bars** — indoor smoking is banned in food and beverage establishments
- **Public transport** — buses, microbuses, and terminals are smoke-free zones
- **Educational institutions** — all schools and universities, including outdoor common areas

Establishments must display visible no-smoking signage. Owners or managers who permit smoking in prohibited areas can face fines.

## Advertising and Packaging

Tobacco advertising is **restricted** across most media. Health warnings are required on cigarette packaging, including graphic images depicting the consequences of tobacco use. Promotional giveaways and sponsorship of events by tobacco companies face limitations.

## Sale to Minors

The sale of tobacco products to persons under **18 years of age** is prohibited. Vendors must verify age before selling. Vending machines selling tobacco products are restricted in areas accessible to minors.

## Enforcement

The **Ministry of Health (MINSAL)** oversees enforcement of tobacco regulations. Violations can result in administrative fines. Repeat offenders may face closure of their establishment. In practice, enforcement is stronger in urban areas and major commercial zones, while compliance in informal markets and rural areas remains inconsistent.
